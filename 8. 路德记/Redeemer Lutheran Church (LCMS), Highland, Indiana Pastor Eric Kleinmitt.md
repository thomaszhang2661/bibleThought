多马评论：

两种解经方式的根本差异

Pastor Eric（LCMS讲道）的路径

他的解经逻辑基本上是：

**波阿斯的每一个细节 → 对应耶稣的某个属性**

- 波阿斯从伯利恒来 → 耶稣生在伯利恒
- 波阿斯给路得水喝 → 耶稣给撒玛利亚妇人活水
- 波阿斯请路得同桌吃饭 → 耶稣与罪人同席
- 波阿斯喂饱了路得还有剩余 → 耶稣喂饱五千人有余
- 波阿斯说"不要惧怕" → 天使报好消息说"不要惧怕"
- 波阿斯救赎路得 → 耶稣救赎我们

这种方法叫做**类型论**（typology）——把旧约人物或事件解读为新约真实的"影子"或"预表"。

### _Word Becoming Flesh_ 的路径

学术著作承认类型论有一定正当性，但同时给出了明确的警告：

> "路得记常常成为这一方法被过度使用的例证。由此并不能得出路得象征'基督的新妇'的结论，波阿斯与基督中保工作之间的其他精细类比很快就会越界。"

它的核心立场是：**在旧约中，真正的** _Go'el_ **是耶和华本人，波阿斯只是祂的代表。**


为什么过度强调预表是个问题

问题一：文本本身的意图被架空

当你把波阿斯的每一个动作都变成耶稣的象征，路得记本身的叙事意义反而消失了。

路得记想说的首先是：**在士师时代普遍的道德败坏中，这里有几个真实的人，活出了** _chesedh_。这本身就是神学——不需要把它变成耶稣的影子才有价值。

拿俄米的苦涩、路得的忠信、波阿斯的正直——这些是活生生的人在真实处境中做出的真实选择，它们的神学意义首先在于它们本身，而不在于它们"预表"了什么。

问题二：预表论会制造出圣经没有的意思

Pastor Eric说路得的最后一句话"指向救赎者的慷慨"，暗示这是路得作为"教会"的最终姿态。但经文本身没有这个意思——路得只是在告诉婆婆今天发生了什么。

当解经者开始说"这个细节象征那个神学概念"，读者就无法验证这个说法是否成立，因为标准变得模糊了。

问题三：弱化了旧约的独立神学价值

过度类型论有一个隐含的假设：旧约的价值在于它"预告"了新约。但这低估了旧约本身的神学深度。

路得记的_chesedh_神学、护理神学、救赎制度，这些在旧约语境中就已经是完整的神学表达，不需要"升级"为基督论才算有意义。

 但类型论本身不是错的

需要说清楚的是：_Word Becoming Flesh_ 并没有否定类型论，它说的是"有其正当性"，但"很快就会越界"。

**合理的类型论**是有圣经根据的：

马太福音1章的族谱把波阿斯纳入耶稣的家谱，这本身就是圣经对路得记的新约诠释。_go'el_（救赎者）的概念在以赛亚书中扩展为神对以色列的救赎，在新约中进一步成为基督救赎的神学基础——这条线索是圣经自己画的。

问题不在于画这条线，而在于**画得有多细**。

---

## 一个判断标准

可以用这个问题来检验一个类型论解读是否合理：

**这个联系是圣经自己建立的，还是解经者加上去的？**

|联系|圣经根据|判断|
|---|---|---|
|波阿斯是救赎者，预表基督的救赎工作|以赛亚书、新约对_go'el_的神学发展|合理|
|路得是"基督的新妇"象征教会|无直接圣经根据|越界|
|波阿斯从伯利恒来预表耶稣生在伯利恒|地理巧合，无神学发展|牵强|
|波阿斯喂饱路得预表耶稣喂饱五千人|两个独立事件，无圣经联系|越界|
|路得记族谱汇入耶稣族谱|马太福音1章明确记载|合理|

---

## 为什么LCMS牧师倾向于这种方式

这不是Pastor Eric个人的问题，这是路德宗讲道传统的一个特点——路德宗神学高度以基督为中心（Christocentric），有一个著名的原则：

**"凡圣经都当传讲基督"**（_Was Christum treibet_）

路德自己说过：圣经的每一处都应当被用来指向基督。这个原则本身是对的，但在实践中容易导致在旧约经文中到处寻找基督的影子，甚至在圣经没有明确建立这种联系的地方也去建立。

_Word Becoming Flesh_ 代表的是更谨慎的历史文法解经（grammatical-historical exegesis）传统——它也是保守福音派、也是路德宗神学家写的，但它更注重先理解文本在原始历史语境中的意义，再谈它在救恩历史中的位置。

---

## 总结

你的感觉准确。两种方法的差异可以这样概括：

**Pastor Eric**：路得记是关于耶稣的故事，用波阿斯和路得来讲。

_**Word Becoming Flesh**_：路得记是关于_chesedh_和护理神学的故事，它真实地发生在历史中，并在救恩历史的脉络中最终汇入基督——但这个汇入是叙事的终点，不是每个细节的解码钥匙。

后者把路得记读得更厚，因为它让路得记先作为它自己，再作为更大故事的一部分。

### Who Wrote Ruth? Is It Fiction?

### 谁写了路得记？它是虚构的吗？

We don't know who wrote Ruth. Some Jewish rabbis assumed it was Samuel, but that doesn't quite add up chronologically. Whoever it was, they were extraordinarily skilled at writing historical narrative — weaving real events and real lives together in a way that reads almost like a novel.

我们不知道是谁写了路得记。一些犹太拉比认为是撒母耳，但时间线上对不上。无论作者是谁，他在写历史叙事方面极为出色——将真实的事件和真实的人物编织在一起，读起来几乎像一部小说。

Some people use that very quality to argue that Ruth is fiction. Pastor Eric rejects that completely — and so did the Jewish people for thousands of years. Here's why it matters: Ruth feeds directly into the genealogy of King David and ultimately of Jesus. The people alive at the time of its writing could have said, _"You're talking about my great-grandmother. She was a real person. This really happened."_

有些人正是因为这一点而认为路得记是虚构的。Pastor Eric完全拒绝这种观点——数千年来犹太人也是如此。这很重要，因为路得记直接关联到大卫王乃至耶稣的族谱。在这书写成的年代，活着的人可以说："你在讲我曾祖母的故事。她是真实存在的人。这件事真的发生过。"

He illustrates the point with a personal story: as a teenager, he and his friends decided to settle who could run a mile faster by hopping the fence of their high school stadium — at 11:30 at night, in summer. Someone called the police, reported a burglary, and Pastor Eric ended up in the back of a police car. He can tell you that story without quoting word-for-word what everyone said. That doesn't make it fiction. That's just how narratives work. Ruth is that kind of narrative — historical, true, and told with artistry.

他用一个亲身经历来说明这一点：他年少时和朋友们为了一决谁跑一英里更快，在夏天深夜11点半翻越高中体育场的围栏。有人报警说学校被盗，结果Pastor Eric坐上了警车。他可以向你讲述这件事，却不必逐字引用当时每个人说的话。这不代表故事是假的，这只是叙事的方式。路得记就是这样的叙事——真实的历史，用艺术的方式呈现。

### The Background You Need: Land, Law, and Redemption

### 必要的背景知识：土地、律法与救赎

To understand Ruth, you must first understand what land meant to Israel — because the entire plot of Ruth rotates around property law.

要读懂路得记，你必须先理解土地对以色列人意味着什么——因为路得记的整个情节都围绕着财产律法展开。

**Genesis 12: The Promise** **创世记12章：应许**

God called Abram and made him a sweeping promise: _"I will make of you a great nation… and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed."_ He also promised Abram's descendants a specific piece of land — the land of Canaan.

神呼召亚伯兰并赐下宽广的应许："我必叫你成为大国……地上的万族都要因你得福。"祂也向亚伯兰的后裔应许了一块具体的土地——迦南地。

**Numbers 26: The Division** **民数记26章：分配**

When Israel entered the promised land, God instructed Moses to divide it by tribe, proportional to each tribe's size. The position of each territory was determined by lot — by God's providence. No favoritism. And each family within each tribe received a portion as their inheritance, meant to be passed down forever. That word _forever_ is the key to everything in Ruth.

以色列人进入应许之地时，神吩咐摩西按支派分地，各支派所得的大小与人口成比例。每块地的位置由抽签——神的护理——来决定。没有偏袒。各支派中的每个家族都领受了一份产业作为永久的基业。"永久"这个词是理解路得记一切的关键。

**Leviticus 25: The Right of Redemption** **利未记25章：救赎的权利**

The land could not be sold permanently:

土地不可永久出售：

> _"The land shall not be sold in perpetuity, for the land is mine. You are strangers and sojourners with me."_ — Leviticus 25:23
> 
> _"地不可永卖，因为地是我的；你们在我面前是客旅，是寄居的。"_——利未记25:23

However, if someone fell into poverty and had to sell, his nearest kinsman — the _go'el_, the **redeemer** — had the right and the responsibility to buy it back at the original price. If no redeemer was available, and the original owner later became prosperous, he could buy it back himself — but had to pay a price adjusted for the years elapsed. And if neither happened? Every fiftieth year — the **Year of Jubilee** (7×7=49 years, then the 50th) — the land automatically reverted to the original family. God's perfect number times God's perfect number. No one ever permanently lost their inheritance.

然而若有人因贫穷不得不出售，他最近的亲属——_go'el_，即**救赎者**——有权利也有责任以原价将其买回。若找不到救赎者，而原业主后来富裕了，他可以自己赎回——但价格须根据已过的年数调整。若两者都没有发生？每五十年——**禧年**（7×7=49年，然后第50年）——土地自动归还原来的家族。神的完全数乘以神的完全数。没有人会永久失去自己的产业。

The goal of all Israelite property law can be summarized in one sentence: **the land stays with the tribe, and with the family.**

以色列所有财产律法的目标可以用一句话概括：**土地留在支派中，留在家族里。**

**Deuteronomy 25: Levirate Marriage** **申命记25章：兄弟婚（收继婚）**

Now here's something that will feel strange to modern ears. If a married man died without a son, his brother was obligated to marry the widow and produce an heir. That child would legally bear the dead man's name and inherit his property — not the brother's. The purpose was entirely about keeping the land in the family.

现在有一件事对现代人来说会感到陌生。若一个已婚男人死去且无子嗣，他的兄弟有义务娶寡嫂并养育后嗣。那个孩子在法律上承继死者的名分并继承其产业——而不是兄弟的。这一制度的目的完全是为了让土地留在家族之中。

If the brother refused? The widow would go before the elders, pull off his sandal, and spit in his face. In that culture, there was no greater public disgrace. God actually _built that shame into the law_ to enforce compliance.

若兄弟拒绝？寡妇要当着众长老的面脱下他的鞋，吐在他脸上。在那个文化中，没有比这更大的公开羞辱了。神实际上_将这种羞辱写进了律法_，以强制执行这一义务。

One critical note: **levirate marriage does NOT apply in the book of Ruth.** There are no surviving brothers of Elimelech, Mahlon, or Chilion. Many readers assume it does, especially during the confrontation between Boaz and the unnamed redeemer in chapter 4 — but it doesn't. Keep that in mind.

一个关键说明：**兄弟婚制度在路得记中并不适用。**以利米勒、玛伦和基连都没有在世的兄弟。许多读者以为适用，尤其是在第四章波阿斯与那位无名救赎者对峙时——但实际上不适用。请记住这一点。

### Why Moab? Why Does It Matter?

### 为何是摩押？这有何重要性？

Ruth is a Moabite. She will be called a Moabite **eight times** across four chapters. If you're reading and thinking, _"Yes, we know, why do you keep saying it?"_ — that's exactly the point. The author wants you to feel it every single time.

路得是摩押女子。她在四章中被称为摩押女子**八次**。如果你读到这里心想："好了我们知道了，为什么反复说？"——这正是重点所在。作者想让你每次读到都有感觉。

To understand why, we have to go all the way back to Genesis 19. TVMA warning.

要理解原因，我们必须追溯到创世记19章。内容较为成熟，请注意。

Sodom and Gomorrah are destroyed. Lot and his two daughters escape. They end up in a cave in the hills. The daughters, convinced that all mankind has been wiped out — they apparently forgot that other cities still existed — hatch a plan. They make their father drunk two nights in a row and sleep with him. He is so drunk he doesn't even know what happened either night.

所多玛和蛾摩拉被毁灭。罗得与两个女儿逃脱，最终住在山洞里。两个女儿确信世上的男人都已被消灭——她们显然忘了还有其他城市存在——密谋了一个计划。她们连续两晚让父亲喝醉，然后与他同寝。他醉得根本不知道发生了什么。

The firstborn daughter's son was named **Moab** — which in Hebrew sounds like _"from father."_ She didn't even try to hide what happened. She named her sin and gave birth to it. The younger daughter's son was Ben-ammi — father of the Ammonites. Both peoples would go on to harass Israel for generations.

大女儿所生的儿子名叫**摩押**——希伯来语听起来像"从父而来"。她甚至没有试图掩盖所发生的事。她将自己的罪命名，并生育了它。小女儿所生的儿子是便亚米——亚扪人的祖先。这两个民族此后数代都骚扰以色列。

Then, when Israel came out of Egypt and needed to pass through Moabite territory, the Moabites didn't just refuse to help — they hired Balaam to curse God's people (Numbers 22). God turned that curse into blessing. And then God responded with one of the most severe judgments in all the Old Testament:

后来，以色列人出埃及需要经过摩押的土地时，摩押人不仅拒绝帮助，还雇用巴兰来咒诅神的百姓（民数记22章）。神将那咒诅转为祝福。然后神发出了整个旧约中最严厉的判决之一：

> _"No Ammonite or Moabite may enter the assembly of the Lord. Even to the tenth generation, none of them may enter the assembly of the Lord **forever**."_ — Deuteronomy 23:3
> 
> _"亚扪人或摩押人不可入耶和华的会；他们的子孙，虽过十代，也**永不**可入耶和华的会。"_——申命记23:3

God doesn't even say this about Egypt. For Egypt, the third generation could enter the assembly, because they were far enough removed from the original sin. But for Moab — not even to the tenth generation. Not ever. Forever excluded.

神对埃及都没有这样说。对埃及人，第三代就可以入会，因为他们距离当初的罪已经足够远。但对摩押——甚至过了十代都不行。永远不行。永远被排除在外。

And yet. Ruth is a Moabite. **This is why it's beautiful.**

然而。路得是摩押女子。**这正是路得记之所以美丽的原因。**

The number eight in Scripture almost always points to eternity — boys were circumcised on the eighth day, entering the covenant; the eighth day begins a new week that never ends. God swore off the Moabites _forever_. And he's going to redeem Ruth _forever_. Eight times Moabite. Eight times eternity. The tension is held deliberately.

圣经中数字八几乎总是指向永恒——男孩在第八天受割礼，进入立约；第八天开启永无止尽的新一周。神_永远_弃绝了摩押人。而祂却要_永远_救赎路得。八次摩押女子。八次永恒。这个张力是刻意保留的。

And Ruth is called "Ruth the Moabite" directly by name exactly **five times**. Five, in Hebrew tradition and Christian liturgical usage, represents the **five wounds of Christ** — the nails in His hands, the nail in His feet, the pierced side, the crown of thorns. You'll find five incense nails on the paschal candle every Easter, representing exactly this. Ruth the Moabite — five times — is redeemed through the passion of Christ. Every number the author chose is doing theological work.

而路得被直接称为"摩押女子路得"恰好**五次**。五，在希伯来传统和基督教礼仪传统中，代表**基督的五处伤痕**——手上的钉痕、脚上的钉痕、被刺的肋旁、荆棘的冠冕。每年复活节你都会在逾越节蜡烛上看到五根乳香钉，正代表于此。摩押女子路得——五次——通过基督的受难得到救赎。作者选择的每个数字都在做神学工作。

### The Story: Naomi Returns Empty

### 故事：拿俄米空手而归

_"In the days when the judges ruled, there was a famine in the land."_ (Ruth 1:1)

_"当士师秉政的时候，国中遭遇饥荒。"_（路得记1:1）

The famine is God's agent. As with Joseph being sold into Egypt, as with Abraham going down to Egypt — famines in the Bible move people to where they need to be. Without this famine, none of what follows would have happened. Who controls famines? Only God.

饥荒是神的器具。就像约瑟被卖到埃及，就像亚伯拉罕下到埃及——圣经中的饥荒推动人们去他们需要去的地方。没有这场饥荒，后来的一切都不会发生。谁能掌控饥荒？只有神。

A man named Elimelech — from Bethlehem in Judah, the tribe of Judah, the same tribe Jesus will be born into — leaves with his wife Naomi and sons Mahlon and Chilion for Moab. Before going, he most likely sold the usage rights of his land to a kinsman in the tribe. The land remains his in principle — he just can't work it from Moab.

一个名叫以利米勒的人——来自犹大的伯利恒，属犹大支派，也是耶稣将要降生的那个支派——带着妻子拿俄米和儿子玛伦、基连前往摩押。临行前，他很可能将土地的使用权卖给了同支派的亲属。土地在原则上仍属于他——只是他在摩押无法耕种而已。

In Moab, Elimelech dies. His sons take Moabite wives — Orpah and Ruth — something Israelites were not supposed to do, but as Pastor Eric says with a smile, _"you can't stand in the way of love."_ After about ten years, both sons die too, leaving no children. Now Naomi has no husband, no sons, no legal representative, no heir, and two daughters-in-law who have no legal obligation to stay with her.

在摩押，以利米勒死了。他的儿子娶了摩押女子为妻——俄珥巴和路得——这是以色列人本不应做的事，但正如Pastor Eric含笑所说："拦不住爱情嘛。"大约十年后，两个儿子也相继去世，且没有留下孩子。现在拿俄米没有丈夫、没有儿子、没有法律代理人、没有后嗣，还有两个在法律上没有义务留下来的媳妇。

She hears the famine in Judah is over. She sets out to return. On the road, she tells both daughters-in-law to go back to their mothers' houses — to find new husbands, to live their lives. She uses the name of the Lord — _Yahweh_ — to bless them. She doesn't say "the God I worship bless you." She says "the LORD bless you." They already know who she means. This tells us that through years of shared family life, Naomi had been _evangelizing these Moabite women_. Evangelism, it turns out, happens best in families.

她听说犹大的饥荒已过，便启程回去。在路上，她叫两个媳妇各自回娘家——去寻找新的丈夫，重新生活。她用神的名——_耶和华_——来祝福她们。她没有说"我所敬拜的神祝福你们"，她说"愿耶和华祝福你们"。她们已经知道她指的是谁。这告诉我们，通过多年的家庭共同生活，拿俄米一直在_向这两个摩押女子传福音_。原来，福音最好的传播方式就是在家庭之中。

Orpah kisses Naomi and turns back. She returns to her people and her gods — the text doesn't condemn her for it, because Ruth contains almost no condemnation of sin at all. It is almost entirely gospel. But Ruth? Ruth clings to Naomi:

俄珥巴吻别拿俄米转身回去。她回到自己的民中和她的神那里——经文没有为此谴责她，因为路得记几乎没有任何对罪的定罪。它几乎完全是福音。但路得呢？路得紧紧跟随拿俄米：

> _"Do not urge me to leave you or to return from following you. For where you go I will go, and where you lodge I will lodge. Your people shall be my people, and your God my God. Where you die I will die, and there will I be buried."_ — Ruth 1:16-17
> 
> _"你往哪里去，我也往那里去；你在哪里住宿，我也在那里住宿；你的国就是我的国，你的神就是我的神。你在哪里死，我也在那里死，也葬在那里。"_——路得记1:16-17

This is Ruth's conversion. She is saying: I've left that world behind. I've come to know Yahweh through Naomi's witness. I would rather be a despised widow in Israel, worshipping the true God, than go home to comfort and familiarity. The clinging she does here is similar to the language used of Mary Magdalene when she clings to the risen Jesus in the garden — an overwhelming, loving, _I-am-not-letting-go_ grip.

这是路得的归信。她在说：我已经离开了那个世界。我通过拿俄米的见证认识了耶和华。我宁愿在以色列做一个被轻视的寡妇、敬拜真神，也不愿回到舒适熟悉的家乡。她在这里的紧跟，与抹大拉的马利亚在园中紧抱复活的耶稣时的描述语言相似——那是一种压倒性的、充满爱的、_我-不-松-手_的紧握。

They arrive in Bethlehem — _Bet Lechem_ in Hebrew, meaning **"House of Bread"** — at the beginning of the barley harvest. The whole town is stirred. People who knew Naomi before can barely recognize her. She tells them: _"Do not call me Naomi"_ — which means "Pleasant" — _"call me Mara"_ — which means "Bitter." She says: I went away full, and the Lord has brought me back empty.

她们到达伯利恒——希伯来语 _Bet Lechem_，意为**"饼家"**——正是大麦收割开始的时候。全城都震动了。认识拿俄米的人几乎认不出她来了。她告诉她们："不要叫我拿俄米"——意思是"甜"——"要叫我玛拉"——意思是"苦"。她说：我满满地出去，耶和华使我空空地回来。

This is not a lack of faith. This is not blasphemy. Naomi is simply being honest: God could have prevented this and He didn't. I don't understand why, and I'm not happy about it — but I still believe He holds everything in His hands. The book of Ruth contains almost no condemnation of sin. What it does contain, in Naomi's bitterness, is raw and honest lament — and that, too, is a mark of faith. Jesus was angry. Paul says _"be angry and do not sin."_ Being angry at the way things are is not the same as cursing God.

这不是信心的缺失，这不是亵渎。拿俄米只是在诚实地说：神本可以阻止这一切，但祂没有。我不明白为什么，我也不高兴——但我仍然相信祂掌管一切。路得记几乎不对罪进行任何定罪。但它包含的，是拿俄米的苦涩中那原始而诚实的哀叹——而这，也是信心的印记。耶稣曾发怒，保罗说"生气却不要犯罪"。对现实处境感到愤怒，与咒诅神并不相同。

Note also: _Mara_ — bitter — is the root of the name _Mary_. Years later in Bethlehem, another Mary will arrive, and where Naomi said _"I went away full and came back empty,"_ Mary sings: _"He has filled the hungry with good things, and the rich he has sent away empty."_ God reverses Naomi's experience at Bethlehem through her namesake, Mary, at the birth of the Savior. The Bible is not a collection of disconnected stories. It is one story.

还需注意：_玛拉_——苦——是名字_马利亚_（Mary）的词根。多年后在伯利恒，另一位马利亚将要到来，拿俄米说"我满满地出去，空空地回来"，马利亚却唱道："他叫饥饿的得饱美食，叫富足的空手回去。"神在伯利恒，藉着与拿俄米同名的马利亚，在救主降生时翻转了拿俄米的经历。圣经不是一堆互不相关的故事，它是一个故事。

### Remember Points for Chapter 1

### 第一章记住要点

> **1. Let the Bible say what it means. 让圣经说它本身的意思。** 不要把你的猜测和偏见读进去。

> **2. Evangelism happens best in families. 福音在家庭中传播最有效。** 拿俄米在多年的家庭生活中，悄悄地将信仰传给了俄珥巴和路得。为你孩子的婚姻祷告——现在就开始。

> **3. God hates Moab — and yet His mercy gets the better of Him. 神憎恶摩押——然而祂的怜悯胜过了一切。** 神永远弃绝了摩押人。然而祂要使一个摩押女子成为耶稣的祖先。若没有路得，就没有耶稣。

> **4. The land must stay with the tribe and the family. 土地必须留在支派和家族中。** 这条律法是整个路得记情节的骨架。牢记于心。

# Chapter 2 · 第二章

### Boaz the Worthy Man: A Picture of Jesus

### 波阿斯，那个有德行的人：耶稣的预表

---

### Introducing Boaz: The Total Package

### 介绍波阿斯：完整的男人

_"Now Naomi had a relative of her husband's, a worthy man of the clan of Elimelech, whose name was Boaz."_ (Ruth 2:1)

_"拿俄米的丈夫以利米勒的亲族中，有一个人名叫波阿斯，是个大财主。"_（路得记2:1）

Before we go any further, let's set up the most important equation for the rest of this study:

在继续之前，让我们建立贯穿整个研究的最重要方程式：

> **Boaz = Jesus. Ruth = Us (the Church).** **波阿斯 ＝ 耶稣。路得 ＝ 我们（教会）。**

Everything Boaz does and is, points forward to Jesus. Everything Ruth is — a foreigner with no standing, no rights, no claim — points to us. There is no reason God should love us. There is no reason Boaz should care about Ruth. And yet.

波阿斯的一切所是和所做，都预表耶稣。路得的一切所是——一个外邦人，没有地位，没有权利，没有资格——都指向我们。神没有理由爱我们。波阿斯没有理由在乎路得。然而。

Now: Boaz is described as a _"worthy man"_ — and in Hebrew, the phrase used is actually _gibbor chayil_, a **"mighty man of valor."** That phrase in the Old Testament is reserved for elite warriors — think Navy SEALs, the best of the best, men who had been to war and were frankly terrifying. That's the level of respect and strength this phrase carries. But here it's used not in the context of battle, but of Boaz's character, his honor, his integrity. He is the mench of menches — everything good about masculinity embodied in one person.

现在：波阿斯被描述为"大财主"——希伯来语原文实际上是 _gibbor chayil_，即**"大能的勇士"**。旧约中这个词语专门用于精英战士——想想海豹突击队，最精锐的人，上过战场、令人望而生畏的人。这个词携带的是这种级别的尊重与力量。但这里它用于波阿斯的品格、荣誉与正直，而非战场背景。他是男子汉中的男子汉——一切男性美德的化身。

He is honorable. He is generous. He is protective. He is strong. He is a leader of men who are obeyed — not because they're afraid of him, but because they respect him. Compare that with Ruth: she is immediately called "Ruth the Moabite" again — a foreigner from the worst of foreign places, a widow, with no legal standing and no claim on anything. The contrast the author sets up is sharp and deliberate.

他正直，慷慨，有保护意识，强壮，是被人服从的领袖——人们服从他不是因为害怕他，而是因为尊重他。与之对比的是路得：她立刻又被称为"摩押女子路得"——一个来自最糟糕外邦之地的外邦人，一个寡妇，没有法律地位，对任何事物都没有任何主张。作者刻意建立的对比是鲜明而刻意的。

And one critical reminder: **let the Bible say what it means.** There has always been a temptation — ancient and modern — to read impropriety into the relationship between Boaz and Ruth. People want to wink and say, _"Sure he's a 'worthy man,' but he was human too, right?"_ Pastor Eric has zero patience for this. The Bible tells us Boaz was a righteous man. The same word is used of Joseph — who "did not know" Mary until after they were married. To read immorality into Boaz and Ruth is to deny the biblical truth sitting right in front of you. We'll return to this forcefully when we reach chapter 3.

还有一个关键提醒：**让圣经说它本身的意思。**人们一直有一种诱惑——古今皆然——要把不正当的意味读进波阿斯与路得的关系中。人们想使个眼色说："他确实是'大能的勇士'，但他也是人嘛，对吧？"Pastor Eric对此毫无耐心。圣经告诉我们波阿斯是个正直的人。这个词同样用于约瑟——他在婚前"没有认识"马利亚。将不道德的意味读进波阿斯和路得，就是否认摆在你面前的圣经真理。我们在第三章时会再次有力地回到这一点。

### The Welfare System God Designed

### 神设计的福利制度

Ruth says to Naomi: _"Let me go to the field and glean."_ To understand this, we need to go to Leviticus 23:22:

路得对拿俄米说："容我往田间去，我蒙谁的恩，就在谁的身后拾取麦穗。"要理解这一点，我们需要去利未记23:22：

> _"When you reap the harvest of your land, you shall not reap your field right up to its edge, nor shall you gather the gleanings after your harvest. You shall leave them for the poor and for the sojourner."_
> 
> _"你们在自己的地收割庄稼，不可割尽田角，也不可拾取所遗落的；要留给穷人和寄居的外人。"_

God built a food bank into the harvest law. Whatever fell on the ground during reaping — the farmer could not go back and pick it up. It had to be left. Widows, foreigners, the poor — they followed behind the reapers and gathered what was left. This was Ruth's plan. She had no legal standing, she was a foreigner from the most despised nation, and she was prepared to be at the very back of the line of gleaners — getting the leftover of the leftovers. She went out early and worked without stopping.

神将一个食物救济制度内置于收割律法中。收割期间凡落在地上的——农夫不可回去捡取，必须留下。寡妇、外邦人、穷人——他们跟在收割者身后拾取剩下的。这就是路得的计划。她没有法律地位，她是来自最被轻视民族的外邦人，她准备站在拾穗者队伍的最末尾——捡取剩余之物的剩余。她一大早出去，毫不停歇地工作。

By the way — Ruth is stronger than she looks. By the time we finish chapter 2, we'll know she carried home about **30 pounds of grain** — an ephah of barley. Picture six 5-pound bags of flour, then imagine carrying them several miles home without a backpack. Pastor Eric's advice: _don't arm-wrestle Ruth._ She will throw you off the table.

顺便说一句——路得比看上去更强壮。读完第二章，我们会知道她带回家了大约**30磅的粮食**——一伊法大麦。想象六袋5磅装的面粉，然后想象没有背包、徒步数英里背回家。Pastor Eric的建议：*不要跟路得掰手腕。*她会把你从桌上扔出去。

### Boaz Arrives: The First Picture of Jesus

### 波阿斯到来：第一幅耶稣的图画

_"And behold, Boaz came from Bethlehem."_ (Ruth 2:4)

_"波阿斯正从伯利恒来。"_（路得记2:4）

Stop. The author wants you to notice something:

停。作者想让你注意一件事：

Boaz. Tribe of Judah. From **Bethlehem**. Righteous. A redeemer. Go ahead and think about Jesus of Nazareth: tribe of Judah, born in **Bethlehem**, righteous, the Redeemer. This is not a coincidence. From this moment on, Boaz is waving a giant flag that says: _when I talk about Boaz, start thinking about Jesus._

波阿斯。犹大支派。来自**伯利恒**。正直。救赎者。请想想拿撒勒人耶稣：犹大支派，降生在**伯利恒**，正直，救赎主。这不是巧合。从这一刻起，波阿斯举着一面大旗，上面写着：_当我谈到波阿斯，开始想想耶稣。_

The very first thing Boaz does when he arrives at his field? He greets his workers:

波阿斯到达田间做的第一件事是什么？他向工人们打招呼：

> _"The Lord be with you!" — "The Lord bless you!"_
> 
> _"愿耶和华与你们同在！"——"愿耶和华赐福与你！"_

This is antiphonal worship — call and response, exactly like the liturgy. _"The Lord be with you" — "And also with you."_ Boaz has taught his household and his employees this exchange. He is not ashamed of his faith in front of his workers. He lives it publicly, in the field, every day. That's part of what makes him a mench.

这是应答式礼拜——一问一答，就像礼仪中一样。"愿主与你同在"——"也与你的灵同在。"波阿斯已经教导他的家人和雇员这种应答。他在工人面前不以自己的信仰为耻。他在田间、每天都公开地活出信仰。这正是他作为男子汉的一部分。

His foreman tells him about Ruth: _"She is the Moabite woman who came back with Naomi from the country of Moab."_ He doesn't even know her name. Just: she's a Moabite. Right here, Boaz has a choice. He could say: _"Get her out of here. She has no right to be on my land."_ His foreman is probably watching, waiting to see how he handles this.

他的管工告诉他关于路得的事："那是跟拿俄米从摩押地回来的摩押女子。"他甚至不知道她的名字，只知道：她是摩押女子。就在这里，波阿斯面临选择。他可以说："把她赶走。她没有权利在我的地里。"他的管工大概正在旁观，等着看他如何处理。

Instead, he walks up to Ruth and says — **listen, my daughter.** He adopts her before he knows anything about her. He calls her the same thing Naomi calls her. He's saying: you are connected to my family. You belong here. He doesn't wait to determine whether she's legally entitled to his kindness. He leads with mercy.

波阿斯却走向路得，说——**我女儿啊，你听我说。**他在对她一无所知之前就收养了她。他用与拿俄米同样的称呼叫她。他在说：你与我的家族有联系，你属于这里。他没有等着判断她是否在法律上有权得到他的善待，他以怜悯为先。

Then he tells her: _"Do not go to glean in another field or leave this one, but keep close to my young women."_ He is not kicking her out. He is **forcing her to stay.** And then comes this: _"Have I not charged the young men not to touch you?"_

然后他告诉她："不要往别人田里去，也不要离开这里，要与我的使女们常在一处。"他不是在赶她走，他是在**强迫她留下。**接着是这句话："我岂不是吩咐那些仆人不可欺负你吗？"

Ruth didn't know this. Boaz had already, before she arrived, sat his hired men down and told them: you do not touch this woman. I don't care where she's from. If you lay a hand on her, you answer to me. And the Hebrew here is in the perfect tense — it is already accomplished, as sure and certain as God saying _"Let there be light."_ His command is done. The protection is absolute.

路得不知道这件事。波阿斯在她到来之前，已经把雇来的男工们叫到一起告诉他们：你们不可欺负这个女人，不管她从哪里来，若有人动她，就要向我交账。这里的希伯来语是完成时态——这已是既成事实，如同神说"要有光"一样确定无疑。他的命令已经执行，保护是绝对的。

Then, unbelievably, he tells her: _"And when you are thirsty, go to the vessels and drink what the young men have drawn."_

然后，令人难以置信的是，他告诉她："你若渴了，就去喝仆人打来的水。"

This needs context. In that culture, foreigners drew water for Israelites. Women drew water for men. Ruth, being both a foreigner and a woman, would have been expected to draw water for everyone else — the very bottom of the hierarchy. Instead, Boaz has his young men draw water specifically for her. He has completely inverted the social order on her behalf.

这需要背景说明。在那个文化中，外邦人为以色列人打水，女人为男人打水。路得既是外邦人又是女人，本应为其他所有人打水——处于等级制度的最底层。但波阿斯却让他的仆人专门为她打水。他完全为她颠倒了社会秩序。

Jump to John 4. Jesus — a man from Bethlehem, an Israelite — sits at Jacob's well. A Samaritan woman comes to draw water. Samaritans were despised by Jews, and this woman was additionally living in sin — shacked up with a man who wasn't her husband. Jesus says to her: _"Give me a drink."_ She's a little snotty about it: _"You're a Jew, why are you asking me?"_ And Jesus says: _"If you knew who I was, you would have asked me, and I would have given you living water."_ A man from Bethlehem — an Israelite — offering water to a foreign woman of the lowest social standing. Sound familiar? Boaz and the Samaritan woman at the well are pointing at the same Jesus, from opposite ends of the Bible.

跳到约翰福音4章。耶稣——一个来自伯利恒的以色列人——坐在雅各井旁。一个撒玛利亚女人来打水。撒玛利亚人被犹太人所轻视，这个女人还与一个非她丈夫的男人同居。耶稣对她说："请你给我水喝。"她有点不客气地回应："你是犹太人，为什么向我要水喝？"耶稣说："你若知道我是谁，你早就求我了，我也早就给了你活水。"一个来自伯利恒的以色列人——向一个社会地位最低的外邦女人提供水。听起来熟悉吗？波阿斯和撒玛利亚妇人，从圣经的两端，指向同一个耶稣。

### Meal at the Table: More Connections

### 桌上的饭食：更多联系

At mealtime, Boaz invites Ruth to his table: _"Come here and eat some bread and dip your morsel in the wine."_ She sat beside the reapers — with the men — and Boaz personally passed her roasted grain. She ate until she was **satisfied**, and had some **left over**.

到了吃饭的时候，波阿斯邀请路得到他的桌前："你来这里吃饼，将饼蘸在醋里。"她坐在收割的人旁边——与男人们同桌——波阿斯亲自递给她炒麦粒。她吃饱了，还**有剩余**。

Two connections: First, Matthew 26 — Jesus at the Last Supper, eating with His disciples. Among them is Judas, the one who will betray Him. And yet Jesus doesn't say: _"You don't belong here. Get out."_ He sits at table with the worst of the worst, dipping bread in the same dish. Boaz does the same with Ruth — the lowest of the low, invited to the table of the most honored man.

两个联系：第一，马太福音26章——耶稣在最后的晚餐与门徒同席。其中有犹大，那个将要出卖祂的人。耶稣却没有说："你不属于这里，出去。"祂与最糟糕的人同席，将饼蘸在同一个碟子里。波阿斯也如此对路得——最卑微的人，被邀请到最受尊敬的人的桌前。

Second: the feeding of the 5,000 (Matthew 14). Jesus broke the loaves and gave them to the disciples, who gave them to the crowd. They all ate and were **satisfied**, and they took up **twelve baskets of broken pieces left over**. Jesus always provides more than is necessary. Boaz does the same — he doesn't just feed her, he gives her more than she can eat, and she gets to take it home.

第二：五千人得吃饱（马太福音14章）。耶稣擘饼交给门徒，门徒又分给众人。众人都吃**饱了**，还收拾了剩下的零碎，装满了**十二个篮子**。耶稣总是提供超过所需的。波阿斯也一样——他不仅仅是喂饱她，还给了她超出能吃的，让她可以带回家。

He's not done. Back out in the field, Boaz instructs his workers: _"Let her glean even among the sheaves, and do not rebuke her, and also pull out some from the bundles for her and leave it for her to glean."_ Not only is she allowed at the front of the line — he's telling his reapers to deliberately drop extra grain for her to pick up. You can imagine his workers thinking: _"Boss, really? You're already doing so much. Now you want us to sabotage our own harvest on purpose?"_ But nobody questions Boaz. Nobody. He is the final word. And so they do it.

他还没有结束。回到田间，波阿斯吩咐工人们："也要从捆里抽出些来，留在地下任她拾取，不可叱吓她。"她不仅被允许站在队伍最前面——他还让收割的人故意丢下额外的谷物让她捡取。可以想象他的工人们心想："老板，真的吗？您已经做了这么多了。现在还要我们故意破坏自己的收成？"但没有人质疑波阿斯，一个人都没有。他是最终的权威，工人们照做了。

### Naomi Lights Up: "That Man Is Our Redeemer"

### 拿俄米眼睛发亮："那人是我们的至近亲属"

Ruth comes home with 30 pounds of grain plus her leftover lunch. Naomi sees it and is astonished: _"Where did you glean today? Blessed be the man who took notice of you!"_ Ruth tells her: Boaz. And a light goes on for Naomi.

路得回到家，带着30磅的粮食加上午饭的剩余。拿俄米看见大为惊讶："你今日在哪里拾取麦穗，在哪里做工呢？愿那顾念你的得福！"路得告诉她：波阿斯。拿俄米眼睛立刻发亮了。

> _"May he be blessed by the Lord, whose kindness has not forsaken the living or the dead! This man is a close relative of ours, one of our redeemers."_
> 
> _"愿那人蒙耶和华赐福，因为他不断地恩待活人死人。"又说："那人与我们至近，是我们一个至近的亲属。"_

The word _redeemer_ — _go'el_ — will appear **seven times** in the next chapter. Start hearing it everywhere, because it is the linchpin of everything. Naomi senses that this might be the way God restores everything — the land, their standing, their future. She doesn't seek out the nearer redeemer first. She has her eyes on Boaz. Maybe she knows something about the other guy — that he's kind of a schlub. We don't know. But she is betting on Boaz.

"救赎者"这个词——_go'el_——在下一章将出现**七次**。开始在各处听见它，因为它是一切的枢纽。拿俄米感到这可能就是神恢复一切的方式——土地、地位、未来。她没有先去寻找那位更近的亲属，她的眼睛已经锁定在波阿斯身上。也许她对另一个人有所了解——他是个不靠谱的家伙。我们不知道。但她把赌注押在了波阿斯身上。

And notice: the narrator, at this point, calls Ruth "Ruth the Moabite" again. Why now? Because Naomi just said _"one of our redeemers"_ — and the narrator is pointing out the irony. God swore the Moabites would never be redeemed. And here Naomi is saying: _yes, he's one of ours._ She already knows the grace of God will welcome in the ones He has sworn off.

注意：叙事者在这里再次称路得为"摩押女子路得"。为何在这时候？因为拿俄米刚刚说了"我们的一个至近亲属"——叙事者是在指出其中的反讽。神曾宣告摩押人永不得救赎，而拿俄米却说：*是的，他是我们的一个。*她已经知道，神的恩典会接纳那些被祂弃绝的人。

So she kept close to the young women of Boaz, gleaning until the end of the barley and wheat harvests. And she lived with her mother-in-law. Time passes. A relationship quietly deepens. The stage is set for chapter 3.

于是路得紧随波阿斯的使女们，直到收完大麦和小麦，住在婆婆家里。时光流逝，一段关系悄悄深化。第三章的舞台已经搭好。

### Remember Points for Chapter 2

### 第二章记住要点

> **Boaz = Jesus. Ruth = Us.** This equation runs through everything. **波阿斯 ＝ 耶稣。路得 ＝ 我们。**这个方程式贯穿一切。

> **Boaz is the picture of biblical masculinity. 波阿斯是圣经中男子气概的图画。** 正直，慷慨，以信仰为荣，保护弱者，言出必行，赢得人心。如果你想要一个圣经中男性品格的榜样，波阿斯就是。

> **Ruth is the picture of biblical femininity. 路得是圣经中女性气质的图画。** 勤劳，有礼，勇敢，体贴，忠诚，刚强。她扛着30磅的粮食走了好几英里。不要轻视她。

> **Pray for the young people in your life. 为你生命中的年轻人祷告。** 愿他们找到自己的波阿斯，找到自己的路得——彼此忠信，共同扎根于神的话语。

# Chapter 3 · 第三章

### The Threshing Floor: Great Risk, Greater Righteousness

### 禾场之夜：巨大的风险，更大的正直

---

### Everything Is Done Freely — No One Owes Anyone Anything

### 一切出于自由意志——没有人亏欠任何人

Before we get into the dramatic events of this chapter, Pastor Eric wants us to hold onto one foundational truth that makes everything in it so extraordinary:

在进入这一章的戏剧性事件之前，Pastor Eric希望我们抓住一个基础性的真理，正是这个真理使这章中的一切如此非凡：

**Nobody in this story owes anybody anything.**

**这个故事里没有人亏欠任何人任何东西。**

Ruth's husband is dead. That death dissolved the marriage covenant and severed every legal tie she had to Naomi and to Israel. Naomi owes Ruth nothing. Ruth owes Naomi nothing. Israel owes Ruth nothing. There is no legal obligation anywhere in this chapter. Ruth didn't have to go out and glean for Naomi — she could have gone home to Moab, found a nice young Moabite man, and lived happily ever after. But she chose to stay and care for an elderly woman who had no claim on her whatsoever.

路得的丈夫死了。那死亡解除了婚约，切断了她与拿俄米和以色列之间的所有法律联系。拿俄米对路得没有亏欠，路得对拿俄米没有亏欠，以色列对路得没有亏欠。这一章中任何地方都没有法律义务。路得本不需要出去为拿俄米拾取麦穗——她可以回摩押，找一个好的摩押年轻人，从此过上幸福的生活。但她选择留下来，照顾一个对她毫无主张的老年妇人。

And as for Boaz — he barely has any legal obligation toward Naomi, and he has **zero** legal obligation toward Ruth. Yet everything he does in this chapter, he does freely, out of righteousness, out of mercy, out of the goodness of his heart.

至于波阿斯——他对拿俄米几乎没有任何法律义务，对路得则是**零**法律义务。然而他在这章中所做的一切，都是出于自由意志，出于正直，出于怜悯，出于内心的善良。

This is, of course, a microcosm of what God does for us. He doesn't owe us redemption. We broke His covenant. He held up His end of the bargain — we didn't. And yet He redeems us anyway. As Luther's Small Catechism puts it: _"without any merit or worthiness in me, out of pure fatherly divine goodness and mercy."_ That is Boaz. That is Jesus. That is this chapter.

这当然是神为我们所做之事的缩影。祂不亏欠我们救赎。是我们破坏了祂的约，祂履行了自己的那一份——我们没有履行。然而祂仍然救赎我们。正如路德小教义问答所说：*"不是出于我任何的功劳和价值，而是出于纯粹的父亲般的神圣善良和怜悯。"*这就是波阿斯，这就是耶稣，这就是这一章。

---

### Naomi's Plan: Bold, Risky, and Brilliant

### 拿俄米的计划：大胆、冒险而聪明

Naomi calls Ruth and lays out a plan. She has heard — through what we might call the ancient small-town gossip network — that Boaz will be at the threshing floor tonight, winnowing barley. She tells Ruth:

拿俄米叫来路得，制定了一个计划。她听说——通过我们可能称之为古代小镇八卦网络的渠道——波阿斯今晚要在禾场上扬大麦。她告诉路得：

> _"Wash therefore and anoint yourself, and put on your cloak and go down to the threshing floor… but do not make yourself known to the man until he has finished eating and drinking. When he lies down, observe the place where he lies. Then go and uncover his feet and lie down, and he will tell you what to do."_ — Ruth 3:3-4
> 
> _"你要沐浴抹膏，换上衣服，下到禾场去……他未吃完喝完，你不要叫他知道你。他躺下的时候，你看准他躺的地方，就进去掀开他脚上的被，躺卧在那里，他自会告诉你当做什么。"_——路得记3:3-4

Now, Pastor Eric acknowledges frankly: if you read this with modern eyes and modern suspicions, nearly every sentence in this chapter sounds like it could be hinting at something improper. The threshing floor. Washing and anointing yourself. Waiting until he's eaten and drunk. Uncovering his feet. Lying down beside him at night. There are probably ten or twelve places in this chapter where the Hebrew words, _in a different form or context_, could carry immoral connotations.

现在，Pastor Eric坦率地承认：如果你用现代的眼光和现代的疑心来读这段经文，这一章中几乎每一句话听起来都好像在暗示什么不恰当的事情。禾场。沐浴抹膏。等他吃完喝完。掀开他脚上的被。夜晚躺在他身旁。这一章中大概有十到十二处地方，用的希伯来词语_在不同的语境或形式下_可以带有道德上不洁的含义。

**But.** The Bible has told us that Boaz is a righteous man. And the book of Ruth contains almost no condemnation of sin anywhere. We must not read our modern suspicions back into a text that God's Holy Spirit inspired. Remember: **let the Bible say what it means.**

**但是。**圣经已经告诉我们波阿斯是个正直的人。而且路得记几乎在任何地方都没有对罪的定罪。我们不可将现代的猜疑读回一个由神的圣灵所默示的经文。记住：**让圣经说它本身的意思。**

So let's read it charitably and carefully, the way the text demands.

所以让我们以经文所要求的方式，体贴而仔细地来读它。

**"Wash and anoint yourself."** Before this, Ruth would have been wearing widow's garments — the physical dress code of mourning that publicly announced: my husband has died, I am not available, I am still in grief. Many cultures still have equivalents today. Naomi is saying: your time of mourning for my son is over. It is time to re-enter society. The change of clothes is not seduction — it is the completion of a mourning period. It is Naomi giving her daughter-in-law permission to live again.

**"沐浴抹膏，换上衣服。"**在此之前，路得一直穿着寡妇的丧服——这是一种公开宣告哀悼的着装规范：我的丈夫已经去世，我无法被追求，我仍在悲痛中。许多文化今天仍然有类似的习俗。拿俄米是在说：你为我儿子守丧的时间已满，是时候重返社会了。换上衣服不是诱惑——而是哀悼期的结束，是拿俄米给她的媳妇重新生活的许可。

**"Do not make yourself known until he has finished eating and drinking."** Don't interrupt his workday. He's been threshing grain all day — a hard physical job — and now he's guarding it overnight. He eats supper, he finishes, he rests. That's all the text tells us. It does not say he was drunk. He is not Lot — who drank so much that he didn't know his own daughters were sleeping with him. Boaz had supper. He is on watch. He is completely aware.

**"他未吃完喝完，你不要叫他知道你。"**不要打断他的工作。他整天都在扬谷——这是繁重的体力劳动——现在他要在夜间看守。他吃了晚饭，吃完之后休息。这就是经文告诉我们的全部。经文没有说他喝醉了。他不是罗得——罗得喝得如此之多，甚至不知道自己的女儿与他同寝。波阿斯只是吃了晚饭，他在守夜，他完全清醒。

**"Uncover his feet."** A lot of people assume this is a euphemism for something physical — that Ruth crept in, undressed Boaz, and snuggled up next to him. Pastor Eric says: not at all. The Hebrew here actually says _"she uncovered the place of his feet."_ She simply pulled back his blanket slightly, exposing his feet to the cold night air. The idea is beautifully practical: the cold would gradually wake Boaz — he'd stir, feel the chill, roll over, and find someone there — _without_ being startled awake, shouting, and blowing the whole cover. This was Ruth's way of creating a quiet, private moment for an important conversation. Subtle. Thoughtful. Entirely above board.

**"掀开他脚上的被。"__很多人以为这是对某种身体行为的委婉说法——路得悄悄进来，给波阿斯脱衣，然后依偎在他身旁。Pastor Eric说：完全不是。这里的希伯来语实际上是说_"她掀开了他脚部的地方"_。她只是轻轻拉开他的被子，让他的脚暴露在寒冷的夜风中。这个想法非常实际：寒冷会逐渐唤醒波阿斯——他会动一动，感到凉意，翻个身，发现有人在那里——_而不是_被惊醒、大喊大叫，把整件事搞砸。这是路得为一次重要谈话创造安静私密时机的方式。细腻，体贴，完全正大光明。

There is also a deeper legal meaning here. Recall from Deuteronomy 25 that the act of redemption involved a sandal — a shoe removed and handed over, like a handshake. For Ruth to uncover the feet of Boaz is, on a deeper level, **a request for redemption** — an unspoken appeal for him to use his feet to redeem her. When we get to chapter 4, the sandal exchange will make this connection explicit. The feet are not incidental. They are the request.

这里还有更深的法律含义。回忆申命记25章，救赎的行为涉及一只鞋——脱下来递给对方，就像握手一样。对路得而言，掀开波阿斯脚上的被，在更深的层面上，是**一个救赎的请求**——一个无声的恳求，请他用他的脚来救赎她。当我们读到第四章，那只鞋的交换将使这个联系变得明确。脚不是无关紧要的，它就是那个请求。

---

### "Who Are You?" — The Only Time Ruth Is Just Ruth

### "你是谁？"——路得唯一一次只是路得

At midnight, Boaz shivers — the Hebrew word here means _shivered_, not groped; the word that could mean grope appears in a completely different form — rolls over, and finds a woman lying at his feet.

到了半夜，波阿斯一惊而醒——这里的希伯来词意思是_发抖_，而不是摸索；那个可能意味着摸索的词以完全不同的形式出现——他翻了个身，发现脚边躺着一个女人。

_"Who are you?"_

_"你是谁？"_

And she answers: _"I am Ruth, your servant."_

她回答："我是你的婢女路得。"

This is the only place in the entire book of Ruth where she is simply called **Ruth**. Not Ruth the Moabite. Not Ruth, Naomi's daughter-in-law. Not the Moabite woman. Just Ruth. In the Bible, to know someone's name is to know the fullness of who they are — it's why God gives His own name to Moses at the burning bush. And here, in this intimate, private, high-risk moment, the only person who gets to know her fully — just as herself — is her future husband. It is, in every sense of the word, a betrothal scene. Beautiful and quiet and full of meaning.

这是整本路得记中她唯一被简单称为**路得**的地方。不是摩押女子路得，不是拿俄米的媳妇路得，不是那个摩押女子。就是路得。在圣经中，知道一个人的名字就是知道他们的全部——这就是为什么神在燃烧的荆棘丛前把自己的名字告诉摩西。而在这个私密、亲密、充满风险的时刻，唯一完整地认识她——只作为她自己——的人，正是她未来的丈夫。这在每一个意义上都是一个订婚场景，美丽、安静而充满意义。

She continues: _"Spread your wings over your servant, for you are a redeemer."_

她继续说："求你用你的衣襟遮盖我，因为你是我的至近亲属。"

That phrase — _spread your wings_ — is not a euphemism for anything physical. It is the exact same word used to describe the cherubim spreading their wings over the ark of the covenant, over the mercy seat. It is a word of faithfulness, of God's covenant protection, of Yahweh's sheltering presence. Ruth is saying: _will you do what God would want you to do — be my redeemer, cover me as God covers His people?_ She calls on the ancient law and their distant family connection. Boaz could easily have said: _"Yes, technically, but nobody actually does that anymore."_ That is not who Boaz is.

那个词组——_用衣襟遮盖_——不是任何身体行为的委婉语。它正是用来描述基路伯展开翅膀遮盖约柜、遮盖施恩座的那个词。这是忠信的词，是神的立约保护，是耶和华庇护同在的词语。路得在说：*你愿意做神所希望你做的事吗——作我的救赎者，如同神遮盖祂的子民那样遮盖我？*她诉诸于那古老的律法和他们遥远的家族联系。波阿斯完全可以说："是的，从技术上讲，但没有人真的这样做了。"但这不是波阿斯其人。

---

### Boaz Responds: "Do Not Fear"

### 波阿斯的回应："不要惧怕"

> _"May you be blessed by the Lord, my daughter. You have made this last kindness greater than the first… Do not fear. I will do for you all that you ask."_ — Ruth 3:10-11
> 
> _"女儿啊，愿你蒙耶和华赐福……不要惧怕，凡你所说的，我都必照着行。"_——路得记3:10-11

First, he calls her _my daughter_ — ratifying the adoption Naomi began. He is saying: Naomi calls you family. Naomi is family to me. Therefore you are family to me. He doesn't say: _you're a Moabite, you have no claim on me._ He says: you're mine.

首先，他称她为_我女儿_——批准了拿俄米所开始的收养。他在说：拿俄米称你为家人，拿俄米是我的家人，因此你也是我的家人。他没有说：你是摩押人，你对我没有任何主张。他说：你是我的人。

Then he blesses her — using the Lord's name, putting Yahweh's blessing on her publicly. He lives his faith even here, alone in the dark, in a moment no one else will know about.

然后他祝福她——用神的名，公开地将耶和华的祝福加在她身上。即使在这里，在黑暗中独处，在一个没有人知道的时刻，他仍然活出他的信仰。

Then the most important words: _**"Do not fear."**_

然后是最重要的话：_**"不要惧怕。"**_

Pastor Eric points out that this chapter has what scholars call a **chiastic structure** — a literary pattern where the text builds to a central point, and then mirrors itself back out, like a set of brackets narrowing to the most important thing in the middle. The central point — the heart of the entire chapter — is these words in verse 11: _"Do not fear. I will do for you all that you ask."_

Pastor Eric指出，这一章具有学者所称的**交叉结构**——一种文学模式，文本向一个中心点推进，然后向外镜像反映，就像一组括号向中间最重要的事物收窄。中心点——整章的核心——就是第11节中的这句话："不要惧怕，凡你所说的，我都必照着行。"

Think about what those words would have meant to Ruth in that moment. She is alone. She has no legal recourse. She has no one to defend her. If Boaz had chosen to do something immoral and criminal, she had no way to stop him and no one to go to afterward. There is enormous risk in what she has done. And Boaz — this righteous man, this picture of Jesus — looks at her and says: _do not be afraid._ The same words the angels use when they deliver God's messages. _Do not be afraid._ Boaz here is acting as a messenger of God's redemption — an angel in human form, if you will.

想想那些话在那一刻对路得意味着什么。她独自一人。她没有法律救济途径。她没有人为她辩护。如果波阿斯选择做不道德的事，她无法阻止他，事后也无处申诉。她所做的事情伴随着巨大的风险。而波阿斯——这个正直的人，这个耶稣的预表——望着她说：*不要惧怕。*就是天使传达神旨意时所用的同样话语。*不要惧怕。*波阿斯在这里作为神救赎的使者而行动——如果你愿意，可以说他是人形的天使。

Paul echoes this in 1 Thessalonians 5: _"He who calls you is faithful; he will surely do it."_ Jesus says it in John 14: _"Let not your hearts be troubled, neither let them be afraid. In my Father's house are many rooms. I go to prepare a place for you… I will come again and take you to myself."_ The redeemer uses this language. Boaz is patterned on the same truth.

保罗在帖撒罗尼迦前书5章呼应了这一点："那召你们的本是信实的，他必成就这事。"耶稣在约翰福音14章说："你们心里不要忧愁，也不要胆怯……我父的家里有许多住处……我去原是为你们预备地方去……我必再来接你们到我那里去。"救赎者使用这样的语言。波阿斯是按同一真理模塑而成的。

_"I will do for you all that you ask."_ Submission — not to a boss lady, but to what is right. Ruth is asking for something that is aligned with God's will: that the land remain with the family, that the name of the dead not be blotted out, that a righteous woman not be abandoned. Boaz recognizes this and says: _yes_. Then he reveals something Ruth may not have known about herself:

*"凡你所说的，我都必照着行。"*这里的顺服——不是对一位女强人的顺服，而是对正确之事的顺服。路得所请求的与神的旨意一致：土地留在家族中，死者的名字不被抹去，一个正直的女子不被遗弃。波阿斯认识到这一点，说：*是的。*然后他揭示了一些路得可能不知道关于自己的事：

> _"All my fellow townsmen know that you are a worthy woman."_
> 
> _"我本城的人都知道你是个贤德的女子。"_

When Ruth first arrived in Bethlehem, she was _Ruth the Moabite who came back with Naomi._ What's she doing here? She's an outsider. But over the months of the harvest, the people of Bethlehem had been watching. They saw her faithfulness to Naomi. They saw her hard work. They saw her integrity. And they talked. Boaz reveals to her, for the first time, that the town is no longer seeing her as an outsider — they are seeing her as someone good. Even though she is from Moab.

当路得第一次到达伯利恒时，她是_与拿俄米一起回来的摩押女子路得_。她在这里做什么？她是个外人。但在几个月的收割期间，伯利恒的人们一直在观察。他们看到了她对拿俄米的忠诚，看到了她的勤劳，看到了她的正直。他们谈论这些。波阿斯第一次向她揭示，这座城的人不再把她视为外人——他们视她为一个好人。尽管她来自摩押。

### The Hitch: There Is a Nearer Redeemer

### 一个障碍：有一个更近的亲属

Then comes the complication:

然后出现了一个复杂情况：

> _"Now it is true that I am a redeemer. Yet there is a redeemer nearer than I."_ — Ruth 3:12
> 
> _"我实在是你一个至近的亲属，然而还有一个人比我更近。"_——路得记3:12

If Ruth didn't already know about this other man, her heart probably sank. She had been hoping Boaz would simply say yes. But Boaz is not only righteous in what he's willing to do — he's righteous in _how_ he does it. He will not bypass the man who has the first right of redemption. He submits to the proper order even when he doesn't have to. And this submission will cost him a sleepless night of uncertainty. That is what honor actually looks like.

如果路得不知道这个人，她的心大概沉下去了。她一直希望波阿斯直接说是。但波阿斯不仅在他愿意做什么上是正直的——他在_如何_做上也是正直的。他不会绕过那个有第一救赎权的人。他顺服正确的秩序，即使他没有必要这样做。这份顺服会让他度过一个充满不确定性的不眠之夜。这才是荣誉真正的样子。

He says: _"Remain tonight, and in the morning, if he will redeem you, good; let him do it. But if he is not willing to redeem you, then, as the Lord lives, I will redeem you."_ — _As the Lord lives._ Who is the living Lord? It is Jesus. And the redeemer couples his promise with the name of the living God. _I will redeem you — like Jesus._ That is essentially what Boaz is saying.

他说："今夜你在这里住宿，明早他若肯为你尽亲属的本分，就由他吧；倘若不肯，我指着永生的耶和华起誓，我必为你尽这本分。"——*指着永生的耶和华。*谁是永生的神？就是耶稣。这位救赎者将他的承诺与永生神的名字联系在一起。*我必救赎你——如同耶稣所做的。*这基本上就是波阿斯所说的意思。

She stays at his feet until the morning — _not_ sleeping together in any romantic or physical sense; she simply remained where she was, then rose before anyone could recognize her. Before she leaves, Boaz loads her cloak with six measures of barley — somewhere between 60 and 90 pounds. Why? Two reasons. First, practical cover: anyone who saw Ruth walking back into the city would see she was carrying grain. _Oh right, Boaz has been generous to her. Nothing unusual._ Second, provision: he would not let her go back to her mother-in-law empty-handed. The same Jesus who said _"I go to prepare a place for you"_ also said _"I am the bread of life."_ Boaz sends her home with bread. Of course he does.

她在他脚边住到天亮——_不是_在任何浪漫或身体意义上同睡；她只是留在原处，然后在有人认出她之前起身离开。在她离开之前，波阿斯在她的外衣里装了六簸箕大麦——大约60到90磅。为什么？两个原因。第一，实际的掩护：任何看到路得走回城里的人都会看到她带着粮食。*哦对，波阿斯一直对她很慷慨，没什么不寻常的。*第二，供应：他不让她空手回到婆婆那里。同一个说"我去为你们预备地方"的耶稣，也说"我是生命的粮"。波阿斯送她回家时带着粮食，理所当然。

Ruth returns and tells Naomi everything. And then — remarkably — these are the **last words Ruth ever speaks** in the entire book of Ruth. Her final words are about Boaz's gracious provision: _"These six measures of barley he gave to me, for he said, 'Do not go back empty-handed to your mother-in-law.'"_ The woman the book is named after, in her last words, points entirely away from herself — to the generosity of her redeemer. As it should be. As it will be for us, too, in the end.

路得回去，把一切事告诉拿俄米。然后——令人惊叹的是——这是路得在整本路得记中说的**最后一句话**。她最后的话是关于波阿斯慷慨的供应："他又对我说，你不可空手回到你婆婆那里，就把这六簸箕大麦给了我。"这本书以她命名的女子，在她最后的话中，完全指向她自身之外——指向她的救赎者的慷慨。理应如此。最终对我们来说也将如此。

Naomi's final word of chapter 3: _"Wait, my daughter, until you learn how the matter turns out, for the man will not rest but will settle the matter today."_ She knows Boaz. She knows his reputation. If he said he'll handle it, he'll handle it. Today.

拿俄米在第三章的最后一句话："女儿啊，你只管等候，看这事怎样成就，因为那人今日不办成这事必不休息。"她了解波阿斯，了解他的声誉。如果他说他会处理，他就会处理。今天。

# Chapter 4 · 第四章

### The Gate, The Sandal, and The Genealogy of Grace

### 城门、鞋子与恩典的族谱

### The Man Who Keeps His Word

### 守信之人

Boaz made a promise at the threshing floor. He said he would settle the matter today. And he did — first thing in the morning, before anyone else was moving. He went straight to the city gate, which was the courthouse, the town square, the place of business and judicial proceedings of the ancient world, all rolled into one. And he waited.

波阿斯在禾场上许下了承诺，他说他今天就会处理这件事。他做到了——一大早，在其他人还没有行动之前。他径直去了城门口——那是古代世界的法院、广场、商业和司法裁决之地，集于一身。然后他等候。

This is important. Boaz is a picture of God. And God keeps His promises. Full stop.

这很重要。波阿斯是神的写照。神守祂的承诺，就这么简单。

The man he needed to intercept — the nearer redeemer, the one with the first right to buy back Elimelech's land — came through the gate. And here the author of Ruth does something quietly amusing. He never gives this man a name. In English translations, he's often called "friend" — but in Hebrew, the word is more literally _peloni almoni_, which means something like **Mr. So-and-So**. The dismissive equivalent of "some guy." The author of Ruth — who is meticulous about names, who understood that _names matter deeply in Scripture_ — chose to give this man no name at all. Boaz gets a name. Ruth gets a name. Even Mahlon and Chilion, the dead sons, have names. Elimelech's name must not be blotted out — that's the whole point of the plot. But this man? He doesn't even get a name. Because he didn't do what he was supposed to do. So he doesn't get one.

他需要拦截的那个人——那位更近的亲属，对以利米勒土地有第一赎买权的人——从城门走过。在这里，路得记的作者悄悄地做了一件很有趣的事。他从未给这个人一个名字。在英文翻译中，他常被称为"朋友"——但在希伯来语中，这个词更接近于 _peloni almoni_，意思大约是**某某人**。就是那种不屑一顾的"某个家伙"。路得记的作者——那位对名字极为考究、深知_名字在圣经中意义重大_的人——选择完全不给这个人任何名字。波阿斯有名字，路得有名字，就连已故的玛伦和基连也有名字。以利米勒的名字绝不可被涂抹——这正是整个故事情节的意义所在。但这个人？他甚至没有名字。因为他没有做他应该做的事，所以他得不到名字。

Jesus calls each of us by name. God puts His name on us in Holy Baptism. As Shakespeare wrote: _"What's in a name?"_ In the Bible: apparently a great deal more than we might expect.

耶稣呼叫我们每一个人的名字，神在圣洗中将祂的名字加在我们身上。正如莎士比亚所写："名字里有什么？"在圣经里：显然远比我们预期的要多。

### The Legal Proceedings: Brass Tacks at the City Gate

### 法律程序：在城门口谈正事

Boaz calls over ten elders of the city to witness the proceedings. Twelve total — Boaz, Mr. So-and-So, and ten elders — representing the twelve tribes of Israel, the number of completeness and totality in Scripture. Later: twelve apostles. The number is never accidental.

波阿斯召来城中十位长老见证这一程序。共十二人——波阿斯、某某人，和十位长老——代表以色列十二支派，圣经中完全性和整体性的数字。后来：十二使徒。这个数字从不是偶然的。

Then Boaz lays out the situation plainly: Naomi has come back from Moab and needs someone to redeem the parcel of land that belonged to Elimelech. _"If you will redeem it, redeem it. But if you will not, tell me, for there is no one besides you to redeem it, and I come after you."_

然后波阿斯直接说明情况：拿俄米从摩押回来，需要有人赎回属于以利米勒的那块地。"你若肯赎就赎，若不肯赎，告诉我，我好知道；因为除你以外没有别人有赎的权利，我在你后面。"

The unnamed redeemer immediately says: _"I will redeem it."_ Of course he will. Because here's what he's calculated: Elimelech's land is now Naomi's. Naomi is old, well past childbearing age. Her Moabite daughters-in-law have no legal standing to inherit — they weren't living in Israel, so their marriages to Israelites don't grant them Israelite status. As far as Mr. So-and-So can see, there is no heir coming. So he redeems the land, takes care of Naomi for the rest of her days, and then — with no heir to contest it — the land becomes his permanently. He gets a good piece of property and it looks entirely legal and righteous. What's not to like?

那位无名亲属立刻说："我肯赎。"当然他会这么说。因为他已经算计好了：以利米勒的地现在是拿俄米的。拿俄米年老，早已过了生育年龄。她的摩押媳妇没有法律地位可以继承——因为她们不在以色列居住，嫁给以色列人并不赋予她们以色列人的身份。依某某人的估算，不会有后嗣出现。所以他赎回这块地，照顾拿俄米余生，然后——没有继承人来争夺——土地就永久归他所有。他得到了一块好地产，看起来完全合法正当。有什么不好的呢？

Then Boaz drops the bombshell.

然后波阿斯扔下了一颗炸弹。

### A Translation Question That Changes Everything

### 一个改变一切的翻译问题

This is where most English translations create a problem — and Pastor Eric digs into the Hebrew to sort it out.

这是大多数英文翻译造成混乱的地方——Pastor Eric深入希伯来语来厘清这一点。

Most translations render Boaz's next statement as: _"The day you buy the field from the hand of Naomi, you also acquire Ruth the Moabite, the widow of the dead, in order to perpetuate the name of the dead in his inheritance."_ This makes it sound like Boaz is _surprising_ Mr. So-and-So with an obligation to marry Ruth — as if Ruth came as part of the deal, a requirement he didn't know about.

大多数翻译将波阿斯的下一句话译为："你从拿俄米手里买这地的时候，也当娶死人的妻摩押女子路得，使死人在产业上存留他的名。"这听起来像是波阿斯在_出其不意_地要求某某人娶路得——好像路得是交易的一部分，一个他不知道的要求。

But this doesn't make sense for several reasons. First, everybody in Bethlehem already knew who Ruth was. She had been gleaning in the fields for months. She was not a surprise. Second, levirate marriage — as we've established — does not apply in this situation. There are no surviving brothers. And levirate marriage never applied to foreigners anyway. There was no legal requirement for anyone to marry Ruth. If Boaz had said "you are legally required to marry her," Mr. So-and-So could have simply said: "No, I'm not. She's a Moabite. The law doesn't cover this." And everyone would have known he was right.

但这在几个方面说不通。第一，伯利恒的所有人都已经知道路得是谁，她在田间拾穗已经好几个月了，她并不是什么突然出现的人。第二，兄弟婚制度——正如我们已经确立的——在这种情况下并不适用，没有在世的兄弟，而且兄弟婚制度本来就从未适用于外邦人。没有任何法律要求任何人娶路得。如果波阿斯说"你在法律上被要求娶她"，某某人完全可以说："不，我没有。她是摩押人，律法不涵盖这种情况。"所有人都会知道他说得对。

The Hebrew verb in this verse can be read differently. The form allows for the translation: _"On the day you buy the field from the hand of Naomi, **I** will acquire Ruth the Moabite, the widow of the dead, to perpetuate the name of the dead in his inheritance."_ In other words: Boaz is not threatening Mr. So-and-So with an obligation. **He is announcing his own intent to marry Ruth.** He is saying: the moment you complete this transaction, I am going to marry Ruth and raise up an heir for Elimelech. And that heir — legally reckoned as Elimelech's descendant — will eventually be entitled to claim back the land you just paid good money for.

这节经文中的希伯来动词可以有不同的读法。这种形式允许这样翻译："你从拿俄米手里买这地的那天，**我**将娶摩押女子路得，死人的妻，使死人在产业上存留他的名。"换句话说：波阿斯不是在以义务来威胁某某人，**他是在宣告自己迎娶路得的意图。**他在说：你一完成这笔交易，我就要娶路得，并为以利米勒养育一个后嗣。那个后嗣——在法律上被视为以利米勒的后代——最终将有权主张收回你刚刚花了大价钱买的那块地。

This explains everything. The elders hear Boaz say this and they don't object — because they recognize it as righteous and godly, going above and beyond what the law requires. They're all probably looking at each other going: _"Boaz thinks like God. This man is incredible."_

这解释了一切。长老们听到波阿斯这样说，并没有反对——因为他们认出这是正直和敬虔的，超越了律法所要求的。他们大概都在彼此对视，心想：_"波阿斯的思维方式和神一样，这个人真了不起。"_

And Mr. So-and-So? His calculation has just been blown up. If Boaz marries Ruth and produces an heir, that child will be reckoned as Elimelech's son — and will eventually inherit the land he just paid to redeem. Worse, if Mr. So-and-So _himself_ had to raise up an heir by Ruth, that child would not only inherit Elimelech's land — he'd also be entitled to a share of Mr. So-and-So's own estate, diluting the inheritance of his existing children. Either way, he loses.

而某某人呢？他的算计刚刚被打乱了。如果波阿斯娶了路得并生育后嗣，那个孩子将被视为以利米勒的儿子——并最终继承他刚刚花钱赎回的那块地。更糟糕的是，如果某某人_自己_需要靠路得养育后嗣，那个孩子不仅会继承以利米勒的地——他还有权分得某某人自己的产业，稀释他现有孩子的继承份额。无论哪种方式，他都是输家。

And so Mr. So-and-So does a complete 180:

于是某某人来了个180度的大转弯：

> _"I cannot redeem it for myself, lest I impair my own inheritance. Take my right of redemption yourself, for I cannot redeem it."_
> 
> _"我不能赎，恐怕于我的产业有碍。你可以赎我所当赎的，我不能赎了。"_

The word _impair_ here — in Hebrew _shachath_ — is the same word used in Genesis 38 for what Onan did when he refused to raise up children for his deceased brother. _He ruined it._ Mr. So-and-So uses the same word to describe what would happen to his inheritance if he went through with the redemption. He chooses money over righteousness. And that is why he has no name. He is just so-and-so. He doesn't matter. The story moves on without him.

这里"有碍"这个词——希伯来语 _shachath_——与创世记38章中形容俄南拒绝为其兄弟养育后嗣时所用的词相同。*他毁掉了它。*某某人用同样的词来描述如果他继续进行救赎，他的产业会发生什么。他选择了金钱而非正义。这就是为什么他没有名字。他只是某某人，无关紧要。故事没有他照样继续。

### The Sandal: A Legal Handshake

### 那只鞋：一次法律握手

> _"Now this was the custom in former times in Israel concerning redeeming and exchanging: to confirm a transaction, the one drew off his sandal and gave it to the other, and this was the manner of attesting in Israel."_ — Ruth 4:7
> 
> _"从前，在以色列中要定夺什么事，或赎回，或交易，这人就脱鞋给那人。以色列人都以此为证据。"_——路得记4:7

Mr. So-and-So draws off his sandal and gives it to Boaz. The transaction is complete. Boaz now has the right of redemption.

某某人脱下他的鞋交给波阿斯，交易完成，波阿斯现在拥有救赎权。

Now remember: when Ruth went to the threshing floor and uncovered the feet of Boaz — what was she really doing? She was making a request for redemption through the symbolic language of feet and sandals. And now, in the city gate, the redemption is sealed with a sandal. The feet at the threshing floor and the sandal at the gate are the same story told twice. The author is extraordinarily careful. Nothing in this book is accidental.

现在回想：当路得去禾场掀开波阿斯脚上的被时——她真正在做什么？她是通过脚和鞋子的象征性语言提出一个救赎的请求。而现在，在城门口，救赎以一只鞋封印。禾场上的脚和城门口的鞋，是同一个故事讲述了两次。作者极为仔细，这本书中没有任何事情是偶然的。

There is also a connection to Moses at the burning bush: _"Take off your sandals, for the place where you are standing is holy ground."_ The promised land — the very land being discussed and transacted here — is holy ground, God's gift, His promise to Abraham. To hand over a sandal in connection with this land is freighted with all of that history.

这里还有一个与摩西在燃烧荆棘丛前的联系："当把你脚上的鞋脱下来，因为你所站之地是圣地。"应许之地——也就是这里正在讨论和交易的土地——是圣地，是神的恩赐，是祂对亚伯拉罕的承诺。在与这块土地有关的场合交出一只鞋，承载着所有这段历史的分量。

### The Public Declaration

### 公开的宣告

Boaz turns to the elders and all the people gathered and makes a formal public declaration:

波阿斯转向长老和所有聚集的人，做出正式的公开宣告：

> _"You are witnesses this day that I have bought from the hand of Naomi all that belonged to Elimelech and all that belonged to Chilion and to Mahlon. Also Ruth the Moabite, the widow of Mahlon, I have acquired to be my wife, to perpetuate the name of the dead in his inheritance, that the name of the dead may not be cut off from among his brothers and from the gate of his native place."_ — Ruth 4:9-10
> 
> _"你们今日作见证，凡属以利米勒的，和凡属基连、玛伦的，我都从拿俄米手里置买了；又娶了玛伦的妻摩押女子路得为妻，使死人在产业上存留他的名，免得死人的名在本族本地消灭。"_——路得记4:9-10

_"Acquired to be my wife"_ — some readers are alarmed by the word "acquired," as if Ruth is being purchased like property. This is legal language. It simply means: I am taking legal responsibility for her. I am becoming her representative, her advocate, her husband. Ruth is very much on board — she's the one who proposed this. And as Boaz says: _you are witnesses._ The whole community hears. The whole community ratifies.

"娶……为妻"——有些读者对"娶"这个字感到警觉，好像路得像财产一样被购买。这是法律语言，它只是意味着：我承担对她的法律责任，我成为她的代理人、她的辩护人、她的丈夫。路得非常赞同——是她提出这个请求的。而波阿斯说：*你们作见证。*整个社区都听到了，整个社区都认可了。

And notice what Boaz announces as his purpose: _"that the name of the dead may not be cut off."_ Not personal gain. Not romantic conquest. The purpose is entirely godly — that God's promise to Elimelech's family would be honored, that the inheritance would remain, that a name would live on in Israel. Boaz speaks as God's mouthpiece. That is why everyone recognizes him for what he is.

注意波阿斯宣告的目的："免得死人的名……消灭。"不是个人利益，不是浪漫的征服。目的完全是敬虔的——神对以利米勒家族的应许得以尊重，产业得以保留，一个名字在以色列中存留。波阿斯作为神的代言人说话，这就是为什么所有人都认出他是谁。

The people at the gate respond with a beautiful communal blessing:

城门处的人们以一段美丽的共同祝福回应：

> _"We are witnesses. May the Lord make the woman who is coming into your house like Rachel and Leah, who together built up the house of Israel… and may your house be like the house of Perez, whom Tamar bore to Judah."_
> 
> _"我们作见证。愿耶和华使进你家的这女子……像建立以色列家的拉结、利亚二人一样……又愿你从这少年女子所生的后裔，使你的家像法勒斯的家……"_

In this blessing, Ruth is adopted by the entire community of Bethlehem. She is no longer the Moabite outsider. She is like Rachel and Leah — the mothers of Israel. And the reference to Perez is our cue to go somewhere that requires a maturity warning.

在这个祝福中，路得被伯利恒的整个社区所收养。她不再是外来的摩押人，她像拉结和利亚——以色列的母亲们。而提到法勒斯，是引导我们去一个需要成熟内容警示的地方。

### Judah and Tamar: The TVMA Background You Need

### 犹大与他玛：你需要知道的成熟背景

Pastor Eric issues his TVMA warning here. If you have small children in the room who are going to ask about words, now is the time to make other arrangements.

Pastor Eric在这里发出成熟内容警示。如果房间里有会追问词义的小孩子，现在是时候做出其他安排了。

The reference to Perez takes us to Genesis 38 — the story of Judah and Tamar. And this story matters because Judah is the tribe of Jesus, and Perez is directly in the genealogy of Boaz and therefore of David and therefore of Jesus.

提到法勒斯引我们去创世记38章——犹大和他玛的故事。这个故事很重要，因为犹大是耶稣的支派，法勒斯直接在波阿斯乃至大卫乃至耶稣的族谱中。

Here is what happened. Judah has three sons: Er, Onan, and Shelah. Er marries a woman named Tamar — but Er is so wicked that God puts him to death. So Judah tells his second son Onan: _"Go into your brother's wife and perform the duty of a brother-in-law to her."_ This is levirate marriage, exactly as designed. The child would be reckoned as Er's son and heir.

事情是这样的。犹大有三个儿子：珥、俄南和示拉。珥娶了一个名叫他玛的女子——但珥极其邪恶，神让他死了。于是犹大告诉他的次子俄南："你当与你哥哥的妻子同房，向她尽你为弟兄的本分。"这正是兄弟婚制度，按照设计运作。那个孩子将被视为珥的儿子和继承人。

But Onan refused to do it properly. He knew any child born would not be legally his — so he wasted his seed on the ground rather than give Tamar an heir. This was wickedness in the sight of the Lord, and God put him to death as well.

但俄南拒绝正确地履行这一义务。他知道任何出生的孩子在法律上都不会是他的——所以他把精子倾泻在地上，而不是给他玛一个后嗣。这在神眼中是邪恶的，神也让他死了。

Judah then tells Tamar to wait for his third son Shelah to grow up. But when Shelah grew up, Judah didn't follow through — probably afraid God would kill this son too. Time passes. Judah's own wife dies. He goes off to his sheepshearers with his friend Hirah.

犹大然后告诉他玛等他的第三个儿子示拉长大。但示拉长大后，犹大没有履行承诺——可能是害怕神也会杀死这个儿子。时光流逝，犹大自己的妻子去世。他和朋友希拉去他剪羊毛的地方。

Tamar hears about this. She realizes Judah has broken his word — Shelah is grown and she has not been given to him. So she takes drastic action: she removes her widow's garments, covers herself with a veil, and sits at the roadside where Judah will pass. She poses as a prostitute. Pastor Eric: _"Two wrongs don't make a right, people."_

他玛听说了这件事。她意识到犹大没有守信——示拉长大了，她却没有嫁给他。于是她采取了极端行动：她脱下寡妇的衣服，用面纱遮住自己，坐在犹大必经之路旁。她装扮成一个妓女。Pastor Eric说：_"两个错误不能变成一个对的，弟兄姐妹们。"_

Judah doesn't recognize her — he thinks she's a cult prostitute, which makes it worse — and he sleeps with her. She asks for his signet, cord, and staff as a pledge until he can send payment. He agrees. She conceives.

犹大没有认出她——他以为她是一个庙妓，这让情况更糟——然后他与她同寝。她要他的印、带子和杖作为抵押，等他送来酬谢。他同意了。她怀孕了。

Three months later, someone tells Judah: your daughter-in-law Tamar is pregnant, she's been immoral. Judah, playing the role of the righteous father-in-law, immediately declares: _"Bring her out and let her be burned."_ Not even stoned — burned. As she is being led out, she sends him his signet, cord, and staff: _"By the man to whom these belong, I am pregnant."_

三个月后，有人告诉犹大：你的媳妇他玛不检点，她怀孕了。犹大扮演正直岳父的角色，立刻宣布："拉出去，把她烧了。"不是用石头打死——是烧死。就在她被押出去的时候，她派人送去他的印、带子和杖："那使我怀孕的人就是这些东西的主人。"

And here — finally — Judah's faith surfaces. He says: _"She is more righteous than I, since I did not give her to my son Shelah."_ He confesses. He repents. He changes. Whatever you think of everything that just happened, Judah ultimately does the right thing — he owns his sin completely.

就在这里——终于——犹大的信仰浮现出来。他说："她比我更有义，因为我没有将她给我的儿子示拉。"他认罪，悔改，改变。无论你对刚才发生的一切有何看法，犹大最终做了正确的事——他完全承担了自己的罪。

Tamar gives birth to twins. There's even a scuffle about which one comes out first — one puts out his hand, the midwife ties a scarlet thread on it, then he draws the hand back and his brother emerges first. The one who came out is named **Perez**, meaning "breach." And Perez is in the direct genealogy of Boaz, David, and Jesus.

他玛生了双胞胎。甚至还有一番关于谁先出来的波折——一个先伸出手，助产士在上面绑了一根朱红线，然后他把手缩了回去，他的兄弟先出来了。先出来的被命名为**法勒斯**，意思是"闯出来"。而法勒斯正在波阿斯、大卫和耶稣的直系族谱中。

So: Ruth is a Moabite, descended from the incestuous union of Lot and his daughter. And Boaz is descended from Perez, the product of Judah sleeping with his daughter-in-law while she was disguised as a prostitute. And the people of Bethlehem bless Ruth by comparing her to these ancestors — because they all know the stories. And they know that God works through all of it. God doesn't cause these sins. He's not up in heaven saying "go sleep with your daughter-in-law." He is up there going: _"Don't do this. Don't do this. Don't do this. …Yep. There you go. Okay. Well, I've ordered everything so it still works out in the end. Here we go."_

所以：路得是摩押人，源自罗得与他女儿乱伦的结合。而波阿斯源自法勒斯——犹大与装扮成妓女的媳妇同寝所生的孩子。伯利恒的人们以这些祖先来祝福路得——因为他们都知道这些故事，也知道神借着这一切工作。神不是这些罪的原因。祂不是在天上说"去和你的媳妇同寝"。祂是在上面说：_"不要这样做，不要这样做，不要这样做……好吧，就这样了。好的，我已经把一切都安排好了，最终还是会有好结果的，走着瞧吧。"_

That is the God of grace. He fills His son's genealogy not with perfectly righteous people, but with sinners — Tamar, Perez, Rahab the prostitute (who was actually Boaz's mother, by the way — so the apple doesn't fall far from the tree in terms of scandalous ancestry), Ruth the Moabite, and on down to David who committed adultery and murder. God fills His family line with the broken and the despised. And that should comfort every single one of us. Because it means sinners have a place in God's redemption.

这就是恩典之神。祂没有用完全正直的人来填充祂儿子的族谱，而是用罪人——他玛、法勒斯、妓女喇合（顺便说一句，喇合实际上是波阿斯的母亲——所以从丑闻祖先的角度来看，苹果没有离树太远）、摩押女子路得，一直到犯了奸淫和谋杀的大卫。神用破碎的人和被轻视的人来填充祂的家族谱系。这应该安慰我们每一个人，因为这意味着罪人在神的救赎中有一席之地。

---

### The Birth of Obed: Salvation History Turns on This Moment

### 俄备得的诞生：救恩历史在此转折

> _"So Boaz took Ruth, and she became his wife. And he went in to her, and the Lord gave her conception, and she bore a son."_ — Ruth 4:13
> 
> _"于是波阿斯娶了路得为妻，与她同房。耶和华使她怀孕，她就生了一个儿子。"_——路得记4:13

Notice: it is the Lord who gives her conception. The whole book of Ruth has unfolded under God's quiet providence — the famine, the return, the field that Ruth "happened" to end up in, the kindness of Boaz, the legal proceedings. God was working through all of it without ever appearing directly. And now He gives the child. Exactly what Mr. So-and-So feared — and rightly so.

注意：是耶和华使她怀孕。整本路得记在神安静的护理下展开——饥荒、归回、路得"碰巧"到达的那块田、波阿斯的善待、法律程序。神在这一切中工作，却从未直接显现。现在祂赐下孩子，正是某某人所担心的——而且是有道理的。

The women of Bethlehem — who at the beginning of chapter 1 could barely recognize Naomi — now crowd around her with a blessing:

伯利恒的妇女们——在第一章开始时几乎认不出拿俄米——现在聚集在她周围，带着祝福：

> _"Blessed be the Lord, who has not left you this day without a redeemer, and may his name be renowned in Israel! He shall be to you a restorer of life and a nourisher of your old age, for your daughter-in-law who loves you, who is more to you than seven sons, has given birth to him."_
> 
> _"耶和华是应当称颂的！因为他今日没有撇下你，使你不断后。愿这孩子在以色列中得名声。他必提你的精神，奉养你的老，因为是爱慕你的那儿媳所生的。有这儿媳比有七个儿子还好。"_

_"More to you than seven sons."_ Seven — the number of completion. They are saying: Ruth is worth more than the most complete family you could have imagined. She — the Moabite, the foreigner, the one God swore would never enter his assembly — has become more precious than all of that.

*"比有七个儿子还好。"*七——完全的数字。她们在说：路得比你能想象到的最完整的家庭还要珍贵。她——那位摩押人，那位外邦人，那位神宣告永不得入祂会的人——已经变得比这一切都更宝贵。

Naomi takes the child and holds him in her lap, becoming his nurse. _"A son has been born to Naomi."_ The community reckons this child as Naomi's — as Elimelech's descendant — exactly as the law intended. The name they give him is **Obed**, meaning _servant_. He will serve this family as their redeemer's child. And Obed becomes the father of Jesse, and Jesse becomes the father of **David the King**.

拿俄米抱起孩子，放在自己怀中，作了他的养母。"拿俄米得了一个儿子。"社区将这个孩子认作拿俄米的孩子——作为以利米勒的后裔——正如律法所意图的那样。他们给他起名叫**俄备得**，意思是_仆人_。他将作为救赎者的孩子服事这个家族。而俄备得成了耶西的父亲，耶西成了**大卫王**的父亲。

### The Genealogy: God Fills His Family Line With Sinners

### 族谱：神用罪人填充祂的家族谱系

The book closes with a genealogy from Perez to David — ten generations, the number of completion:

这本书以从法勒斯到大卫的族谱结束——十代，完全的数字：

_Perez → Hezron → Ram → Amminadab → Nahshon → Salmon → Boaz → Obed → Jesse → David_

_法勒斯→希斯仑→兰→亚米拿达→拿顺→撒门→波阿斯→俄备得→耶西→大卫_

A few notes on this genealogy that Pastor Eric finds irresistible:

关于这份族谱，有几点Pastor Eric觉得忍不住要说：

**Nahshon** appears in the fifth position. In Hebrew genealogies of twelve generations, the seventh position was a position of honor — like God's perfect number. But the fifth position also carried significance, representing — as we've seen throughout Ruth — the five wounds of Christ, commitment, and bravery. And Nahshon certainly earned it: according to rabbinic oral tradition, when Israel stood at the shore of the Red Sea with the Egyptians bearing down on them and Moses still praying, Nahshon — the moment he heard God say _"Go forward"_ — started walking into the sea. He got up to his **neck** in water before Moses finally stretched out his staff and the waters parted. He was so certain God would keep His word that he expected to either breathe underwater or that something would happen. Maybe it's oral tradition, maybe it's a great-great-grandchild's embellishment. But it's a wonderful story.

**拿顺**出现在第五个位置。在十二代的希伯来族谱中，第七个位置是荣誉位置——就像神的完全数字。但第五个位置也有意义，代表——正如我们在路得记中一路所见——基督的五处伤痕、承诺和勇气。拿顺当之无愧：根据拉比的口述传统，当以色列站在红海岸边、埃及人从后面追来、摩西还在祷告的时候，拿顺——在他听到神说"前进"的那一刻——就开始走进海里。他一直走到水深及**颈**，摩西才终于伸出杖，海水分开。他如此确信神会守祂的话，以至于他期待要么能在水下呼吸，要么会发生什么事情。也许这是口述传统，也许是曾孙子辈的夸大。但这是一个美妙的故事。

**Boaz** falls in the seventh position — the position of honor. Of course he does.

**波阿斯**处于第七个位置——荣誉位置。当然如此。

**Salmon** — Boaz's father — married **Rahab the prostitute**. You might know Rahab from the book of Joshua: she hid the Israelite spies in Jericho and was saved when the city fell. Rahab. A Canaanite prostitute. Boaz's mother. So Boaz's mother was a Gentile woman with a scandalous past, and his wife was a Moabite woman. Jesus, looking down this family line, apparently had a type — and that type was: _"the ones nobody else wanted."_

**撒门**——波阿斯的父亲——娶了**妓女喇合**。你可能从约书亚记中认识喇合：她藏匿了以色列探子，城市陷落时她得救了。喇合。一个迦南妓女，波阿斯的母亲。所以波阿斯的母亲是一个有着丑闻过去的外邦女子，而他的妻子是一个摩押女子。耶稣俯视这条家族谱系，显然有一种偏好——那种偏好就是：_"没有人要的那些人。"_

Jesus could have populated His genealogy with exclusively righteous, accomplished, admirable people. He didn't. He chose Tamar. He chose Perez. He chose Rahab. He chose Ruth. He chose David — who would later commit adultery and murder. God fills His son's family line with sinners. Which means sinners have always been welcome in God's family. Which means you are welcome.

耶稣本可以只用正直、有成就、令人钦佩的人来填充祂的族谱，但祂没有。祂选择了他玛，选择了法勒斯，选择了喇合，选择了路得，选择了大卫——那位后来犯了奸淫和谋杀的人。神用罪人填充祂儿子的家族谱系，这意味着罪人一直都受欢迎在神的家里，这意味着你也受欢迎。

And without Ruth — Ruth the Moabite, the one God declared would never enter His assembly — you never get to David. And without David, you never get to Jesus.

没有路得——神宣告永不得入祂会的摩押女子路得——就没有大卫。没有大卫，就没有耶稣。

The entire history of salvation **hinges on Ruth the Moabite.**

整个救恩历史**系于摩押女子路得。**

Far from being pushed to the margins, women stand at the very center of God's story. Ruth and Mary — both of whom God used to bring His Son into the world — are the hinge points of all history. The book of Ruth is named after her. And it ends with a genealogy that places her in an unbroken line running straight to the King of Kings.

女性非但没有被推到边缘，反而站在神故事的中心。路得和马利亚——神都藉着她们将祂的儿子带入世界——是所有历史的转折点。这本书以她命名，并以一份族谱结束，将她置于一条直通万王之王的不间断谱系之中。

### Remember Points for Chapter 4

### 第四章记住要点

> **God keeps His promises — and so does Boaz. 神守祂的承诺——波阿斯也如此。** 你所依靠的救赎者绝不食言。

> **Mr. So-and-So chose money over righteousness — and lost his name. 某某人选择了金钱而非正义——他失去了自己的名字。** 不做神所要求的事，就是把自己从祂的故事里抹去。

> **God fills His son's family line with sinners. 神用罪人填充祂儿子的家族谱系。** 他玛、喇合、路得、大卫——这不是事故，这是恩典。

> **The entire history of salvation hinges on Ruth the Moabite. 整个救恩历史系于摩押女子路得。** 神永远弃绝了摩押人。然后祂让一个摩押女子成为耶稣的祖先。这就是神的怜悯，永远胜过律法。

## Final Summary · 全书总结

The book of Ruth is four chapters long. It contains no miracles, no divine appearances, no dramatic signs. God works entirely through ordinary people making extraordinary choices — Naomi sharing her faith through a lifetime of family, Ruth clinging to a God she barely knew, Boaz going above and beyond the letter of the law at every turn.

路得记只有四章。它没有神迹，没有神的显现，没有戏剧性的异兆。神完全通过普通人做出非凡选择来工作——拿俄米用一生的家庭生活分享她的信仰，路得紧跟一位她刚刚认识的神，波阿斯在每一个转折点都超越律法的文字而行。

And underneath all of it — beneath the famine and the grief and the harvest and the threshing floor and the sandal and the gate — God is quietly, patiently, providentially arranging the birth of His own Son.

在这一切之下——在饥荒、悲痛、收割、禾场、鞋子和城门之下——神安静地、耐心地、按祂的护理安排着祂自己儿子的诞生。

Ruth = Us. Boaz = Jesus. The redeemer came — not because we deserved it, not because the law required it, not because He owed us anything. He came because His mercy is greater than His judgment, because His love gets the better of Him, because He looked at the lowest of the low — the Moabites of this world — and said:

路得 ＝ 我们。波阿斯 ＝ 耶稣。救赎者来了——不是因为我们值得，不是因为律法要求，不是因为祂亏欠我们什么。祂来是因为祂的怜悯大过祂的审判，因为祂的爱胜过了一切，因为祂俯视那最卑微的人——这世界上的摩押人——说：

_"Do not fear. I will do for you all that you ask."_

_"不要惧怕，凡你所说的，我都必照着行。"_

**Soli Deo Gloria. 愿荣耀单归神。**
