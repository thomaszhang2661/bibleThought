3:1

‫וְ הַ נָחָ ש הָ יָה עָרּום מִ כֹל חַ יַת הַ שָ דֶ ה אֲשֶ ר עָשָ ה יְ הוָה אֱֹלהִ ים‬1
1a

Now the serpent was more crafty than any beast of the field which the Lord God had made.
1a 现在说到那条蛇，在耶和华神所造的一切田间野兽之中，属它最狡猾。
“Now the serpent …” Again this is not the normal order of a Hebrew sentence. The subject is
in the emphatic position instead of the verb. The serpent is thereby thrust before our eyes. We are
definitely dealing, first of all, with a serpent, a snake. It is a beast of the field, one of the creatures
of God, for with those creatures it is compared. Nothing is said, however, about its kind or its size.
If we do not grant that we are dealing with a serpent, if we assume that it is only a symbol, then we
cannot be sure of the historicity of anything else in the chapter. Then other things could just as well
be symbols.
“现在说到那条蛇......”，我们在这里再次看到了这种并不常见的希伯来句式，即主语位
于句首起强调作用，而非动词。如此，那条蛇就猛然出现在我们眼前。首先，我们所面对的
当然是一条蛇。它是一种田间野兽，神的造物之一，因摩西将它与其它受造作了比较。然而，
经文并未谈到它的种类以及尺寸。如果我们认为我们所面对的并非一条蛇，而仅仅是一种象
征，那么我们就无法确认该章节其它任何内容的历史真实性了。如此一来，圣经其它内容可
能也都不过是些象征而已。
Moses asserts that this serpent was ‫עָרּום‬, crafty, subtle, cunning. It was more subtle and crafty
than all the beasts of the field. Since this craftiness and cunning is not described, not explained, we
must conclude that we are directed to perceive the craftiness from what the serpent does. It speaks.
The words which it speaks are spoken to tempt Eve, to draw her into moral evil.
摩西坚称这是一条‫（עָרּום‬狡猾、诡诈、奸刁）的蛇，且比田间任何野兽都要诡诈与狡
猾。由于摩西并未对这种狡猾与奸刁作任何解释，因此我们必须得出以下结论：摩西引导我
们根据这条蛇的行为来理解这里“狡猾”一词的含义。它说话，并且它所说的话是为试探夏
娃，好将她拖入道德之恶的泥潭之中。
This kind of craftiness is something that could belong only to a rational creature who has moral
responsibility. No mere snake, no beast of the field, all of which are amoral, could display the
craftiness and cunning displayed in the serpent’s discourse with Eve. The word crafty is, therefore,
the first hint that we are dealing with more than a snake. Whatever cunning the serpent possessed
as its natural characteristic had become the vehicle of a craftiness which only a rational being with
moral responsibility could possess.
这种狡猾只可能属于某种既有理性又必须承担道德责任的受造物。普通的蛇类以及田间
的任何野兽都不具备道德观念，它们也就无法展现出这条蛇与夏娃对话中所展现出的这种狡
猾与奸刁。因此，这里的狡猾一词就是第一处暗示——暗示我们所面对的不仅仅是一条蛇。
无论这条蛇原本具有怎样狡猾的特征，这些狡猾都已成了“让一种既有理性又必须承担道德
责任的存在施展其狡猾的”工具。
The text tells us this serpent spoke. Yet serpents do not speak. Adam, who had named the
animals which the Lord God had brought to him in a discerning and meaningful manner, knew this.
Eve, who possessed the same perfect faculties, must also have realized that, though dealing with a
serpent, she was at the same time dealing with more than a serpent. More we cannot say. By what
the serpent said—denying God’s Word, questioning God’s goodness—the actual tempter speaking
through the serpent revealed himself as an enemy of God. Still Moses is content throughout the
account of the fall to speak of this rational, morally accountable but wicked tempter who approached
Eve in terms of the serpent, the beast that the tempter used as his vehicle.
文本告诉我们，这条蛇开口说话了。然而，蛇并不会说话。耶和华神以一种巧妙又有意
义的方式将那些动物带到了亚当面前，亚当已对它们一一命名，因此亚当深知这一点。夏娃，
与亚当拥有同样完美能力的她，也必早已意识到了这一点：尽管她面对的是一条蛇，但与此
同时，她所面对的却不仅仅是一条蛇。除此之外，我们也就无法再多说些什么了。通过这条
蛇所说的话——否认神的话语并质疑神的良善，背后那位藉这条蛇说话的试探者将牠自己显
露为了神的仇敌。不过，纵观人类堕落这段记载，摩西始终满足于将牠——这位理性、必须
承担道德责任却又邪恶的、接近夏娃的试探者——称为蛇，也就是这位试探者用作工具的那
野兽。
God has not been pleased in his Word to reveal everything in detail at once or at one and the
same time. God’s revelation is progressive. This is not true, however, in the sense that God’s later
revelations serve to correct or even to set aside earlier imperfect revelations. Such progressive
revelations, though frequently and erroneously asserted, must be denied. God’s revelation is
progressive only in the sense that as time passed on, God’s revelation supplied more and more details,
unfolded and clarified earlier revelations, but never corrected or contradicted what had been said
earlier. The expressions and statements in the earlier revelations are always of such a nature that
they are fully adequate to include the additional truths and the additional details of information
furnished by subsequent revelations.
上帝并不乐意在他的话语中一次性或于同一时间将所有信息都启示清楚，他乐意逐步显
明他的信息。然而，这并不意味着上帝稍后的启示是要修正早先那些“不完美的启示”，或
者甚至是要将后者搁置一旁。尽管人们常常错误地宣讲后一种“逐步”启示，但我们必须拒
绝这种观点。上帝的逐步启示仅仅在于：随着时间的流逝，上帝的启示会补足更多的细节，
揭示并澄清早前的那些启示，但却绝非纠正后者或与后者自相矛盾。早先的启示所表明的内
容总是具有如下性质：它们完全能够将那些由稍后的启示所提供的附加真理与附加细节囊括
进去。
God’s New Testament revelation still speaks of the serpent. St. Paul writes: “But I am afraid
that just as Eve was deceived by the serpent’s cunning, your minds may somehow be led astray from
your sincere and pure devotion to Christ” (2 Co 11:3). Yet in unfolding this very admonition, St.
Paul makes it quite evident who, through the serpent, tempted Eve. He indicates it as he adds the
warning: “And no wonder, for Satan himself masquerades as an angel of light” (2 Co 11:14). In
Revelation 12:9 John also speaks of both the serpent and of Satan in connection with man’s fall into
sin, saying: “The great dragon was hurled down—that ancient serpent called the devil, or Satan,
who leads the whole world astray.” In Revelation 20:2 John speaks once more of “that ancient
serpent, who is the devil, or Satan.”

神在新约的启示中也谈到了这条蛇。圣保罗如此写到：“我只怕你们的心或偏于邪，失
去那向基督所存纯一清洁的心，就像那条蛇用诡诈诱惑了夏娃一样”（林后 11:3）。然而，
当圣保罗随即揭示这一警告时，他便挑明了藉这条蛇引诱夏娃的那位究竟是谁。当他继续发
出警告时，他这样说到：“这也不足为怪。因为连撒但也装作光明的天使”（林后 11:14）。
在启示录 12:9 中，当约翰论到人类的犯罪堕落之时，他也同时谈到了这条蛇与撒旦：“这
条大龙就是那条古蛇，名叫魔鬼，又叫撒但，是迷惑普天下的。它被摔在地上。”在启示录
20:2 中，约翰再次论及“那条古蛇，又叫魔鬼，也叫撒但”。
Jesus identifies the tempter who made up the basic lie that both God and his will are not good,
the tempter who through this lie brought death upon mankind. Jesus says to the unbelieving Jews:
“You belong to your father, the devil, and you want to carry out your father’s desire. He was a
murderer (ἀνθρωποκτόνος) from the beginning, not holding to the truth, for there is no truth in him.
When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he is a liar and the father of lies” (Jn 8:44).
耶稣也同样指出了这位曾谎称上帝及其旨意并非良善的试探者，这位曾藉这份谎言将死
亡带给人类的试探者。耶稣对那些不信的犹太人如此说到：“你们是出于你们的父魔鬼，你
们父的私欲，你们偏要行，他从起初是杀人的（ἀνθρωποκτόνος），不守真理。因他心里没有
真理，他说谎是出于自己，因他本来是说谎的，也是说谎之人的父”（约 8:44）。
The fatal temptation came to Eve through a serpent and, by means of the serpent, through Satan.
Let us look at the tempter’s first question.
藉着这条蛇，藉着撒旦所使用的这条蛇，这致命的诱惑临到了夏娃。让我们一起来看看
那位试探者的第一问吧。

‫וַ י ֹאמֶ ר אֶ ל־הָ אִ שָ ה אַ ף כִ י־אָ מַ ר אֱֹלהִ ים ל ֹא ת ֹאכְ לּו מִ כֹ ל עֵץ הַ גָן‬
1bAnd he said to the woman, “Did God really say, ‘You shall not eat of all the trees of the

garden’?”
1b 牠对那女人说，“神岂是真说‘你们不可吃园中所有树上的果子’吗？”
From the woman’s answer, it is evident that the serpent’s statement is a question, even though
it is not introduced by an interrogative particle. With this question the tempter implies that God’s
commandment is a burdensome restriction. He knows and wants to know of no other purpose for a
commandment. He knows nothing of a commandment as a blessed and welcome opportunity for
man to show his love and thankfulness to his divine benefactor. He knows nothing of a
commandment as a blessed opportunity for man to exercise his heartfelt obedience and, thereby, to
become confirmed in such obedience. Satan refers to God only as ‫ ;אֱֹלהִ ים‬he does not
acknowledge him as the Lord, as the God of free and faithful love. For Satan it is an either/or. Either
God has not said this, has not given this prohibition, or he isn’t really good, isn’t really concerned
about man’s happiness, about man’s welfare and bliss. This is Satan’s ultimate lie. To give way to it
would be the opposite of humble, joyful trust.
尽管这条蛇并未使用什么疑问词，但从这女人的回答中，我们明显可以看出牠是在向她
提出问题。这位试探者所提之问的弦外之音便是：神的命令是一幅沉重的枷锁。牠既不了解，
也无意去了解神的命令所要成就的其它任何目的。牠完全不了解神的命令就是一次让人蒙福
的良机，藉此，人得以向他那位神圣的赐恩者表达爱与感恩。牠完全不了解神的命令也是另
一种让人蒙福的良机，藉此，人得以展现他由衷的顺从，并被固定在这种顺从之中。撒旦仅
以“‫”אֱֹלהִ ים‬称呼上帝；牠并不承认他是那位白白施爱又忠于其爱的上帝。对撒旦而言，这

只是一个非此即彼的问题。要么上帝未曾这样说过，未曾下达过这样的禁令；要么上帝并非
真正良善，对人类的福祉和快乐毫不关心。这就是撒旦的终极谎言。向这谎言屈服将会与谦
卑喜乐的信靠背道而驰。
Either alternative that Satan offers would be fatal. The tempter’s main thrust in this first
temptation is still that of trying to make God’s Word uncertain for Eve. If he succeeded in this, he
could readily induce Eve to transgress God’s will. Satan still begins his temptations in this way. And,
while making this initial thrust, Satan is at the same time already seeking to sow suspicion
concerning God’s goodness.
撒旦所给出的两个选项都将是致命的。这位试探者第一次试探的重点不过是要让夏娃怀
疑上帝的话语。一旦达成了这一步，接下来牠就要立刻诱惑夏娃违背神的旨意了。如今，撒
旦仍在使用这种伎俩向人类抛出他的诱饵。并且，一旦说完他的初始要点，此时撒旦就会立
刻琢磨如何让人怀疑上帝的良善。
Contemporary form critical interpretation, of course, opposes taking the serpent and his
speaking literally. It shows even less understanding for seeing Satan as the real agent. It asks: Why
should we be disturbed about a serpent speaking? We expect that sort of thing in religious parables
and in didactic fables and think nothing of it. This is in itself correct. Yet Genesis 3 does not bear
the marks of a religious parable or of a didactic fable. This is evident as we consider both the larger
and the closer context of this third chapter in the book of Genesis with its carefully structured ten

‫ ּתֹולְ דֹות‬and preceding introduction. It is apparent also in the account of this chapter itself. A
didactic fable has a clearly discernible, or even expressly stated, moral attached to it. None is
attached to this account. Instead, man’s fatal action in this account has lasting consequences for all
mankind. This is not true of actions in parables and fables.
当然，当代形式批判诠释法并不会将这条蛇与牠所说的话当作字面义理解，并且，对于
将撒旦视为真正的幕后主使这一观点，该诠释法更是嗤之以鼻，它这样问到：“我们为何要
对一条蛇开口说话这件事揪着不放呢？这种事在宗教寓言和训诲故事中不是比比皆是吗？
当我们阅读那些东西的时候，我们又何曾自取烦恼呢？”若是抽离语境，这样的说法本身并
无大碍。但是创世记第 3 章却没有什么迹象表明它是宗教寓言或训诲故事。当我们想到“创
世记以十段‫ּתֹולְ דֹות‬精心铺设的结构加之引文”这更大的语境以及其中第 3 章这近处的语境
时，这一点就更加显而易见了。并且，这一点在这一章的记载中也同样明确。训诲故事有其
清晰可辨的、甚至是清晰阐明的寓意，但这份记载却没有这些痕迹。相反，在这份记载中，
人的致命行为给全人类带来了持续性的后果。寓言或故事中的行为却并非如此。
Contemporary form critical interpretations take the serpent and what is expressed in the
serpent’s question to symbolize the evil thoughts and impulses which arise in the human heart
concerning God and his will. In ancient traditions of the East, the serpent was the symbol of the sea,
and the sea was thought of as being in rebellion against the Creator. Hence the serpent became the
symbol of the forces of wickedness.1 But why, then, is such a strong curse spoken upon the serpent?

Others are willing to acknowledge that a temptation took place, a temptation to which Eve as a
real individual was exposed. And yet they want the serpent to be a symbol of the woman’s inner
reasoning with temptation. Yet how did Eve, who was created without sin, come to have evil
thoughts if they were not first addressed to her from the outside through an agent of evil?
Contemporary interpretation, which does not want to stay with what the text says, only raises
additional questions in its futile attempt to explain matters to the satisfaction of human reason.
另有一些人则愿意承认当时却有试探临到了夏娃这个真实的个体，但他们同时又觉得这

Eve did not immediately give up her faith. She fought for it. She was discerning. She understood
the main thrust of the serpent’s question, that he was calling the certainty of God’s commandment
into question. She also caught the insinuation that the commandment, if it had actually been given,
was unduly restrictive and burdensome. She knew that God’s goodness was also being called into

Many commentators contend that Eve changed God’s Word, that she added to it in saying, “…
and you shall not touch it.” Yet it is not out of place to observe the Eighth Commandment even in
interpretation, i.e., to put the best construction on things where this is possible. The tempter did not
pounce on this addition, did not hook into it with his next tempting remark. Hence we can understand
her addition as a legitimate unfolding of God’s commandment. The woman could well have meant
to say: As far as this tree is concerned, eating for food does not at all come into consideration. In all
probability Eve was referring to the kind of touching which would result in taking possession of the
fruit as a first step in eating of it and thus as a first step in disobeying God’s commandment. In
Genesis 20:6, the Hebrew language uses “touch” in this sense of acquiring something for personal
use. Eve wanted to say: God simply has told us not to have anything to do with this tree as far as
food is concerned. It is a matter of ready obedience, an opportunity to show the joyful obedience of
our hearts to our God. Up to this point we can therefore understand the woman’s reply as simple
testimony. There is nothing wrong about elaborating God’s Word as long as we stay within its intent

and meaning.

In her reply the woman also added, “‫פֶן־ּתְ מֻתּון‬, lest you die.” She now stressed the reason for
God’s prohibition. This too can be understood correctly. God did want to keep his children from

“For in the day of your eating of it you will surely die” (Ge 2:17). It was a loving warning which
was to give them occasion to shun this existing evil by a free choice, one prompted by their trust
and faith-born thankful love toward God. They could be expected gladly to avoid what their loving
God pointed out to them as dire evil. In this way God’s children were to become confirmed in their
abhorrence and rejection of evil.

一来，神的儿女就能被固定在憎恶与拒绝邪恶的状态之中。
We cannot establish whether the woman meant it in this proper way. What we can note is that
“lest you die” could easily be twisted into a wrong thought, as though the evil result of disobedience

flesh with which to contend. As she fell into argumentation, she gave Satan an opening with his
temptations. At least it is at this point that we find that the tempter began his next assault. We are
reminded that we are to proclaim God’s Word. We are not asked to prove it or to justify it. As
believers we should always be ready to give a reason for the hope that we have. But we are not to
enter into dialog with errorists with the thought of mutually enriching one another by such
discussion.

name, ‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬, to be an actual plural, “gods.” Satan asserts that God does not want man to be like
him, knowing good and evil.
钦定本将上帝名字的复数形式（‫）אֱֹלהִ ים‬视为真实的复数形式，故译为“像诸神一样”。
但“像神一样”这一表述却更为贴切，撒旦是在断言，上帝并不想让人类像他一样知晓善恶。
Note the attractive suggestiveness. What this knowing of good and evil is and what advantage
it entails, Satan does not say. The thing charms by its vagueness. It is all the more deceptive because
God had actually called the forbidden tree “the tree of the knowing of good and evil.” The devil’s
beguilements, as always, are an inextricable tangle of seeming half-truths and falsehoods. For that
reason they are all the more dangerous and insidious.
请注意这种暗示所具备的诱惑力。知晓善恶究竟是怎么一回事呢？这种认知又会带来怎
样的益处呢？撒旦并未提及。此时之所以具有吸引力，是因为其表述含糊不清。而正因为上
帝确实曾将那棵禁树称为“知晓善恶的树”，撒旦的话就更具欺骗性了。一如既往，魔鬼的
哄骗总是由那些看似半真半假的内容死死地纠缠在一起。正因如此，它们才更具危险性，更
加阴险毒辣。
In the exposition of Genesis 2:17 we noted that the tree of the knowing of good and evil was
actually to impart a godlike knowledge of good and evil to man. By consciously obeying God’s
command in childlike trust, by wholeheartedly entering upon this obedience with mind, heart, and
will, man would have gained an experiential knowledge of good similar to that of God. He would
have become confirmed in devotion to that which was good. On the other hand, by being made
aware of evil and still rejecting it with mind, heart, and will in humble faith and thankful love, he
would have become confirmed in a godlike aversion for all that is evil. The knowledge of good and
evil, however, which Satan promised here on the path of disobedience and which he left undescribed,
turned out to be something quite different. It became a knowledge of good as something that Adam
and Eve had lost and a knowledge of evil as something they now experienced in all of its cursed
reality.
从创世记 2:17 的注释中我们注意到，事实上，知晓善恶之树是要赋予人类如神一般知晓
善恶的知识。以孩童般的信靠有意识地遵行上帝的命令，在智力、情感以及意志上全心如此
顺服，人类本可获得如神一般知晓良善的经验型知识，他本可以被固定在那仅专注于良善之
事的状态之中。另一方面，虽被赋予关乎邪恶的知识，却仍在智力、情感及意志上以谦卑的
信心和感恩的爱拒绝邪恶，藉此，他也本可以被固定在如神一般憎恶所有恶事的状态之中。
然而，撒旦在此所应许的知晓善恶却驰骋在一条悖逆的道路之上，其描述含糊不清，最后的
结果实在是南辕北辙。之后，亚当和夏娃失去了那知晓良善的知识，却亲身经历了邪恶的知
识，体验了它全然受咒诅的真实样貌。
Here we have the basic lie which Satan brought into the world (Jn 8:44), the lie that underlies
all sin and all the delusions of sin. It is the lie that God and his will are not really good, that man’s
welfare and happiness do not lie in clinging to God in humble, joyful trust and in doing his will in
thankful love. It is the lie that a person can find happiness by striking out on his own, by pitting his
own will and judgment against God’s will.
我们在这里看到了撒旦带给世界的这一基本的谎言（约 8:44），这份谎言构成了“所有
罪恶以及罪恶所产生的所有幻觉”的基础。那就是，上帝及其旨意并非真的良善，人类的福
祉和快乐并不在于以谦卑喜乐的信靠依附于上帝，并不在于以感恩之爱遵行他的旨意。那就
是，人类可以倚靠自己大胆寻求快乐，按自己的意愿和判断悖逆上帝的旨意行事。
Satan is an advocate of the new morality, which denies the concept of an absolute authority as
to what is good and evil and which will not accept an absolute standard of conduct. How much of
modern psychology is saturated with Satan’s basic lie: The human soul, it is asserted, is a very tender
thing, to restrain it by imposing categorical law is to harm it. Our souls should be free to develop
and express themselves in freedom and love. Continue to submit to God’s supposed commandments
and you will warp your personality.
撒旦提倡全新的道德观，这种道德观否认定义何为善恶的绝对权柄，这种道德观拒绝一
套绝对的行为准则。现代心理学受到了撒旦这一基本谎言的严重渗透：有人坚称，人类的灵
魂相当脆弱，若是通过将绝对的法则强加于它而约束于它，这将对它造成伤害。我们的灵魂
应当自由发展，在自由和爱中展现自己。你若继续遵循那些所谓的“神的命令”，那么你的
人格就会发生扭曲。
We indeed want to apply the account of the fall to ourselves and to our times. Yet we make this
application after we have first acknowledged that what the account tells us is an actual happening,
which had dire consequences. Contemporary interpretation of the fall wants only application
without acknowledging that what serves as the occasion for application has in itself happened.
我们当然想要将这份讲述人类堕落的记载应用到我们自己身上，也应用到我们这个时代
之上。不过，我们首先得承认这份记载中的内容却曾真实发生过并造成了严重的后果，接着
才是应用。针对人类堕落这份记载的当代诠释则恰恰相反，它只想谈论所谓的应用，却根本
不承认那让我们可以谈论应用的原始场景曾经实实在在发生过。
Faced by Satan’s lie, Eve had to make a decision. Since by God’s creation she bore God’s image
in a perfect bond of trust, she had a free will and the strength to use it in making that choice correctly.
The whole creation around her and every experience of God heretofore formed a symphony of
evidence for God’s goodness and testified against Satan’s bold lie. Still Eve did not use her freedom,
but spurned God’s love and goodness. She relinquished her trust in God. For freedom is not the right
to do what we please. The freedom that God gave man at creation was rather this, that he had the
strength to do God’s will, not because he had to, but because he wanted to do so by compulsion
from within, constrained by joyful trust and thankfulness. The woman, however, believed Satan’s
lie, disobeyed God’s commandment, and induced her husband to share her guilt. We cannot explain
it. It will always remain a mystery to us. We simply acknowledge and accept it as a fact, on the basis
of God’s clear Word as we have it in the next verse of Genesis.
面对撒旦的谎言，夏娃不得不做出决定。藉神的创造，她拥有了神的形像，且藉信心这

纽带完全联于上帝，所以她拥有自由意志，并有力量使用这份自由意志作出正确的选择。目
前为止，她周围的一切受造物，以及她以往所经历的与上帝相关的每一件事，共同构成了一
部证明上帝良善的交响乐，同时也有力地驳斥了撒旦那无耻的谎言。然而，夏娃却并未使用
她的自由，反倒唾弃神的爱和良善。她松开了她信靠上帝的那条纽带。因为自由并非有权利
随心所欲地做我们想做的事，恰恰相反，上帝在创世之初赋予人类的自由乃是这样的：人有
力量去践行上帝的旨意，且并非由于被迫，而是受到内在强烈的驱使、受到欢然信靠和感恩
之心的约束而乐意去做。然而，这女人却信了撒旦的谎言，违背了上帝的命令，并诱使她的
丈夫与她一同犯罪。事情为何发展至此，我们实在无法解释明白，它对我们而言一直就是一
个谜。我们只是基于创世记下一节经文中上帝明确的话语，承认和接受这一事实而已。

The poison of Satan’s lie had done its work. It caused the woman to give up her joyful trust in
her God. She accepted the tempter’s lie instead. Eve pushed God’s Word aside; she subjected herself
to Satan and his thinking. She “saw that the tree was good for food.” With her trust in God gone,
the image of God which she had borne thus far was shattered. No longer were her thoughts in
harmony with God’s thoughts. She now saw this tree to be good for something for which God had
not ordained it, that is, for food. Her feelings, her emotions, were no longer in harmony with the
evaluation which God’s heart placed upon that which he had created. She saw this tree as pleasing
to the eyes, something for which the Lord had not specially placed it into the garden. She also began
to strive with her will for something that God had not set as a goal, attaining wisdom by disobeying
God’s commandment. Now that Eve had given up her trust in God and instead was now convinced
of Satan’s lie, sin had taken possession of her soul, of every aspect of it—her heart, her mind, her
will. This sin now showed itself in action. She ate of the forbidden fruit. She also gave of it to her
husband, and he ate likewise.

Adam’s sinful action is presented as following out of the woman’s action, the action that she
gave the forbidden fruit to her husband. As often in Hebrew, the obvious object of “gave” is omitted.
We are not told that he too was seduced like Eve by Satan’s lie. Adam disobeyed upon Eve’s
inducement. He was persuaded by Eve. This is in keeping with St. Paul’s statement (1 Ti 2:14):
“Adam was not the one deceived; it was the woman who was deceived and became a sinner.”
亚当的罪行呈现为由这女人将禁果给了她的丈夫这一行为所导致。正如希伯来语的常见
用法一样，“给”这个动作直接作用的明确对象在这里同样被省略了。他是否也像夏娃一样

This does not make Adam’s guilt less great. One could speak of it as being even greater. He
sinned in spite of better knowledge. By the very use that St. Paul made of Adam’s and Eve’s different
modes of succumbing to sin, he points out to us that both fell out of their divinely assigned roles in
their transgression. We will be made aware of this fact in God’s remarks as he lays chastisement
upon each of them. Eve, who had been created as a fitting helper for Adam, presumed to take on the
role of leader, a position for which God had not made her. Deceived by Satan, she took the initiative
in disobeying God’s commandment. Adam, who had been created for the role of leadership, did not
supply such leadership. Moses tells us he “was with her” when she took of the forbidden fruit. We
hear of no word of warning. Instead, he listened to the woman and let her persuade him to follow
her in transgressing God’s commandment. St. Paul urges us to let this serve as a warning against
overthrowing God’s order of creation in the relation of the sexes.

现在，我们已经读完了创世记第 3 章第一部分的内容，也就是第 1-6 节。在这里，我们
从这段关于亚当和夏娃的历史记载中，看到了人类如何受诱惑又背离了耶和华救主上帝的慈
爱与良善。我们已经仔细研读了这段文本，相信它所陈述的乃是真实的历史事件。
Much of contemporary interpretation holds that in regarding Adam and Eve as historical
characters, we have completely missed the point of the Genesis narrative. They contend that we
have overlooked its really deep and profound message. These interpreters suggest that we recognize
that these early chapters of Genesis are religious parables, symbolic stories. A parable may or may
not involve factual happenings. The important thing about a parable is that it conveys a moral, a
message.
但许多当代诠释法则认为，当我们将亚当和夏娃视为真实历史人物的时候，我们就完全
偏离了创世记的叙事重点。他们主张，我们这是忽视了其中蕴含着的深邃信息。这些诠释者

认为，我们应当承认创世记这前几章内容不过就是宗教寓言、具象征意义的故事而已。寓言
可以涉及真实事件，也可以不涉及，但寓言重在传达某种寓意，某种信息。
Take the parable of 2 Samuel 12:1–7, which Nathan told when he rebuked David for his sin of
adultery with Uriah’s wife. It was to awaken abhorrence in David’s heart, and is still to do so in our
hearts, for a selfish act of cruelty, such as is portrayed in the rich man’s action with the poor man’s
ewe lamb. In this vividly portrayed selfish act of cruelty, David was to recognize his own selfish
cruelty. To have lost himself in the question of whether this had actually happened was beside the
point. David momentarily assumed that it had been an actual occurrence and vowed proper judicial
action. Yet Nathan turned David’s thoughts away from such considerations by telling him, “You are
the man.” This is the proper function of a parable. Taking the account of the fall as a parable,
contemporary interpreters say: The account of Genesis 3 is to teach me that I am alienated from God
and need to be reconciled to him. I am Adam. Every man is Adam. Only when I begin to understand
this existential truth can I begin to understand this chapter. The actual revelation occurs when I am
confronted with the truth that I am a fallen creature in need of God’s revelation. Through the words
of Scripture I have an encounter with God, and that is the revelation as a present reality. I am Adam.
This is true when, in reading the words of this chapter, I am confronted with this truth and come to
realize that I am alienated from God by my sin.2
比如撒母耳记下 12:1–7，这是拿单在责备大卫与乌利亚的妻子之间的通奸之罪时所讲述
的寓言。该寓言旨在唤醒大卫内心的厌恶感（且对于如今的我们依然如此）——厌恶寓言中

他即刻假定此事确曾发生，并发誓要对此采取相应的司法行动。然而，拿单却告诉他，“你
就是那人”，藉此彻底扭转了他的思考方向。这就是寓言应当起到的作用。而有些当代诠释
者将人类堕落的记载也视为寓言，他们声称：创世记第 3 章是要让我明白，我与上帝隔离

实体验。当我阅读这一章节、当我面对这一真理并意识到自己因罪而与上帝隔离时，我就是
亚当。2
In answer to such contentions we can only say that it would be difficult, yes, impossible, to
derive such a meaning from the text of chapter 3 of Genesis. This position is not taught in the Bible.
It cannot be derived from a simple reading of Genesis 3:1–6. It is imposed upon the Bible from
without. Such an understanding of Genesis has its roots in existential philosophy.

In 1 Timothy 2:13, 14 we read: “For Adam was formed first, then Eve. And Adam was not the

were historical persons and their deeds factual occurrences, St. Paul’s arguments for not permitting
a woman to have authority over a man lose their force. When we study an Old Testament passage,
we seek to get its meaning from the very words in which it is clothed and from the context in which
it is found. But it is also very proper that we, who have the New Testament revelation for which the
Old Testament is the preparation, look to the New Testament’s statements to verify our
understanding of the Old Testament passages. To do full justice to any verse of Scripture, we take
into account all that the Bible has to say about it. If we do that, we will never come to the conclusion
that the Genesis account of the fall is merely a parable. The New Testament makes it very clear that
there was an Adam and an Eve, a tempter who spoke through a serpent, and an actual catastrophic
fall into sin.

According to Romans 5:12ff., Adam fell from an estate that was good into an estate of being
sinful and evil. This is not true of you and me. When we sin we merely give evidence of the sinful
nature with which we came into the world. By sinning, Adam became a sinner. When we committed

our first sinful deed, we were already sinners. Adam’s sin plunged the whole human race into sin
and death. That is not true of our sin. For St. Paul, Adam and his fall into sin were unique.3 No other

from Adam’s parallel position. Scripture presents Adam’s fatal deed not as a universal predicament
but as a unique happening.

Other documents which have come down to us from antiquity contrast the golden age of the
past with a later age when everything is no longer perfect. All of them leave unanswered what
brought about this change. Genesis 3 alone tells us how mankind became sinful. It is unique. It is
God’s Word presenting the factual happening.

Even before God appeared on the scene after the fall, Adam and Eve already showed themselves
as fallen, sinful creatures. From a formal standpoint, the serpent’s word had proved itself as factual.
Their eyes were opened in the sense that they came to a knowledge of good and evil. Still Satan’s
statement had been a horrendous lie. What Adam and Eve experienced was not the desirable thing
that the tempter had held out to them as a vague promise. Neither was it the knowledge of good and
evil which God had in mind for them by placing the special tree in the middle of the garden. Note
the Hebrew verb ‫יָדַ ע‬. It does not designate intellectual understanding and comprehension or mere
awareness of something. That would be expressed by ‫ בִ ין‬or ‫ ָראָ ה‬. The verb ‫ יָדַ ע‬designates
knowledge gained by experience, experiential knowledge. Adam and Eve now knew good and evil
from the standpoint of sinners. They knew good as something that they had forfeited and lost and
evil as something that they were experiencing in its true cursedness. They experienced their own
corruption.

而言，他们的眼睛也确实开了。然而，撒旦所说的话仍然不过是弥天大谎。亚当和夏娃所经
历的也并非那位试探者向他们含糊其辞所保证的那般惹人心动。并且，上帝将那棵特别的树

The special point mentioned at which their eyes were opened was when they experienced their
nakedness. In Genesis 2:25 we were told of Adam and Eve, who had just been joined in marriage,
that “the two of them were naked, the man and his wife, and they were not [at any time] ashamed.”
As long as they still bore God’s image, their will was in full harmony with God’s will relative to all
the impulses with which God had endowed them, including their sexual and procreative impulses.
They exercised these impulses in perfect love toward God and in accordance with his will and,
hence, in perfect love toward each other.

After having lost God’s image, after having become sinful human beings, Adam and Eve no
longer had full control of their impulses. Selfish, inordinate desires concerning the use of these
impulses began to assert themselves. As they became aware of these desires, guilt feelings likewise
arose in their hearts and thus evoked a sense of shame. So strong were these feelings of guilt and
shame in connection with their nakedness that they immediately sought to provide a remedy. They
improvised loincloths for themselves by sewing fig leaves together. Even a contemporary interpreter
like Logan understands the matter along these lines: “The shame arose because each, in the newfound experience of self-will, became aware that sex could become a tool of selfish gratification,
ministering to self-will, and that therefore there should be a pretense of virtue, attempting to use
outward means to hide inward selfishness.”4 We will presently see how reluctant Adam and Eve
were as sinners to acknowledge and confess their guilt. Yet quite unwittingly and unintentionally
they already confessed their guilt as they sought to cover their nakedness. The seed of death and
corruption was now in their hearts, a seed that would ripen and break forth into physical death and
eternal death, everlasting separation from God.

Moses uses a Hithpael in describing the hiding to which Adam and Eve were moved in their
fear. We know what happens when there is a mouse in a dark room and the light is suddenly turned
on. The mouse will scurry here and there to find cover. Such action is aptly expressed by a Hithpael
form of the Hebrew verb. The Hithpael has a reciprocal, intensive, reflexive force. With their fear
of God and their resultant fearful actions, Adam and Eve revealed their depraved condition. Yet we
also want to take note of the sinner’s folly in his guilt-born fear of God. He vainly sought to hide
from God, vainly sought to protect himself from God’s just punishment with his own feeble and
foolish efforts. Here is the beginning of the opinio legis, man’s vain thought of somehow rescuing
and saving himself from God’s just punishment. This opinion of the law has remained indelibly
ingrained in natural man ever since.5

因上帝向人类所怀的信实的大爱，现在他要向他这些堕落的受造物施与帮助，因他们自
己根本无法自助。根据第 14、15 节，耶和华神将他用他信实的爱为他们所制定的那全新计
划告诉了他们。首先，我们从第 14 节中得知，上帝如何咒诅了那条蛇——那用来施展诱惑
的器皿，随即也最重要的是，那位试探者自己——撒旦受到了咒诅。

14 耶和华神对那条蛇说，“你既作了这事，就必受咒诅，比一切牲畜和田间的野兽

If we want to understand this verse, we will have to cling to what was said in connection with
verse 1. God was dealing with a serpent, but he was also dealing with more than a serpent.
如果我们想要理解这节经文，我们就得紧紧依附于我们对第 1 节经文的分析。上帝现在
所面对的是一条蛇，但他所面对的又不仅仅是一条蛇。
A serpent was certainly there, and God was dealing with it. The text says so. It not only states
in so many words that God spoke to the serpent, but it also speaks of a curse which the serpent,
more than all the other beasts, is henceforth to display as it moves about. It is to move, to crawl, on
its belly. In doing so it will eat dust. This is not to say that dust itself will be its food, but that moving
about, as it will, close to the ground, it will invariably consume dust along with its food. Such a
mode of moving about is a sign of defeat and humiliation. In its actions, this creature had exalted
itself above man; forever after it is to be degraded in man’s eyes.
蛇当然就在那里，上帝所面对的确实是一条蛇，因为这是圣经经文所言。经文不仅用不
少篇幅表明上帝确实是在同那条蛇说话，也谈到了上帝对它的咒诅，这咒诅要比其它所有动
物所受的更甚，即从此以后，它都要用肚子行走，以此作为该咒诅的记号。它要用肚子行走，
用肚子爬行。当它爬行的时候，它还要吃土。不过这并不是在说它要以土为食，而是说，当
它紧贴地面四处爬行的时候，它将会在吞吃食物的时候不可避免地咽下尘土。这种四处行走
的模式是一种象征失败与耻辱的记号。该受造物藉其行动将自己高抬，让自己高过人类；但
从此以后，它要在人的眼中永远受辱。
It is idle to speculate about the serpent’s former mode of moving about and to what extent it
had been different before the fall. What is said here is said for the benefit of man living after the fall.
We are told what we are to see in the serpent’s present manner of movement. We are to be reminded
of the wicked fatal deed in which the serpent was involved as an instrument. At the same time, we
are to realize that this wickedness will end not in glory and success but in utter humiliation and
defeat.
这条蛇从前如何行走呢？现在的样子与它在人类堕落前的样子差别又有多大呢？揣测
这些问题实在毫无意义。上帝在这里所说的话是要叫活在人类堕落之后的人受益。这节经文
告诉我们的是，从蛇现在的行走方式中我们应当看到些什么。我们当受提醒，晓得这条被用
作器皿的蛇所牵涉的致命行为是何等邪恶。与此同时，我们也当意识到一点，即这种邪恶的
结局绝非荣耀与成功，而是彻头彻尾的耻辱与失败。
Nevertheless, we cannot here think only of the serpent. We need to face the fact that God spoke
to the serpent. Addressing someone assumes that you are dealing with a rational creature. According
to the creation account, the serpent is not such a rational creature. Thus this speaking of the Lord
God to the serpent is again evidence that he was dealing with more than a serpent, that he was
dealing with the serpent as the instrument of the evil one.
然而，我们不能认为这里仅仅只有这条蛇。我们需要面对一个事实，那就是上帝对着这
条蛇说话了。跟某个人说话意味着你所面对的是一种具有理性的受造物。根据创世记载我们
得知，这条蛇可不是一种具有理性的受造物。因此，耶和华神对这条蛇说话这件事就是另一
项证据，表明他所面对的并不仅仅只是一条蛇而已，而是那恶者用作器皿的那条蛇。
The words spoken by God to the serpent strike out at this evil one, the tempter of mankind,
Satan. This is brought out by the first statement of the Lord: “Because you have done this …” These
are words that can properly be addressed only to an individual who has moral responsibility. It is
Satan, rather than the serpent, who is held accountable for the wicked deed done. Likewise, the
announcement of humiliation and defeat which follows is therefore announced to Satan rather than
to the serpent, though outwardly the serpent is to bear the sign and symbol of defeat.
上帝向这条蛇说话，他用这些话连番猛击那恶者，即试探人类的那一位，撒旦。耶和华
的第一句话就展现出了这一点：“你既作了这事......”这些话只能是说给一位必须承担道德
责任的个体听的。需要对这恶事负责的并非这条蛇，而是撒旦。因此同样，随后上帝也是在
对着撒旦而非这条蛇宣告牠的耻辱与失败，尽管这条蛇要以一种外显的方式承受失败的记
号。
That actually a sign or symbol of defeat and humiliation is involved is evident from other
passages of Scripture where the same language is employed and even a reference to this recorded
incident is included. In Micah 7:17 we read, for example, “They shall lick the dust like a serpent, …
they shall be afraid of the Lord our God and shall fear because of thee” (AV). What Micah is here
depicting is the ultimate triumph of God’s people, God’s church, and the utter defeat and humiliation
of its enemies. Of similar import is Psalm 72:9: “The desert tribes will bow before him and his
enemies will lick the dust.” Noteworthy also is the fact that God asked the tempter, whom he was
really addressing in the serpent, no pedagogical questions. He addressed such questions to Adam
and Eve as evidence of his faithfulness toward them, as evidence of his intent to show them mercy.
God holds out no mercy or grace to the tempter, to Satan, but only judgment, punishment as
retribution for the violation of God’s majesty, not punishment as a corrective chastening.
在这里，这条蛇所受的咒诅确实是象征失败与耻辱的记号，这一点可以从圣经别处的一
些经文中得到佐证，那些经文使用了相同的语言，甚至提到了这里记录的这件事。比如，在
弥迦书 7:17 中我们读到，
“他们必舔土如蛇......他们必害怕我们的神耶和华，必因你而战兢”
（钦定版）。弥迦在这里所刻画的是上帝百姓（即神的教会）的最终得胜及其所有仇敌的彻
底失败与耻辱。诗篇 72:9 为我们呈现了相似的画面：“住在旷野的，必在他面前下拜。他
的仇敌，必要舔土。”另外值得我们注意的是，当上帝质问这条蛇背后的那位试探者时，他
并没有使用什么引导式提问。他向亚当和夏娃那样提问正是他以他的信实对待他们的证据，
正是他向他们显出怜悯的证据。然而，上帝却并未对那位试探者——撒旦——显出任何的怜
悯或恩典，他所显出的唯有审判，唯有对于侵犯上帝的威严所施的报应式惩罚，而非矫正与
管教式惩罚。
Ultimately these words are spoken to the tempter and to the serpent, as his instrument, for man’s
sake. Adam and Eve were to hear these words; they were to hear that Satan would not permanently
triumph in his wickedness, but rather suffer humiliation and defeat. All this is carried out more fully
in verse 15, the protevangelium.
这些对着那位试探者及其器皿（即那条蛇）所说的话最终不过为着人类的益处而说的。
亚当和夏娃应当听见这些话；他们应当听见：撒旦绝不会靠着牠的邪恶一直得胜，反而将会
一直蒙羞，一直失败。第 15 节中的第一福音（protevangelium）将为我们更加详细地阐明这
一点。
It is for man’s sake also that the serpent, the beast employed as Satan’s instrument, is to serve
as a constant reminder of Satan’s ultimate and lasting defeat and humiliation. Elsewhere in Scripture
we meet with instances likewise in which the irrational creature, which as such is not involved in
moral responsibility, must nevertheless, not for its own sake but for man’s sake, suffer punishment
outwardly. In Genesis 9:5 we read that the beast which is instrumental in the loss of human life is
to be put to death in order that man may be reminded of the inviolate sacredness of human life as
sinful man’s God-given time of grace. For a similar reason it was stipulated in the Mosaic Law,
God’s temporary discipline for his Old Testament people, that the ox that had gored a man was to
be stoned (Ex 21:28). Similarly the Mosaic Law prescribed that in cases of human acts of sexual
perversion with a beast, the beast was to be put to death (Lev 20:15f.). Man was thereby to be
reminded that such perversion was worthy of death. In the last analysis, we know from the New
Testament (Ro 8:19–22) that the entire creature world since the fall must perform a function of this
kind for fallen mankind as it groans under the bondage of frustration and corruption. This is not
because of a sinful exercise of its own will, but by God’s will, who wants the constant evidence of
pain, struggle, futility, and death in the creature world around us to be an ever-present, earnest
reminder of the curse of human sin.
同样，为着人类的益处，这条被撒旦用作器皿的蛇——这头野兽，被用来不断提醒我们：
撒旦终将失败与蒙羞，且将永远如此。在圣经别处我们也遇到了一些类似的例子，即某种不
具有理性、不必承担道德责任的受造物却必须要为人类的缘故而非自己的缘故受到外在的惩
罚。在创世记 9:5 中我们读到，若是被用作器皿的野兽叫人丧命，这野兽也必须要被治死，
这是为了人类可以受到提醒：上帝赐给罪人的一生——这“蒙恩的时机”神圣不可侵犯。为
着类似的理由，摩西律法作为上帝对其旧约百姓的现世管教，其中规定，牛若触死人，总要
用石头将它打死（出 21:28）。与之相似，摩西律法还规定，人若与兽行淫，那兽就要被治
死（利 20:15 及其下文）。从而，人当受到提醒：这类邪淫配得一死。最后一例则出自新约
（罗 8:19–22），自从人类堕落之后，当整个受造的世界因服在虚空之下、受到败坏的辖制
而唉声叹息之时，它就是在被迫为着堕落的人类发挥上述这类功用。这并非出于这个世界自
己罪恶的意志，而是出于上帝的旨意，他想要让我们这个受造世界中的痛苦、挣扎、虚空以
及死亡作为那持续的证据，不断真切地提醒着我们人类犯罪所带来的咒诅。
The victory over Satan and over his fatal act of temptation, already touched upon in verse 14,
is fully announced for mankind’s comfort in verse 15.

We noted that verse 14 already alluded to the tempter. The serpent was, however, still in the
foreground. Now the matter is reversed. The evil one is definitely in the foreground, though still
addressed as the serpent and spoken of as the serpent, in whose guise he was present.
我们注意到，第 14 节早就暗暗提到了那位试探者，只是那条蛇却还相对惹眼。尽管上
帝仍然是在对那恶者藉以伪装的这条蛇说话，且仍然以蛇相称，但现在情况却有了反转，显
然那恶者开始相对惹眼起来了。
The object, enmity (‫)אֵ יבָ ה‬, emphatically heads the sentence. It is the all-important concept.
Eve had become the willing servant of Satan. She had obeyed his lie. She had renounced her trust
in God and hence had also withdrawn her obedience from God. She had transferred both, her trust
and her obedience, to Satan. Over all this Satan was rejoicing. He surmised that her trust and
obedience would always stay with him.

敌意（‫）אֵ יבָ ה‬，动词的作用对象置于句首，起到了强调作用。这一点尤为重要。夏娃已
经成了一名乐意服侍撒旦的奴仆。她听从了牠的谎言。她不再信靠上帝，也因而不再顺服上
帝。她将她的信靠对象和顺服对象同时换作了撒旦。撒旦当然是心里乐开了花，牠以为她会
从此一直信靠并顺服牠。
God, however, announced: “Enmity, hostility, will I establish between you and between the
woman.” It is not that there was no enmity present thus far. We saw how Adam and Eve had become
God’s enemies, hiding before him in fear, refusing to accept responsibility for their disobedience of
his will. We saw also how the man had displayed enmity toward the woman, blaming her for his
transgression. But all this enmity and hostility was misdirected. If man was to be helped, this enmity
would have to be redirected in the proper direction. He would once again have to acknowledge God
in humble faith as his gracious benefactor; and his enmity would again have to be directed against
Satan. Unless this would come about, man would remain basically mistaken about all things and
would in a fundamental sense continue to misjudge and misuse everything.
然而，神却宣告说：“论到敌意、仇恨，我要将它置于你和这女人之间。”这并不是说
在此之前敌意这玩意儿就从未存在过。我们看到亚当和夏娃如何成了神的仇敌，战战兢兢躲
避他的面，拒绝为他们悖逆他的旨意而承担责任。我们也看到那男人如何向那女人心怀敌意，
埋怨他的犯罪都是因她所致。然而，所有这些敌意和仇恨全都放错了位置。若是人类要得帮
助，那么这些敌意就要得到矫正并被恰当安置。他将不得不再次以谦卑的信心承认上帝就是
他的赐恩者；他的敌意也将不得不再次被引向撒旦。除非此事达成，否则人类就将从根本上

错误看待一切事物，并不断误判、滥用一切事物。
It is worth noting that God did not proceed to advise Eve to redirect her enmity against the
serpent. She utterly lacked the power to follow such advice. Neither did God command Eve to hate
the evil one. She could not have obeyed such a command. Instead God announced his own intentions:
I, God, will put, establish, enmity between you and the woman. God himself, God who alone was
able, would do it. Proper enmity would be established by his divine initiative. It could be established
in no other way. God was not promoting an enmity that was potentially there; he was not arousing
something that had temporarily become dormant and inactive.
值得我们注意的是，上帝接着并未向夏娃提议，教她如何重新憎恨撒旦。她已彻底丧失
了遵循这类提议的能力。上帝也未命令夏娃去憎恨那恶者。她根本无法听从这样的命令。取
而代之，上帝表明了他自己的意图：我，上帝，要将敌意置于你和那女人之间。上帝亲自作
成了此事，也唯独他能作成此事。正确的敌意将由上帝亲自发动，并无其它任何方式。上帝
并不是在推动某种早已潜在的敌意，而是在发动某种目前已经死寂、沉睡的敌意。
Satan had to hear this, for it was said to him, as the serpent was directly addressed. For Satan
this was an announcement of judgment and defeat. All he could do was listen. God did not invite
him to respond. As soon as God had made this pronouncement to the tempter, the Lord again turned
to Eve and Adam, whom he had previously drawn into conversation, with searching questions. It
was for Eve’s and for Adam’s benefit that God announced this great news that he would again
establish enmity between Satan and the woman. Here we have an initial revelation of God’s
unmerited, yet triumphant grace.
撒旦不得不听到这些内容，因为当上帝对着蛇说话的时候，这些实际上都是说给牠听的。
对于撒旦而言，这些内容就是在宣告牠受审判，宣告牠失败。除了竖起耳朵听之外，牠别无
其它选择。上帝并未请牠作答。当上帝对那位试探者作完宣判之后，耶和华便立刻再次转向
夏娃和亚当。之前他以提问引导他们，而接下去他也要继续同他们展开对话。上帝之所以郑
重其事地宣布了——他要重新在撒旦和这女人之间放置敌意——这一大好消息，是为着夏娃
和亚当的益处。我们在这里看到上帝首次将他的恩典——那并非出于人的功劳却又大大得胜
的恩典——启示了出来。
Would this divinely established enmity be restricted to these two individuals, to the serpent and
the woman? Would it come to an end when they came to their earthly ends? No, God promised more
in his announcement. God is still the subject, enmity is still the object, and God is still speaking
about establishing enmity as he goes on to say that he would put it likewise between “your seed,”
i.e., the serpent’s seed, and between “her seed,” i.e., the woman’s seed. The Hebrew singular noun

‫ ז ֶַרע‬is a collective in both instances. It clearly refers to a plurality of individuals. This in turn makes
it very clear that the previous statement concerning the enmity between the serpent and the woman
has to do with two individuals. The previous statement does not treat in generic terms of the serpents
as a class or of womanhood in general as the latter statement does.
上帝是否仅仅在这条蛇与这女人之间放置了这份敌意呢？此二者若是离开了这个世界，
那么这份敌意是否就从此消失了呢？不，在上帝所宣告的内容中，他的应许不止于此。上帝
仍是主语，敌意仍是宾语，上帝也仍在谈论放置敌意这件事情，他接着表明，他要将敌意置
于“你的后裔”（即这条蛇的后裔）与“她的后裔”（即这女人的后裔）之间。在这两个词
组中，希伯来语单数名词‫（ז ֶַרע‬字面义为“种子”）皆为集合名词，它明显指代某个群体。
这样一来，上一句话中提到的“这条蛇与这女人之间的敌意”也就明显仅限于两名个体之间
了。上一句话并不是在谈论蛇这一类别，也不是在谈论女人这一群体，但这句话采用的却是
这类表述方式。
Who is meant by the woman’s seed and the serpent’s seed as God goes on to say that he will
establish enmity between them also? The promise is certainly intended for all of Eve’s descendants.
Potentially the woman’s seed would therefore be all of Eve’s descendants. But when we think of the
actual establishment of the enmity envisioned in this divine promise, only those of Eve’s
descendants could come into consideration who would come to be like Eve when she would again
be at enmity with the tempter. Only then was Eve again at enmity with Satan when God had brought
her back to faith in him, when she was led by God to trust in his saving grace. For this a complete
change of heart was needed. Yet God had promised to bring this about. It would be the same with
Eve’s seed. Those of her seed, her descendants, whom God would put at enmity with Satan’s seed,
would be those in whom the Lord would likewise awaken a humble trust in his saving grace. They
would be the believers.
上帝声称要在这女人的后裔和这条蛇的后裔之间放置敌意，那么他们的后裔又是指谁呢？
这一应许当然是赐给夏娃所有后裔的。因此，就潜在的可能性而言，这女人的后裔指的是夏
娃的所有后裔。然而，当我们想到上帝在这应许中所放置的未来这份真实的敌意时，我们知
道，只有那些“与那位如今再次与那位试探者为敌的夏娃同类的”后裔才能被纳入考虑范围
之内。而只有当上帝再次赐予夏娃信心之时，只有当她蒙神引导、蒙赐信心而信靠他的救恩
之时，夏娃才开始再次与撒旦为敌。为成就此事，夏娃的心思意念必须被全然改变，不过，
上帝已经应许成就此事。对于夏娃的后裔而言，同样如此。在她的后裔之中，唯有那些蒙上
帝将敌意置于他们与撒旦的后裔之间的后裔，才会与夏娃一样蒙耶和华赐他们信靠他救恩的
心。他们也就是信徒。
Satan’s seed, or the serpent’s seed, would be all of Satan’s followers, evil angels and evil human
beings—all who like Satan would be enemies of God and would be active in destroying God’s good
works. To think of physical descendants is out of the question, since physical propagation does not
come into consideration as far as Satan is concerned. Essentially Satan is an angel. What Jesus says
concerning angels, that marriage and propagation do not come into consideration for them (Mt
22:30), therefore pertains also to Satan, though he is a fallen angel.
撒旦的后裔，或说这条蛇的后裔，则将会是撒旦的所有跟随者，也就是邪恶天使与恶人
——所有与撒旦相似的天使或人都将会是神的仇敌，他们都将积极投身于败坏上帝美好善工
的“伟大事业”之中。这里绝对不是在说肉身所生的后裔，因为就撒旦而言，繁殖后代这件
事根本不在我们的考虑范围之内。本质上，撒旦是一名天使。根据耶稣讲论天使的话语，婚
姻和繁殖这类事与他们并无半点瓜葛（太 22:30），因此，此事也同样适用于撒旦，尽管他
是一名堕落天使。
Such an understanding of the woman’s seed and the serpent’s seed is in keeping with the
language of Scripture elsewhere. In the parable of the weeds among the wheat (Mt 13:24–43), where
the presence of the unbelievers in the world, God’s field, is under discussion, Jesus explains: “…
the good seed stands for the sons of the kingdom. The weeds are the sons of the evil one, and the
enemy who sows them is the devil.” On another occasion when Jesus rebuked the hostile Jewish
leaders, the teachers of the law and the Pharisees, who like their prophet-slaying forefathers were
obstructing and opposing God’s saving activity, he addressed them: “You snakes! You brood of
vipers.” Just as God announced in the protevangelium, the Lord Jesus also announced judgment to
this serpent’s brood: “How will you escape being condemned to hell (Mt 23:33)?”
上述关于这女人的后裔以及这条蛇的后裔的看法，与圣经别处的说法并无二致。在稗子
和麦子的比喻中（太 13:24-43），耶稣谈到了上帝的田地，也就是这个世界，且非信徒便在
其中：“好种，就是天国之子。稗子，就是那恶者之子。撒稗子的仇敌，就是魔鬼。”又有
一次，耶稣责备那些敌对他的犹太领袖——文士与法利赛人，称他们拦阻并反对上帝的救恩
作为，与他们那些杀害先知的先祖并无区别：“你们这些蛇类，毒蛇之种啊。”正如上帝在
第一福音中所宣告的，主耶稣也向这条毒蛇的后裔同样说到：“（你们）怎能逃脱地狱的刑
罚呢？”
When, according to the gospel of St. John, Jesus was testifying to similarly hostile Jews who
scorned and rejected the Savior’s person and work, he told them: “You belong to your father, the
devil, and you want to carry out your father’s desire. He was a murderer from the beginning, not
holding to the truth, for there is no truth in him. When he lies, he speaks his native language, for he
is a liar and the father of lies” (Jn 8:44). This designation of unbelievers as Satan’s seed or offspring
is particularly significant because in elaborating his assertion, Jesus is clearly alluding to happenings
and details pertaining to the fall.
另外，根据约翰福音，针对那些嘲笑、敌对并拒绝我们救主的位格与作为的犹太人，耶
稣同样对他们说到：“你们是出于你们的父魔鬼，你们父的私欲，你们偏要行，他从起初是
杀人的，不守真理。因他心里没有真理，他说谎是出于自己，因他本来是说谎的，也是说谎
之人的父。”这种将非信徒称为撒旦后裔或种子的说法尤其值得我们注意，因为当耶稣在阐
述他的主张时，很明显，他也是在暗指人类堕落这件事以及其中的一些细节。
A striking confirmation of this understanding of the serpent’s seed is found in 1 John 3:8–13.
We will need to content ourselves with a few salient points. In verse 8 we are told: “He who does
what is sinful is of the devil, because the devil has been sinning from the beginning.” Note that in
speaking of sinners as being “of the devil,” this is led back to “the beginning.” This is the very time
about which God’s promise in the garden speaks. Verse 10 then sets forth a contrast: “This is how
we know who the children of God are and who the children of the devil are: Anyone who does not
do what is right is not a child of God; neither is anyone who does not love his brother.” This is then
elucidated in verse 12 by an illustration: “Do not be like Cain, who belonged to the evil one and
murdered his brother. And why did he murder him? Because his own actions were evil and his
brother’s were righteous.” Eve’s very first seed, her first son, Cain, is designated as belonging “to
the evil one” because of his fruit of unbelief. He belonged to the serpent’s seed, Satan’s seed.
上述关于那条蛇的后裔的看法在约翰一书 3:8-13 中得到了充分的证实。接下来我们只需
关注其中涉及到的几个关键点即可。我们从第 8 节中得知：“犯罪的是属魔鬼，因为魔鬼从
起初就犯罪。”我们注意到，论到罪人“属魔鬼”，约翰将这一点追溯到了“起初”，即上

该隐，因其不信所结的恶果而被称为属于“那恶者”。他属于那条蛇的后裔，即撒旦的后裔。
St. John closes the section with the exhortation in verse 13: “Do not be surprised, my brothers,
if the world hates you.” The enmity between the serpent’s seed and the woman’s seed, which the
Lord announced as something that he would bring about, continues. This is the enmity which exists
between God’s believing children and the unbelieving world to this very day. It began with Cain’s
hatred of Abel, and it is experienced by Christians today. It is not that Christians hate the unbelievers
as persons. They know that their heavenly Father so loved the world that he gave his only begotten
Son to redeem them all and to make them each individually his own by faith. So also God’s children,

Christian believers, are intent upon winning everybody in the world through the gospel for Christ
and his salvation. The hostility arises from the unbelieving world, which remains adamant in its sin
and unbelief. The unbelievers, Satan’s seed, resent the Christians’ childlike trust in their Savior and

humble faith and thankful love toward God. Jesus speaks of this when he says, “Do you think I
came to bring peace on earth? No, I tell you, but division” (Lk 12:51; Mt 10:34–36).

The question has been raised whether it is permissible to take the woman’s seed to be a
collective in a part of the protevangelium and then in the last distich to have the pronoun ‫ הּוא‬and
the suffix of ‫ּתְ שּופֶנּו‬, which have the woman’s seed as their antecedent, refer to one individual.
We are convinced that the Hebrew collective lends itself well for just such a usage. This can be
shown from other instances. The collective singular may refer to all the individuals making up a
certain class and at the same time refer also to one individual member by whom this class is in a
special way represented.

kind. But even these interpreters generally agree that in what they call its present position in Genesis
3, this enmity oracle has taken on a further purpose—it is somehow symbolic of man’s constant
struggle with the forces of evil and temptation.

discussion, he is the one who establishes the enmity. Since God is the one who establishes the enmity,

man in this promised victorious champion of mankind. With Luther we will also want to note the

Was this correctly introduced as disciplinary chastening? Or is it punishment? In giving an
answer, we bear in mind that these terms are not always used with strict distinction. They are
frequently used as interchangeable synonyms. But for the purpose of this exposition, it is vital that
we carefully distinguish between the two terms. In its strict sense and usage, punishment which God
inflicts is compensation in suffering for the violation of his divine majesty. Such punishment flows
out of God’s holy and righteous wrath over sin. All suffering now and hereafter ultimately becomes
such punishment for those who are obdurate and remain obdurate in their sin and unbelief (Ro 12:19;
Gal 6:7).
这些是否应被视为管教措施呢？还是说惩罚呢？当我们回答这个问题时，我们得记住，
这些术语之间并非总是有着严格的区分，它们常常可以作为同义词互换着使用。然而就此处
的诠释而言，我们必须仔细区分这两种术语，这一点至关重要。先说惩罚一词，就其严格的
意义和用法而言，上帝所施加的惩罚是因人侵犯其神圣威严而为之，是要叫人通过受苦偿还
他的罪孽。这等惩罚源自上帝向罪恶所怀的圣洁又公义的怒气。现在以及今后所有的痛苦终
会成为这样的惩罚，加在那些在罪和不信中顽梗悖逆并持续顽梗悖逆的人身上。
God’s chastening, on the other hand, is suffering which he inflicts out of love for a corrective
purpose. While God is still offering sinners a time of grace, all the sufferings which God sends upon
them are still chastenings. They are designed to make a person mindful of his sins, of the sins’
merited curses, and of his utter helplessness before God so that he may take refuge in the grace of
God announced in the gospel. During this earthly life, God continues to send sufferings as such

chastenings also upon those who have come to faith and who enjoy his pardoning grace. These
chastenings are to aid them in their bitter struggles with their sinful flesh (Pr 3:11, 12; Heb 12:5, 6).
另一方面，神的管教则是他出于爱、为着纠正的缘故而施加的苦难。只要上帝仍在赐给
罪人“蒙恩的时机”，那么上帝加在他们身上的所有痛苦就仍然都是管教。上帝之所以施以
管教，其目的是要让人意识到自己的罪、罪所招致的咒诅以及他在上帝面前完全无助的光景，
好叫他可以倚靠福音所宣告的神的恩典，以此为他的避难所。只要那些已经信靠他又享受他
赦罪恩典的人还在这世上，上帝就会继续用这样的管教向他们施加痛苦。当他们与他们罪恶
的肉体苦苦相争之时，这些管教是于他们有益的（箴 3:11,12；来 12:5,6）。

corrective chastenings. They were intended to serve as constant reminders of the sin to which the
woman had succumbed, which had made her subject to death and God’s displeasure. Yet in his
faithful love, God had permitted the woman to hear the gospel message of the protevangelium, so
that she might find comfort in it.
现在，耶和华向这女人宣布她要承受的苦难与疼痛，这些都是为着纠正而施加的管教。
其目的旨在不断提醒她所屈服的罪，并且，这罪叫她服于死亡与上帝的怒气之下。然而，上

The particular hardships and discomforts announced to the woman as her future lot were
calculated to keep her mindful of the nature of her fateful, sinful deed. In believing Satan’s lie and
in eating of the forbidden fruit, she had sought exquisite delights. Instead, what sin brought her was
sorrow and pain. God states very emphatically: “I will greatly multiply your pain and your
conception.” It is quite obvious, however, that ‫ עִ צְ בֹונְֵך וְ הֵ רֹ נְֵך‬is to be understood as a hendiadys.
In this figure of speech, two nouns are coordinated when, in actual function, one of them is really
subordinate, having the force of a descriptive adjective. That is why we have previously translated:
“I will greatly increase your painful conception.” For it is not the multiplying of the woman’s
conceptions in itself which is to be thought of as an evil consequence of sin. In mankind’s state of
perfection, immediately after God had created mankind male and female, it was an act of blessing
that God commanded them to be fruitful and to multiply and thereby enabled them to do so. The
reminder of sin and its curses for the woman lay in the pain and discomfort which was henceforth
to characterize each conception, each pregnancy, and each birth—the whole process of bearing and
rearing children. In pain she would now bear children. The fact that sin brought not joy but distress
and pain was to be brought home to the woman in connection with her basic womanly functions,
her functions as wife and mother of the human race.

无法在完全的爱中行使他们的领导权。罪在哪里不受约束地肆意蔓延，男人的管辖也会常常
堕落为对女人残酷无情的支配，甚至会让女人陷入受屈辱的奴隶地位。
Genesis 3:16 is a reaffirmation, not the establishment, of the God-ordained relationship between
man and woman as it is to be expressed most specifically in marriage. As we noted in Genesis 2,
God already established man’s headship and woman’s subordination at the very creation of the man
and of the woman. He established this relationship in the interest of the basic institutions of marriage
and the family. The hardships, and the sin and selfishness, which the woman would now experience
in the marriage relationship were now to serve her by God’s will and announcement as corrective
chastenings, as a wholesome reminder of sin and its curses.
创世记 3:16 是在重新确认上帝所设立的婚姻中所明确建立好的男女关系，而非首次设
立。正如我们在创世记第 2 章中所看到的，上帝早在创造男女的时候就已经设立好了男人的
领袖身份以及女人的从属地位。他之所以设立这种关系，目的是为维护婚姻和家庭的基本制
度。如今，这女人在婚姻关系中所要经历的艰难、罪恶与自私，都将按着上帝的旨意以及他
所宣告的，用作纠正她的管教，用作对她有益的提醒，让她铭记她所犯过的罪以及罪所招致
的咒诅。
Some have considered her subordination to her husband in marriage in itself to be the chastening
or punishment which God laid upon woman because of her sin in the fall. But this viewpoint has no
scriptural support. Nowhere does Scripture place the sole or even the prime responsibility for the
fall into sin upon the woman. If Eve was subordinated to her husband and bidden to be in submission
as a punishment for her disobedience, then Adam would be absolved of all guilt in being appointed
to rule over her. Then Adam’s excuse of blaming Eve (Ge 3:12) would have been upheld. But
actually, with this excuse, Adam revealed his own total depravity. As we shall presently see from
Genesis 3:17–19, Adam is by no means absolved from guilt. Though Adam’s headship over his wife
is upheld after the fall, St. Paul, just because of this headship, ascribes the full responsibility for the
entrance of sin to Adam (Ro 5:12): “Sin entered the world through one man, and death through sin.”
有些人认为，她之所以要在婚姻关系中从属于她的丈夫，这是由于她犯罪堕落之后上帝
对她所施加的管教或惩罚所致。然而，此观点并无圣经依据。圣经从未将人类堕落犯罪的责
任全部甚或主要归咎于那女人。如果夏娃因悖逆上帝之后受到惩罚才从属于她的丈夫、才受
命应当如此顺服，那么亚当也就可以免除他在受命管辖她一事上所犯下的罪了，即，亚当那
时埋怨夏娃（3:12）的借口也就成立了。然而，亚当的这一借口恰恰暴露了他自身全然败坏
的光景。正如我们随后将在创世记 3:17-19 中所看到的，亚当绝无可能免除罪责。尽管堕落
之后亚当管辖其妻的领袖地位依然成立，然而，正是由于这种领袖地位，圣保罗才将“罪进
入世界”的全部责任都归在了亚当身上（罗 5:12）：“这就如罪是从一人入了世界，死又是
从罪来的。”
Above all, the aforementioned viewpoint (subordination itself equals punishment) is not in
keeping with the scriptural concept of the marriage relationship. It is not in harmony with the Lord’s
use of the marriage bond. Both in the Old and the New Testaments, he uses the relationship between
husband and wife to depict the blessed relationship between himself and his people, his church of
believers (Isa 54:5; 62:4, 5; Jer 3:14; 31:32; Eph 5:23, 24; Rev 19:7–9). Only because the marriage
bond is a blessed relation can it depict the blessed relation between the Lord and his church of
believers. This would not be true if the woman’s submission to her husband bore the stigma of a
chastening made necessary because of sin. The scriptural use of the marriage bond to picture the
bond between Christ and his church remains fitting and meaningful only when with St. Paul in 1
Corinthians, on the basis of Genesis 2, we lead it back to creation: “The head of the woman is
man.… For man did not come from woman, but woman from man; neither was man created for
woman, but woman for man” (1 Co 11:3, 8, 9).
最重要的是，上述观点（女人的从属地位本身就等于惩罚）与圣经阐述婚姻关系的观念
并不一致，也与耶和华使用婚姻这条纽带的方式并不一致。无论是在旧约还是在新约中，他
都将丈夫和妻子之间的这种关系比作他自己和他百姓（即他的信徒所组成的教会）之间的那
种美好关系（赛 54:5；62:4,5；耶 3:14；31:32；弗 5:23,24；启 19:7-9）。唯独因为婚姻的纽
带是一种美好的关系，它才能用来比作耶和华和他的教会之间的美好关系。如果这女人之于
其丈夫的从属地位背上了因犯罪而不得不受神管教的污名，那么上述美好的关系也就不得成
立了。“我愿意你们知道，基督是各人的头。男人是女人的头，神是基督的头......起初，男
人不是由女人而出。女人乃是由男人而出。并且男人不是为女人造的。女人乃是为男人造的”
（林前 11:3, 8, 9），只有当我们基于创世记第 2 章，与圣保罗一同回到上帝创造的这番原貌
之时，圣经将婚姻这条纽带比作基督和他教会之间这条纽带的说法方可继续展现其恰当含
义。
With this understanding, Paul could indeed write: “Wives, submit to your husbands as to the
Lord. For the husband is the head of the wife as Christ is the head of the church, his body, of which
he is the Savior. Now as the church submits to Christ, so also wives should submit to their husbands
in everything. Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for
her to make her holy, cleansing her by the washing with water through the word, and to present her
to himself as a radiant church, without stain or wrinkle or any other blemish, but holy and blameless.
In this same way, husbands ought to love their wives as their own bodies” (Eph 5:22–28).
基于这样的观点，保罗如此写到：“你们作妻子的，当顺服自己的丈夫，如同顺服主。
因为丈夫是妻子的头，如同基督是教会的头。他又是教会全体的救主。教会怎样顺服基督，
妻子也要怎样凡事顺服丈夫。你们作丈夫的，要爱你们的妻子，正如基督爱教会，为教会舍
己。要用水借着道，把教会洗净，成为圣洁，可以献给自己，作个荣耀的教会，毫无玷污皱
纹等类的病，乃是圣洁没有瑕疵的。丈夫也当照样爱妻子，如同爱自己的身子。爱妻子，便
是爱自己了”（弗 5:22–28）。
It is a blessed privilege for us as believers to submit to Christ who has redeemed us in selfsacrificing love. To do so as believers in thankful love is the height of blessedness. In this way the
wife will find the true blessedness of God’s gift of the marriage bond to the extent that she submits

in love to her husband as her head. Similarly, the husband will find the true blessedness of God’s
gift of the marriage bond to the extent to which he exercises his headship over his wife in Christlike
self-sacrificing love.
对于我们信徒而言，有幸得以顺服那位用他自己牺牲的爱救赎我们的基督是一项蒙福的
特权。信徒带着感恩的爱如此行，这是他们极大的福气。以这样的方式，妻子要在上帝所赐
的婚姻这条纽带中得到真正的福气，以至于她要在爱中顺服他的丈夫，视他为她的头；同样，
丈夫也要在上帝所赐的婚姻这条纽带中得到真正的福气，以至于他要用基督一般牺牲自我的
爱来带领她的妻子。
The evil consequence of sin upon the marriage bond lies in this, that a sinful nature keeps even
a Christian husband from fully exercising his headship over his wife in Christlike self-sacrificing
love, and in this, that a sinful nature likewise keeps even a Christian wife from submitting to her
husband in everything in perfect love. Thus even Christian spouses can only approximate the full
blessedness which God had in mind when at creation he originally instituted the marriage
relationship.
然而，罪给婚姻这条纽带所带来的恶果在于：罪性使得基督徒丈夫无法完全用基督一般
牺牲自我的爱去带领她的妻子，同样，罪性也使得基督徒妻子无法在任何一件事上以完全的
爱去顺服她的丈夫。因此，即便是基督徒夫妇，他们也只能是接近那份完全的福气而已，就
是上帝创世之初原本在他所设立的婚姻关系中所想要赐下的那份福气。

On the other hand, note the difference of approach in this interpretation. Gerhard von Rad
marvels at the religious insight of the Israelite narrator, the Jehovist, to whom he ascribes this portion
of the Genesis account. He is of the conviction that this human narrator has sought an answer for
the sorrows, contradictions, and the prevailing degradation in woman’s life. It is not a small matter
in von Rad’s eyes that this narrator has not led these afflictions back to God’s natural order of things;
that lie wholly absolves God’s creation of these troublesome features. It is not a small matter for
von Rad that this writer in his faith has rather led it all back to a primeval offense which is receiving
its consequences, that the writer recognizes in these troubles of womanhood a chastening inflicted
by God.
而另一方面，请注意这段诠释中所采用的不同诠释法。格哈德·冯·拉德对那位以色列
叙述者——耶和华典作者——的宗教观点赞叹不已，并将这部分创世记载的作者身份归给了
他。他相信这位叙述者已经在试图探寻女性生活中充满着悲伤、矛盾以及司空见惯的屈辱这
些现象的根源。但在冯·拉德看来，这位叙述者并未将这些困苦追溯到上帝原本创造的秩序
中来，这样的谎言彻底否定了上帝的造物所面临的这些麻烦事儿——这在冯·拉德的眼里并
非什么小问题。在冯·拉德看来，这位作者反倒以他的信心将这一切归咎为一次最初的过犯，
且这次过犯也得到了它应得的后果，这位作者还认为女性生活的这些困境是上帝对这次过犯
所施加的惩戒——这在冯·拉德的眼里也不是什么小问题。
In other words, according to von Rad, divine revelation comes by way of the deep religious
insights of men of faith. These insights have hard kernels of religious truth, though they still have
aspects and features at the same time that are conditioned by the experiences and the limited
understanding of the particular biblical writer and the specific social problems of his day. Hence
modern man must preserve these insights, yet at the same time bring them up-to-date and make
them fully relevant to his own time and circumstances, to his own understanding. We can still lead
woman’s problems, as far as they are still thought to exist, back to mankind’s estrangement from
God, yet without necessarily operating with a primeval offense.
换句话说，按冯·拉德的说法，上帝的启示源自信徒所持有的深刻宗教观。这些观点包
裹着宗教真理的坚硬核心，但与此同时，它们的一些层面与特征却受到了——由某位特定圣
经作者的经历和有限的认知、这位作者所处时代的特定社会问题——的制约。因此，当代人
必须保留这些观点，但同时也要使其与时俱进，使其与他自己所处的时代和环境、与他自己
的看法完全联系在一起。只要人们认为女性所面临的这些问题依然存在，那我们就还是可以
将这些问题追溯到人类与上帝疏远的这件事上来，只是我们并不一定要谈论什么“最初的过
犯”。
It should be obvious what this kind of interpretation leads to. By carefully studying the text,
you may very clearly apprehend what is being said. Still, you choose what pleases you as being
deep religious insight also for our day. What does not please you, or what no longer seems relevant
for modern man, you discount as a limitation in the understanding of the religious insight of the
biblical writer.
我们应该很清楚这样的诠释法会导致怎样的后果。通过仔细研读圣经文本，你可以清楚

你就将它们贬低为圣经作者有限的宗教观。
Treasuring the certainties of God’s Word, we shall want to continue to take also this portion of

happened. In humble faith we shall want to take to heart what God actually said to Eve, what he is
still saying to all of her daughters, and finally to all of us.

One is struck by the efforts that are made and the ingenuity that is expended in dividing what
very naturally belongs together and in putting into contrast what quite well fits together and merely

interpreter’s presuppositions with which he begins.
In The Study of Old Testament Theology Today (Westwood, NJ: F.H. Revell Co., 1959), p. 26,
Edward J. Young makes a very pertinent point: “All men, insofar as they act in consistency with
their basic presuppositions, reason in a circle. And all men, whether consciously or not, have basic
presuppositions. A man, for example, who starts with the presupposition, whether consciously
adopted or not, that the Bible is not a special revelation of God, will arrive at conclusions which are
consonant with his starting point and basic presuppositions. He is reasoning in a circle.” Then Young
himself quotes R. J. Rushdoony, who also stated this truth very aptly (Ibid., 26): “ ‘All reasoning is
circular reasoning, but reasoning from God is God-given, and God-created data has the validity of
conformity to the nature of things. The opponents of inspiration reason from autonomous man’s
reasons, through brute factuality which has no meaning other than man’s interpretations, back again
to man’s basic presupposition. In other words, all reasoning moves in terms of its basic
presupposition, either God or autonomous man, interpreting all reality in terms of the
presupposition.’
“We need not fear, then, if the charge of reasoning in a circle be raised. It is the only way in
which one can legitimately reason.” It is vital that we keep this truth before our minds. This will let
us realize how important it is that we keep mindful of the presuppositions with which interpreters
begin their work. Interpretive conclusions and judgments which seem strange to us, and often
enough also seem quite unnecessary, will not appear that way, or at least much less so, when we try
to look at the text from the interpreter’s presuppositions. This will then also make us wary of taking
over findings and conclusions which have been obtained from anti-supernaturalistic and
evolutionary presuppositions. We will not fit them into an exposition which proceeds from an
altogether different set of presuppositions, namely those which Scripture itself gives us in asserting
that it is God’s inspired and inerrant Word, given to us through human instruments as God’s special
revelation for our salvation.

Verse 22 starts out with a protasis: “Behold, the man is become like one of us with respect to
the knowing of good and evil; and now lest he stretch out his hand and take also of the tree of life
and eat and live forever.…” We now expect an apodosis such as this to follow: “I will therefore
drive him forth out of the garden.” Instead, Moses employs an anacoluthon. He passes over God’s
statement of purpose and immediately reports God’s action. This literary device of an anacoluthon
is very effective here. We don’t need to be told what God decided to do. To know what God actually
did is fully sufficient. In order that the tragic possibility mentioned by God might by no means come
to pass, God acted; God banished man from the Garden of Eden.

Through sin, mankind had forfeited the blissful, restful fellowship with God which had so far
marked life in the Garden of Eden. It would have been futile to have tried to regain it by eating of
the tree of life. Quite a different kind of life would have been perpetuated by such an act. The garden
was no longer for man. From now on man’s earthly life was to be spent outside of the garden, to be
spent tilling the ground from which he was taken. What this would mean we heard as we considered
God’s words to Eve and to Adam (Ge 3:16–19). It would be a life of wearisome toil, and of much
sorrow and distress, ending in physical death.

How long did the cherubim and the flaming sword guard the entrance of the garden? How long
did the garden itself continue to exist? God’s Word is silent concerning both questions. Hence any
speculation on our part is futile. As always, when Scripture is silent, our attention is directed
elsewhere.
基路伯与火剑在这园子的入口处守护了多长时间呢？这园子本身又继续存在了多久呢？
神的话语并未回答这两个问题。因此，我们的任何猜想都不过是枉然。当圣经对某事保持沉
默的时候，它也会一如既往地将我们的注意力引向别处。
What is impressed upon us is the truth that the joy and glory of the garden is no longer meant
for the earthly life of sinful man. Even Adam and Eve seemed to have had difficulty in accepting
this truth and in submitting to it humbly and willingly. For we are not merely told that God

By his nature, man since the fall remains an incurable millennialist. Sinful man tenaciously
clings to the thought that paradisaic bliss and glory may after all be attainable in this life. Though a
child of sin and death, man constantly deceives himself into thinking that his malady is after all
nothing more than a question of proper environment. Man hopes to reenter Paradise by creating a
paradisaic environment for himself by finding better housing, better schooling, better recreation; by
improving ecology; through distributing wealth, through prosperity, and through an abundance of
material possessions; by making progress in banishing ignorance, poverty, and disease. He even
makes heroic efforts to prolong life through transplanting vital organs.
自人类堕落以后，人的本性就染上了一种无法治愈的千禧年主义。罪人坚持不懈地以为，
他总可在今生获得伊甸园中的福乐与荣耀。尽管人类成了罪恶与死亡的儿女，但他却总是自
欺欺人地以为，他的疾病其实不过是环境问题所致。人类希望重返伊甸园，为此，他们尽心
竭力地营造一种如同伊甸园般的生活环境，包括优化住房条件、提升教育水平、丰富娱乐活
动；改善生态环境；分配财富、实现繁荣与丰富的物质财产；逐渐消除无知、贫困和疾病。
他们甚至不惜采取英勇无比的手段，通过移植一些重要的器官来延长寿命。
None of these things mentioned are, of course, wrong in themselves. They can all be welcomed
and received with heartfelt thanksgiving as earthly blessings of God. What is wrong, and at the same
time vain and futile, is man’s feverish effort and hope of again turning this earthly life into a paradise.
What is wrong is when sinful man forgets that ever since the fall, the main function of this earthly
life is to serve as a time of grace. Amid the toil, sorrow, pain, and frustrations during this earthly life
that are constant reminders of his sin and its curses, man above all needs to take refuge in faith
through God’s saving grace that is held out to him as a free gift in the gospel message.
当然，上述这些事情本身并没有什么不对的地方。它们可以作为上帝赐给我们在这世上
的祝福被我们欣然且由衷地接受。其错误且虚妄之处在于，人类狂热地盼望着、又竭力地要
将地上的生活再次变回伊甸园的生活。错误之处在于，罪人忘记了自堕落以来，这地上生活
的主要功能就是用作人类蒙恩的时机。世间生活充满着不止歇的劳苦愁烦、痛苦哀伤，这些
都不断地提醒着人类自己的罪以及罪的咒诅，人类最需要的并非上述这些东西，而是藉上帝
所赐的救恩、在信心之中躲避上帝的忿怒，而这救恩就是福音向人陈明的一份白白的礼物。
