# 创世记 1:1–2:3 注释

创世记 1:1–2:3
Genesis, like all of the Holy Scriptures, is the inspired and inerrant Word of God. It takes us
back to the very beginning of time and touches upon matters that inevitably arouse our human
curiosity. There is nothing wrong about curiosity in itself. It is a God-given human trait, useful in
many ways. As we study Genesis, it is well, however, to remember that God did not give us the
Holy Scriptures, or any part of them, merely to satisfy our intellectual curiosity. On the contrary, he
gave them to us for the express purpose of making us wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus
and rendering us blessed, rich, and fruitful in such faith (2 Ti 3:15–17). As we study Genesis, we
will therefore concentrate our spiritual curiosity upon those things concerning the very beginnings,
which God has revealed to us for this purpose.
与圣经其余书卷一样，创世记也是神所默示的无误之言。它带我们回到时间的开端，也
会触碰那些难免引起我们好奇心的问题。好奇心本身并没有什么错，它是上帝赐给人类的特
征，且多有益处。然而，当我们学习创世记的时候，我们最好还是记住，上帝赐给我们整本
圣经，或说其中任何一卷，都不仅仅是为了满足我们的求知欲而已。相反，他将圣经赐给我
们，特要叫我们藉信耶稣基督有得救的智慧，又要叫我们在这等信心中蒙福、富足与丰盛（提
后 3:15-17）。因此，当我们学习创世记的时候，我们将会把我们的属灵好奇心聚焦在创世
之初的那些事儿上，而上帝也正是为了以上目的才将那些事启示给了我们。
As we begin the study of the opening chapter of Genesis, we will not expect the chapter to
answer every question concerning the subject matter of creation, which it treats. Whatever the
creation account does clearly assert, a Christian will note thankfully. He will reverently treat it as
historical fact, once it is evident that it is presented as such. With the same reverence, he will cling
to it in spite of every contradiction and attack that may be raised against it. He will do so with the
assumption of faith that whatever God revealed to us he somehow deemed useful for our salvation.
当我们开始学习创世记的开篇章节时，我们不用盼着该章节将会就它所讨论的关于创造
这一议题的所有问题作出回答。创世记载所清楚断言之事无论为何，基督徒都将满怀感恩且
铭记于心。某段记载若是明确将自己呈现为历史事实，他就当恭敬地将之视为历史事实。尽
管矛盾以及反对声或将起来攻击它，他都当恭敬地紧紧依附于它。他要带着信心这一前设如
此行，相信上帝无论启示给我们什么，他总是以某种方式为着我们得救的益处而启示给我们
的。
Bearing the purpose of Scripture in mind, the believer will never think of criticizing the creation
account for the many questions concerning the momentous event that it still leaves unanswered. He
will remember that the creation account is not a piece of revelation given just for its own sake. It
has its place at the very beginning of Genesis as a vital introduction to the specific message of
Genesis, the inspired opening section of the Pentateuch. This message is the beginning history of
God’s grace, God’s saving activity. Genesis relates how almost from the very beginning, as soon as
mankind fell into sin, God in his free and faithful grace has been active in behalf of our salvation.
The creation account gives us the vital background against which God wants us to see his amazing
saving grace toward his fallen creatures.
当信徒将上帝启示圣经的这一目的牢记于心的时候，他也将绝不会因一些重大事件尚存
不少疑问而对创世记载指指点点。他将牢记，创世记载并非只是为启示而启示。它在创世记

的开头有其重要地位，它是对于创世记——神所默示的摩西五经中的第一册书——这一特别
信息的重要引言。它是上帝的恩典及其救恩作为的历史起点。几乎从一开始，也就是人类刚
刚犯罪堕落之后，创世记便向我们讲述了上帝如何为着我们的救恩以他白白且又信实的恩典
积极展开行动。上帝想要我们看见他对他那些堕落的创造物所施的奇妙救恩，而创世记载则
为我们就这一点提供了至关重要的背景。
We will notice that in the 34 verses, Genesis 1:1–2:3, which constitute the creation account,
some things are presented in considerable detail, some things are even repeated again and again,
while other matters, for which we may have looked, are passed over altogether in silence. We will
want to note what is emphasized in the creation account as proper background for the message of
Genesis.
在由创世记 1:1–2:3 的 34 节经文所构成创世记载中，我们将会发现，摩西将其中一些事
描述得极为详细，甚至还不断重复一些事。至于另一些事，我们也可能已经注意到了，摩西
却悄无声息地完全忽略了它们。既然创世记载是整册创世记的专属背景，那我们就当留心其
中哪些才是重要之事。
1:1

‫בְ ֵראשִ ית בָ ָרא אֱֹלהִ ים אֵ ת הַ שָ מַ יִ ם וְ אֵ ת הָ אָ ֶרץ‬1
1In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.

1 起初神创造天地。
With the Masoretic Hebrew text, verse 1 should be taken as an independent sentence. This
analysis has, without exception, the support of all the ancient versions. As an independent sentence,
verse 1 is, however, not to be understood as a mere heading, nor is it to be taken as an initial sentence
summarizing the whole creative process narrated in this chapter. The waw with which verse two
begins rules out both of these interpretations. This waw and the descriptions of the earth which it
introduces show that, as a separate sentence, verse 1 sets forth God’s initial creative act by which
he brought heaven and earth into existence. The two constructions which take verse 1 to be a
subordinate temporal clause and then consider either verse 2 or verse 3 to be the main clause are not
acceptable. We will have more to say about this after having expounded the first two verses.
就马所拉希伯来文本而言，第 1 节当被视为独立句。这种解析毫无例外地得到了所有古
代圣经版本的支持。然而，第 1 节虽是独立句，却不应仅被视为某个标题，也不应被视为始
发句，像是对本章所叙述的整个创世过程做个总述似的。第 2 节开头的 waw 否定了这两项
诠释。这里的 waw 以及此词所引荐的随后对于大地的描述都表明，第 1 节作为独立句开启
了那由上帝所发动的创世之举，藉此他叫天地成形。将第 1 节视为从属时间状语从句，然后
将第 2 节或第 3 节视为主句，这两种句法结构都不可容忍。当我们阐释完前两节之后，我们
会回过头来再作说明。
Every word of this first verse deserves our thorough attention. This verse is not the normal
sentence of Hebrew narrative, which ordinarily begins with the verb. Of the next 30 sentences, only
two do not begin with the verb. In this opening sentence, the adverbial phrase ‫ בְ ֵראשִ ית‬is in the
emphatic position. As to form, ‫ ֵראשִ ית‬could be either in the construct or absolute state. Since
there is nothing in its form or in its context in this independent sentence to establish it as a construct,
it is to be taken as being in the absolute state. The Masoretes indicated this as their judgment by

punctuating ‫ בְ ֵראשִ ית‬with a tiphha, a disjunctive accent. For good reason they did not point the
inseparable preposition with the vowel of the article. Terms like ‫ ֵראשִ ית‬and ‫רֹ אֹ ש‬, beginning;

‫קֶ דֶ ם‬, olden times; and ‫עֹולָם‬, eternity, when used in adverbial phrases, generally occur without
the article and in the absolute state (compare Isa 46:10; 40:21; 41:4, 26; Pr 8:23).1 Thus the LXX
translates the Hebrew as ἐν ἀρχῇ, and this has gone over into the New Testament (Jn 1:1), which
speaks of the same absolute beginning. It refers to the beginning of all matter, all space, and all time.
By an exercise of his will, God brought heaven and earth into existence in its initial essential form.
这节经文中的每个词都值得我们全神贯注。这节经文并未采用常见的希伯来语叙述方式，
也就是说，这句经文并未按常见的方式以动词开头。在接下去的 30 句经文中，只有两句经
文没有以动词开头。在这句开篇经文中，副词短语‫ בְ ֵראשִ ית‬位于句首，起到强调的作用。
至于词形，‫ ֵּראשִ ית‬既可以是附属形，也可以是绝对形。由于在这个独立句中，它的词形和
语境中都没有任何线索可以让我们将它确立为附属形，所以我们还是将它视为绝对形。马所
拉文士在‫בְ ֵראשִ ית‬下面标注了分离性重音符号 tiphha，以此表明了他们也是作此判断。另外，
他们并未在该短语开头紧附的介词下方标注定冠词的元音。他们有充分的理由这么做，因为

‫ בָ ָרא‬is the verb used to describe this momentous act of God. In contrast to ‫עָשָ ה‬, likewise
used in the creation account, ‫ בָ ָרא‬is a very specialized verb. ‫ עָשָ ה‬is a very common, colorless
vocable used for any and every kind of making, whether by God or man. In contrast, ‫ בָ ָרא‬is used
in the Old Testament only for divine creative activity. God is exclusively the subject. It always
expresses the idea of producing something new and extraordinary, something epoch-making.2 It
implies effortless production by word and volition, as only the almighty God can do it. These are
the features which are very prominent in this verse and in the other two instances in the creation
account in which the verb is used. It is used in verse 21, where we are told that God created the first
living creatures which moved about by their own volition; and it is used four times in verses 27 and
28 to inform us that God made man in his own image as the very crown of his creation.

‫בָ ָרא‬一词用以描绘上帝的这一重要行动。相比于同样出现在创世记载中的‫עָשָ ה‬一词，
‫בָ ָרא‬则是一个专用动词。‫עָשָ ה‬一词极为常见与苍白，用以表示任何一种制作的行为，既适
用于上帝，也适用于人类。与之相对，‫בָ ָרא‬一词在旧约中则仅用来表示属神的创造之举，其
主语皆为上帝，无一例外。它总是在传达着某种关于产出某些崭新、非凡以及具划时代意义
事物的概念。2 它意味着毫不费力地使用话语和意志产出某些事物，也唯独全能的上帝可以
如此行。这就是这节经文的显著特征，而在创世记载中另两处使用该动词的例子中，情况亦
是如此。在使用该动词的第 21 节中，我们得知上帝造了第一批可以按它们自己意志四处走
动的生物；而第 27、28 节共四次使用了该动词，告诉我们上帝按他自己的形像造了人，也
就是他的造物之首。

That God is the subject of the first verse of Genesis has been repeatedly noted. What still calls
for comment is the name, ‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬, used for God here and throughout the creation account—31
times in all. According to the Hebrew lexicon of Ludwig Koehler, ‫ אֱֹלהִ ים‬occurs 2,550 times in
the Old Testament. Since there is only one true and living God, the name really belongs to him alone.

Where this thought is in the foreground, as in the creation account, ‫ אֱֹלהִ ים‬is used as a proper
noun and has no need of an article. ‫ אֱֹלהִ ים‬is, however, also used for idols made by human hands
and minds. Though they do not exist, except in human minds and hearts, they do affect human lives
and actions. For that reason the Old Testament takes note of them. In direct or indirect contrast to
such vain idols, the one true and living God is then frequently designated as ‫הָ אֱֹלהִ ים‬, with an
article.
上帝是创世记第 1 节的主语，
这点已被多次指出。另外值得我们一提的就是这里的名字，

‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬，此处乃至整段创世记载都用此词来称呼上帝——总共 31 次。根据路德维希·克勒
编撰的希伯来语词典，‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬一词在旧约中共出现了 2550 次。既然世间只有一位真正的永
生神，那么这名字就实在唯独属于他。此概念在创世记载各处都尤为凸显，而‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬一词也
被用作了专有名词，并不需要定冠词加以限制。然而，‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬一词也被用来称呼人手及人脑
所造的那些偶像。尽管它们并不存在，不过存在于人自己的脑中与心中而已，但它们也确实
影响着人类的生活和行动。旧约也注意到了这一问题。故而圣经以直接或间接的方式将那位
独一真神与那些虚无的偶像对立起来，并时常加上定冠词，以‫הָ אֱֹלהִ ים‬称呼前者。

‫ אֱֹלהִ ים‬has been derived from a Semitic verb root ‫אָ לַּה‬, used in Arabic but not in biblical
Hebrew, meaning “to fear, to reverence.” Others derive it, as they do ‫אֵ ל‬, from the root ‫אּול‬, “to
be mighty.” ‫ אֱֹלהִ ים‬is the one who because of his eternal being, his transcendent attributes, and
his marvelous works should inevitably arouse man’s awe and reverence. It is a fitting name for God
in this opening verse, in which he reveals himself to us as the only one who is eternal, the one who
brought heaven and earth into existence out of nothing by a mere act of his volition. It is an
appropriate name of God for this entire first chapter of Holy Scripture, which records his creative
works of divine omnipotence, wisdom, and benevolence.

‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬一词源自闪语词根‫אָ לַּה‬，后者用于阿拉伯语中，而非圣经希伯来语中，意为“害
怕，敬畏”。另有些人从词根‫אּול‬中提取此词，该词根意为“大能的”，这与他们对待‫אֵ ל‬一
词的方式如出一辙。‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬因其永恒、超凡的属性及其奇妙作为理当叫人心生敬畏。圣经首
节经文使用此词来称呼上帝再合适不过了，在这里，他将他自己——那位仅凭他自己的意志
就叫天地形成于虚无之中的独一永在者——启示给我们。圣经第一章既然谱写了上帝全能、
智慧与仁慈的创造之工，那么这整章使用此词来称呼上帝同样恰到好处。

Old Testament. Forty of these instances are, however, in the unique book of Job. Yet the plural form

‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬, when designating the only true God, with but few exceptions4 is construed with a singular
verb and with singular modifiers, as it is in this verse. The plural has been called a plural of majesty
and also a potential plural, setting forth the vast potentialities of the Godhead, including the mystery
of the Holy Trinity. With R. B. Girdlestone, Synonyms of the Old Testament, we will be ready to say:
“There is certainly nothing unreasonable in the supposition that the name of the Deity was given to
man in this form, so as to prepare him for the truth that in the unity of the Godhead there are three
persons.” What will make us the more ready to say this is the fact that, as we shall see, the creation
account itself in a number of instances hints at the multiplicity of persons in the unity of the

God’s revelation of himself and of his gift of salvation is progressive. This is, of course, not
true, though often erroneously asserted, in the sense that earlier revelation is still faulty and defective
and thus needs to be superseded and corrected by later revelations. God’s revelation is progressive
in the sense that it did not immediately supply all the details but rather supplied them gradually as
there was distinct need and purpose for them. This applies particularly also to the mystery of God’s
being, that in his one divine essence there are three distinct persons. These truths will always be
beyond the grasp of our human reason and remain incomprehensible mysteries for us. God has
therefore revealed them to us only because his gift of salvation is inextricably bound up with these
truths, rests on them, and cannot be fully known to us or adequately appropriated by us apart from
these truths. As God therefore gradually unfolded and finally completed his gift of salvation through
the life, death, and resurrection of his incarnate Son, he gradually revealed more and more
concerning the mysteries of his divine being. What we want to note, however, is that the statements
of God’s earlier revelation always adequately allow for the fuller revelation when it was made. The
earlier statement need not be discarded, overhauled, or corrected.

第 2 节的结尾如下：“神的灵翱翔在水面上。”‫עַל־פְ נֵי הַ מָ יִ ם‬仍指起初那些覆盖大地的

这个希伯来词既可意为“风”，又可意为“灵”，这点已被公认。因此，其词义必须视具体

的专用名。确定的属格会让整个复合概念成为确定的概念。因此，诸如“神的一个灵”或者
“一个神圣的灵”这样的译法就都被排除在外了。在希伯来语中，尽管带属格的名词结构通
常会取代英语中的名词和形容词，但这种情况也只有在属格不确定的情况下才有可能成立。
这样一来，“（那个）神的灵”就成了唯一正确的译法。我们可以说这里指的就是圣灵。然
而，我们无法因此就宣称创世记第 2 节早已向我们清楚传达了以下真理，即神的灵是神格之
中的一个独立位格。但是，我们已经拥有新约所启示的、关乎三位一体的完整真理，所以我
们能够也应该晓得，这里提到的圣灵正积极参与在上帝的创世活动之中。
That the Spirit of God spoken of here is indeed a person is emphasized by the text itself, namely
by the verbal action ascribed to him through ‫מְ ַרחֶ פֶת‬, the Piel active participle of ‫ ָרחַ ף‬. This verb
occurs in only two other Old Testament passages, Jeremiah 23:9 and Deuteronomy 32:11. In the
first of these passages, Jeremiah uses it in a Qal form to express the shaking or trembling of his
bones which he experiences in his agitation over the judgment that threatens his countrymen because
of their false prophets. This does not offer too much enlightenment for discerning its meaning here.
The other passage is more helpful. There Moses himself uses ‫ ָרחַ ף‬, likewise a Piel form, in
carrying out the vivid picture of the mother eagle who high up on a mountain crag is giving her
young their first lesson in flying. She pushes the young eagles out of her nest and then hovers
protectively over them, carefully watching for the first sign of exhaustion in her fledglings. This
will cause her to swoop beneath them so that they may rest on her outspread wings and catch their
breath for further efforts at flying.

covered by an abyss of waters; but the Spirit of God was hovering protectively over this earth. He
was active in preserving it. At the same time he was ready to be active also as the Lord and Giver
of life when subsequent divine creative acts would bring to the earth the order and all the forms of
life which God intended it to have. All was in God’s hands; all was under the control of the Spirit

chaotic, we do so only in an attempt to give a brief description of the divinely intended first stage
in the formation of the perfectly ordered earth of which we are told in verse 31.

except those within the Godhead (the opera ad intra) are common to all three persons—Father, Son,
and Holy Spirit. Psalm 33:6, for example, does not speak of a creative activity of the Spirit which
was preparatory to that of the creative Word of God, but of one that occurred in one and the same
creative act. All three persons are active in all the divine works which relate to the world outside of
God because there is only one divine essence. This Scripture truth is concisely expressed in the wellknown Augustinian formula: Opera ad extra sunt indivisa vel communia.

So far we have occupied ourselves with the exposition of the first two verses of Genesis. In
doing so we take them to be two independent sentences. This analysis is followed by all the ancient
versions and is indicated by the accentuation of the Masoretic Hebrew text, and we see no good
reason to depart from it. This analysis does justice to the text, fits into the whole structure and
progression of the creation account, and is in agreement with what the rest of Scripture states
concerning God’s creative activity. It is the most natural analysis. It was followed by Luther in his
German Bible, by the English Authorized Version (AV), and is still endorsed by the majority of
translations and commentaries.
到目前为止，我们已经花了大量精力来诠释创世记的前两节经文。我们将它们视为两个
独立句。所有的古代圣经版本都采用了这一分析法，并且，马所拉希伯来文本中的重音符号
也向我们如此表明，所以我们并没有足够的理由与此分道扬镳。这种分析法对文本处理得当，
与创世记载的整体结构与进程相符，且与圣经其余经文就上帝创世作为所陈明的内容亦彼此
契合。这样的分析法最为自然。路德的德语圣经和英语钦定本都遵循这种分析法，且多数译
本和注释书亦复如是。

“When God began to form the universe, the world was void and vacant, darkness lay upon the abyss;
but the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters. God said, ‘Let there be light,’ and there was
light.”14

ַ‫ ָרקִ יע‬is a noun formation from the verb root ‫ ָרקַ ע‬, meaning “to spread out.” In Exodus 39:3

expressions used in passages like Psalm 78:23; Genesis 7:11; and 2 Kings 7:2 speak of the doors
and windows of heaven and in 2 Samuel 22:8 speak of the foundations of heaven shaking. Some
have therefore argued that the Old Testament depicts the ַ‫ ָרקִ יע‬as a kind of solid dome. The LXX
rendering στερέωμα and that of the Vulgate firmamentum (adapted as “firmament” in the AV), both
of which mean “prop, support,” tend to support this interpretation. It is, however, an unwarranted
exegetical conclusion drawn from a specialized verb usage and from figurative expressions.

Commentaries do generally agree that what is meant by the expanse, the ַ‫ ָרקִ יע‬, is the sky, the
blue vault of heaven above us. The ַ‫ ָרקִ יע‬includes not merely the lower heavens, or atmospheric
sky, but the whole visible expanse up to and including the region of the fixed stars. We are told that
on the fourth day God placed the sun, moon, and stars in the expanse of heaven. In the account of
the fifth day we hear that the birds are to fly upon the face of the expanse of heaven. The creation
account speaks of the various aspects of the expanse in much the same way as we speak of the sky
in different senses.

Verse 7 tells us that God made the expanse and that he separated the water under the expanse
from the water above it. In differentiating these waters, the Hebrew in each case uses three
prepositions speaking of waters, ַ‫ מִ ּתַ חַ ת לָרָ קִ יע‬and ‫מֵ עַל לָרָ קִ יע‬. Idiomatic English cannot
reproduce the precision effected by these multiple Hebrew prepositions. It must in each case content
itself with one: under or above. Verse 7 is not to be understood as though God’s creative fiat of itself
could not and did not suffice to bring the expanse into existence or to effect the separation of waters.
That would contradict Psalm 33:9 and Hebrews 11:3. Rather, the purpose of verse 7 is to emphasize
and to unfold in greater detail that God’s fiat enlisted the full exercise of God’s omnipotence and of
his infinite wisdom in bringing about what it had decreed. Thus the verse ends with the statement:

‫וַיְ הִ י־כֵן‬, “and it was so.” By a creative act through God’s Word, the expanse now existed and the
separation of waters was in effect.

ַ‫。לָרָ קִ יע‬这些希伯来介词所传达的精确含义难以用中文习语表达清楚。因此，中文分别只能
采用一个介词来表达：以下和以上。我们不应认为第 7 节表明上帝的创世口谕本身不能也不
足以造出天空或叫水分开，这等见解与诗篇 33:9 和希伯来书 11:3 互相矛盾。相反，第 7 节
旨在强调并更为详细地揭示——上帝命立就立，他的口谕充分展现了他的全能及其无限的智

that time” and “the present heavens and earth.” Water deluged the former world and destroyed it.

The second part of verse 14 indicates a second purpose of the heavenly lights. They are to serve
as signs both for seasons and for days and years. We have become quite familiar with the very literal
translation of the King James Version: “And let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days,
and years.” The preposition ‫ ל‬is rendered the same way in all three instances that it occurs in the
clause. Thus four individual purposes are enumerated, the last two joined together by a copula. This
is basically the analysis followed by many other versions, including the LXX, Vulgate, RSV, and
NASB. It is also espoused by many commentators, including C. F. Keil, J. P. Lange, F. Delitzsch,
H. C. Leupold. As they interpret how the heavenly luminaries are for signs, for seasons, “for days
and years,” it becomes evident, however, that the preposition ‫ל‬, translated “for,” must be
understood in a variety of disparate ways. This difficulty is avoided in recognizing that ‫ ל‬in the
first combination of ‫ הָ יָה ל‬here has the idiomatic meaning of “to serve as.” It has that meaning
also in Genesis 1:29 and 6:21. Then the preposition ‫ ל‬can have the meaning “for” in the other two
occurrences, being used to tell us what the things are which the heavenly lights are to indicate as
signs. The preposition ‫ ל‬is in both of these instances prefixed by a correlative waw. This analysis
lets us translate verse 14b: “And let them serve as signs, both for seasons as well as for days and
years.” This understanding of the text is reflected in the NIV and advocated in the Genesis
commentaries of J. C. Tuch, Eduard Koenig, Umberto Cassuto, and H. G. Stigers. Ever since the
fourth day, the heavenly lights, sun, moon, and stellar constellations have continued to establish
calendars. They have fixed and indicated days, appointed times (both sacred and secular), months,
seasons, and years, solar as well as lunar.

borne out by the more descriptive designation ‫ ֶרמֶ ש הָ אֲדָ מָ ה‬in verse 25. There, as the execution
of God’s first creative act on the sixth day is related, all three classes of land animals are again
enumerated, though not in the same order.

word, the dry land did bring forth living creatures in abundance, not only of the three classes
mentioned, but also of an unspecified number of kinds within these classes. On the sixth day, God
not only made all of these first land animals from the ground but also programmed each of these
creatures to reproduce offspring according to its kind. Since the offspring of two mates can embody
various combinations of the genetic factors (size, color, shape, and other features) belonging to a
kind, this allowed for considerable variety within the kinds of land animals. It did not allow for new
kinds or mixtures of kinds. Modern study of genetics, which began with Gregor Mendel,
underscores these truths. Mutations, due to damage of reproductive cells, are slight and regressive
and do not develop new kinds. “And God saw that it was good.” The land animals, like everything
previously created, were also good in God’s judgment when he made them. So were the kinds which
he established as boundaries for their reproduction until the end of time. Neither needed to become
good gradually by a long process of evolution.

That human beings would be very close to God’s heart is indicated by the very special and
solemn manner in which God approached their creation. Verse 26 stands in clear contrast to verses
3, 6, 9, 14, 20, and 24. In all of these other creative acts, God merely spoke and it was so. Here we
have no creative command at all, but a solemn exhortation instead: “Let us make man in our image,
after our likeness.…” The great moment had now come to bring that creature, for whom everything
else had been made in preparation, into existence.
上帝以他独特又庄重的方式对待人类这一受造物，从这点我们就足以看出他对人类的重
视。第 26 节与第 3、6、9、14、20、24 节经文形成了鲜明对照，当上帝施展其余所有创世
作为的时候，他仅仅发令，随后事就那样成了。但在这里，我们却根本没有看到什么创世指
令，而是一番庄重的彼此劝勉：“让我们照着我们的形像、按着我们的样式造人吧......”。
为人类所造的其它一切都已准备就绪，现在，人类受造的这一重要时刻终于来到了。
The plural verb ‫נַ עֲשֶ ה‬, let us make, and the plural possessive pronominal suffixes in ‫בְ צַ לְ מֵ נּו‬,
in our image, and ‫כִ דְ מּותֵ נּו‬, in our likeness, can be satisfactorily accounted for only when they are
recognized as shedding light on the mystery of the Holy Trinity. Father, Son, and Holy Spirit were
engaged in mutual, joyful consultation as God called into existence the creature upon whom he had
from all eternity determined to lavish his love and fellowship. This understanding cannot appear

forced to anyone who already has the benefit of God’s New Testament revelation and who
approaches all of Scripture as God’s inspired and inerrant Word with a unified message. We are
mindful that in the creation account, we have previously met with foreshadowing of the great
mystery of God’s being, both of the truth of the unity of the Godhead and of the plurality of persons.
We encountered such foreshadowing in the opening verse of Genesis, in the plural form of the divine
name ‫אֱֹלהִ ים‬, and again in verse 2, where we saw the Spirit of God acting as a distinct divine
person. We are not saying, of course, that the truths of the Holy Trinity are explicitly taught in any
of these instances, nor are we passing any judgment on how much Old Testament saints were already
able to learn from these instances about the mystery of God’s being.

没有什么着落了。上帝在永恒之中便已决定将他的爱与相交慷慨地施与人类这一受造，而当
他发令创造他们之时，父、子和圣灵则一起满心喜乐地商议着此事，共同参与在其中。读者
若是早已受益于上帝的新约启示，视圣经所有内容为神所默示的无误之言，且以为其中所传
递的信息前后一致，那么以上说法对他们而言并不会显得多么牵强附会。我们想到了创世记

我们也无法判断旧约时代的圣徒从这些例子中到底习得了多少关乎上帝存在本身的奥秘。
On the other hand, the interpretation that in verse 26 God is merely speaking of himself in terms
of a majestic plural has no analogy elsewhere in the Old Testament to support it. The other suggested
interpretation that God consulted with the holy angels in his determination to create mankind is
definitely ruled out by the two plural pronominal suffixes which have been previously discussed.
Scripture knows nothing of man also being created in the image of the holy angels.
另有一些人宣称，在第 26 节中，上帝不过是在用威荣类的复数形式称呼自己，这样的
说法没有类似的旧约经文可以给予支持。还有一些人则认为，这里上帝是在与圣天使一同商
议决定创造人类的相关事宜，但这样的说法必须被排除在外，前文提到的两个复数代词后缀
便是明证，圣经从未有过上帝按圣天使的形像创造人类的这般无稽之谈。
The preeminence of mankind in God’s creation is explicitly established by the unrestricted
dominion over the earth and all that it contained, which God assigned to the first human beings,

‫הָ אָ דָ ם‬, whom he created male and female. This all-inclusive dominion is announced twice in
majestic, sweeping terms. It is introduced first with the jussive, ‫וְ יִ ְרדּו‬, and then again, in
connection with the divine blessing, by the two imperatives, ָ‫ וְ כִ בְ שֻ ה‬and ‫ְ דּו‬
‫ּור‬. They, the human
beings created male and female, were to subdue the earth and rule over its other creatures. All of the
earth’s powers and resources—physical, chemical, electrical, and atomic—were placed under their
jurisdiction. This dominion has not been withdrawn from man. It has, however, been greatly

modified by man’s fall into sin. Sinful depravity now causes mankind to abuse and misuse and
exploit this God-given dominion. Moreover, as St. Paul says in Romans 8:19–21, God himself, upon
the entrance of sin, made the whole creature world subject to frustration and placed it under the
bondage of corruption. God’s purpose in this will be discussed in the exposition of the third chapter
of Genesis. Yet this much can be said here: Man’s dominion continues; even since the fall, the earth
and its creatures have no purposes or destinies of their own but are still to serve the needs of man
and the purposes and goals set for man.
人类在神的所有创造之中占据首位，这点从他赋予首对男女的治理范围就可以看出——
全地以及其中所有都在他们的管理之下。上帝的两次措辞威严且具囊括性，向我们清楚陈明
了人类所拥有的全方位治理权。我们首先看到的是一个祈愿式——‫וְ יִ ְרדּו‬，随后又是两个与
神的祝福相联的祈使式—— ָ‫וְ כִ בְ שֻ ה‬和‫ְ דּו‬
‫。ּור‬他们，受造的男人与女人，要治理这地，管理
这地其它所有的受造物。地上所有的能量与资源——物理的、化学的、电子的、原子的——
都在他们的管辖范围之内。即便如今，上帝也并未将这样的治理权从人类手中夺走，只是这
种权柄因人类的堕落犯罪而发生了巨大的改变。人类罪恶的堕落导致他们滥用、误用又利用
上帝所赐的这种治理权。此外，正如圣保罗在罗马书 8:19-21 中所说的，当罪进入世界之后，
上帝便叫所有受造之物都服在虚空之下并受到败坏的辖制。至于上帝为何这样做，我们将在
第三章另作解释。但此时我们仍可以说：人类的治理权依然属于他们；即便自人类堕落以来，
大地以及其中的受造物全都漫无目的地服在虚空之下，但它们仍将服务于人类的需求，服务
于那些为人类所设定的目的与目标。
We have repeatedly heard that the earth, its vegetation, and all its creatures, which God now
placed under man’s dominion, were in God’s judgment good in the fullest sense. We are not told
that God made them foolproof. He did not need to do that because of the special way in which God
equipped man for his position of dominion. God made man in his own image. God said, “Let us
make man in our image, after our likeness, and let them rule.…” One could also translate “in order
that they may rule.” The jussive ‫ וְ יִ ְרדּו‬with a simple waw following the cohortative ‫ נַ עֲשֶ ה‬in a
way indicates intention, whether we explicitly express that in the translation or not (cf. GK 109f.).
As long as man bore the image of God, there was no danger that he would misuse or abuse God’s
creation. The fact that man was made in the image of God is the greatest evidence of his preeminence
in God’s creation.
当上帝创造大地、植被与动物的时候，“神看着是好的”这句话总是不绝于耳，然而论
到管理这一切的人类，我们却反而没有听到上帝造他们万无一失的说法。摩西并不需要写下
这句话，因为上帝以独特的方式将人类放在治理全地的位置之上，即，他用他自己的形像造
了他们。神说，“让我们照着我们的形像、按着我们的样式造人吧，让他们管理......”，或
者我们可以像和合本那样将最后一句译为“使他们管理......”。无论我们是否将和合本所传
达的“目的”清晰地体现在译文之中，伴随普通 waw 的祈愿式‫ וְ יִ ְרדּו‬若是跟在鼓励式‫נַ עֲשֶ ה‬
之后，它就可以在一定程度上表示某种意图（GK109f）。只要人类拥有神的形像，那他就不
会误用或滥用上帝的创造物。因此，人类按神的形像受造这一点就是他在神的所有受造之中
占据首位的最大证据。

A creature made in the image of God, made after God’s likeness, was, and still is, something
wonderful, extraordinary, epoch making. Therefore, three times Moses used ‫ בָ ָרא‬as the fitting
word for reporting the divine act by which such a creature was called into existence. “So God created
man in his own image, in the image of God he created him; male and female he created them.” Both
man and woman were created in the image of God.
人类照着神的形像、按着神的样式受造，这是一件美妙、非凡且具划时代意义的事，过
去是，现在依然是。因此，摩西连续三次使用了‫בָ ָרא‬这个动词来描述上帝创造人类的作为。
“神就照着自己的形像造人，就是照着神的形像创造了他；他所创造的有男有女。”男人和
女人都是按神的形像所造的。
As God expressed his intention to create man, he used two terms: ‫ צֶ לֶם‬and ‫דְ מּות‬. Often
when we describe something very important we use more than one term for various reasons. We
may use two terms because one term alone does not cover all the features of the important matter
under discussion. A second term becomes necessary to express additional features. At other times,
several synonyms are used in referring to something important merely to stress and give emphasis
to the matter involved. Here the latter reason for using two terms seems to apply. For, in stating the
fact twice in verse 27 that God did create man in his own image, Moses used only the term ‫צֶ לֶם‬.
In 5:1, where this truth is again stated, he used the other term, ‫דְ מּות‬. Either term seems to cover
the truth fully. Whatever distinctive connotation either term may therefore have in other biblical
contexts need not be considered here.
当上帝发出创造人类的指令时，他用了两个词：‫צֶ לֶם‬和‫。דְ מּות‬出于各种不同的理由，
我们通常会使用不止一个词汇来描绘某些重要事物。我们可能会因为一个词不足以刻画被讨
论事物的所有特征而选择使用两个词，如此一来，第二个词便可用来表明该事物的其余特征。
而在另外一些情况下，我们则会使用多个同义词来描绘某些重要事物，其目的只为强调。此
处，第二种理由似乎更为贴切。理由是，在第 27 节中，为了再次表明上帝确实按照他的形
像造了人类，摩西仅仅使用了‫צֶ לֶם‬这个词；而在 5:1 中，当他又一次陈明这一真理的时候，
他却使用了另一个词，即‫。דְ מּות‬这样看来，它们中的任何一个词似乎都能完整地传达这一
真理。因此，无论它们在其它圣经语境下的其它独特含义为何，在这里我们都不必予以考虑。
But what was the created image of God? God is an absolute spirit (Jn 4:24) and thus does not
have flesh and bones (Lk 24:39). The image of God in which man was created cannot therefore
pertain to the human body. Rather, we must seek the divine image in man’s soul, his spiritual
component. The superior nature of our human bodies as well as the faculties of our souls best reflect
the truth that we are creatures meant to bear the image of God.
那么，神的形像到底又是什么呢？神是灵（约 4:24），无骨无肉（路 24:39），因此，神
的形像肯定与人类的身体无关。相反，我们必须在人的灵魂中，即人的灵魂组成部分中寻找
神的形像。我们人类身体所具有的这一高级特征以及我们灵魂的各项机能最能体现这一真
理：上帝的旨意就是要按照他的形像造我们，叫我们可以拥有他的形像。
Some have sought the divine image in the fact that a human being is a person, possessing selfconsciousness and a fully developed reason. A cat does not know that it is a cat, nor does a dog
know that it is a dog; though both may do some simple practical reasoning, neither can develop a
philosophy of life. Bearing God’s image indeed presupposes a personality possessing selfconsciousness and a fully developed intellect. But these features in themselves are not yet the divine
image. According to Scripture, human beings have lost the image of God through the fall into sin,
even though they still remain rational, self-conscious personalities.
一些人将神的形像诉诸以下事实：即，人类作为人拥有自我意识以及高度发达的理性。
猫并不知道它是一只猫，狗也不知道它是一条狗；二者也许都能作出一些简易的实操性判断，
但它们根本无法折腾出一套生命哲学来。拥有神的形像确实预设了一项先决条件，即拥有者
必须具备——自我意识和高度发达的智力——这一人格。然而，这些特征本身却仍不是神的
形像。按照圣经，即便人类现在仍然具备——理性及自我意识——这一人格，但他们早因堕
落犯罪而丧失了神的形像。
Scripture tells us that the new life of faith which the gospel engenders in our hearts, the new
man in us, is a restoration of the image of God in us. On the other hand, our natural sinful state, our
sinful flesh, our Old Adam, is the very opposite of the divine image in which God initially created
mankind. This tells us that we cannot include anything in the definition of the image of God which
we still possess in our natural sinful state and which is not distinctive of the new man in us.
圣经告诉我们，福音在我们心里所造的、那拥有信心的新生命，即我们里面的新人，就
是神的形像在我们里面所得的恢复。另一方面，我们与生俱来的罪恶状态，我们罪恶的肉体，
我们的老亚当，则与上帝起初造人时赋予他们的神圣形像截然相反。这说明一点，当我们在
为神的形像下定义时，我们绝不能将我们如今在那与生俱来的罪恶状态中仍拥有的、不属于
我们里面新人的任何东西加入其中。
Colossians 3:5–10 urges us to put on “the new self, which is being renewed in knowledge in
the image of its Creator.” True knowledge of God as the source of every blessing was a part of the
divine image in which man was created. All of man’s thoughts agreed with God’s thoughts. God
alone is omniscient. All knowledge is forever present with him as a part of his very essence. Even
at creation man was not omniscient. In Genesis 2 we see him acquiring knowledge by observation.
But all the thoughts he entertained and the conclusions he reached agreed with God’s thoughts.
歌罗西书 3:5-10 敦促我们穿上“新人，这新人照他创造主的形像在知识上渐渐更新”。
神的真知识作为一切祝福的源头，它也是人类受造时上帝所赋予的神圣形像的一部分。那时，
人类的所有想法都符合上帝的所有想法。唯独上帝无所不知。所有知识都永远全然呈现在他
的面前，全知就是他本质的一部分，而人类即便在他们受造之时也并非全知。在创世记第 2
章中，我们看到亚当通过观察获得知识，只是他所拥有的所有想法以及他得出的所有结论尽
都符合上帝的想法。
In Ephesians 4:22–24 we are commanded “to put on the new self, created to be like God in true
righteousness and holiness.” The divine image consisted also in this: man’s feelings and emotions,
his organs of evaluation, were in complete harmony with God’s evaluation of things. Man found joy
and delight in that which pleased God. Man’s will—all of his impulses, desires, and actions—was
likewise in complete harmony with God’s holy will. Again we get glimpses of this holiness and
righteousness in chapter 2. There we see Adam and, to a lesser degree, Eve acting and expressing
their thoughts and feelings while they still bore the image of God. Expressed negatively we can say
that they were without sin.
在以弗所书 4:22-24 中，上帝命令我们当“穿上新人，这新人是照着神的形像造的，有

真理的仁义，和圣洁”。这形像的神圣之处在于：人的感受与情感，全身上下评估外界刺激
的感官，尽都符合上帝的评估准则。神所喜悦之事，即人所喜闻乐见之事。人类的意志——
他的所有冲动、欲望以及行动——全然符合上帝圣洁的旨意。在第 2 章中，我们将会再次瞥

表达他的想法与感受，同样我们也会略微看到夏娃的如此种种。从消极的角度来说，那时的
他们未曾犯罪。
For a proper understanding of the divine image, it is very vital that we remember that the
Christian’s new self is a restoration of it. That knowledge unlocks the secret of man’s original
righteousness and holiness, the secret of his sinlessness. The divine image flowed out of the bond
of faith, out of perfect trust in God, with which man, male and female, was created. Break that bond
of faith and the image of God would be lost. Satan knew that. That is why his attack was against
Eve’s trust in God and for his own goodness. When Satan succeeded in breaking that trust with his
lie, Eve’s divine image, and subsequently that of Adam, was shattered and lost.39
为正确理解神的形像，我们必须得牢记，基督徒的新人是这形像所得的恢复。这样的认
知帮助我们揭开了人类原本的公义、圣洁与无罪之迷。神的形像源自于信心这条纽带，源自
于对上帝的全然信靠，而人类，男人与女人，他们受造之时便是如此信靠上帝。若是砍断这
条信心的纽带，他们所拥有的神的形像就必丧失。撒旦深知这一点，这就是为何牠所攻击的
正是夏娃对于上帝及其良善的信靠。当撒旦用牠的谎言成功破坏这种信靠时，夏娃所拥有的
神的形像就破碎并丧失了，而随后，亚当亦是如此。39

justification before God. But such God-wrought saving faith is at the same time new spiritual life,

understanding of the origin of all things. Evolution asserts initial imperfection followed by very
gradual improvement involving vast periods of time.

In the formula closing the sixth day, for the first time we have an ordinal number with a definite
article, ‫הַ שִ שִ י‬. That only the attributive has the article is to be expected with ‫יֹום‬, the substantive
which it defines (cf. GK 126w). As can be seen from Genesis 2:3 and Exodus 12:15 and 20:10, the
meaning is the same as though the definite article were attached also to the noun. Each of the
previous days, as one day in a series of creation days, had been left indefinite. The sixth day ( ‫יֹום‬

‫ )הַ שִ שִ י‬was made definite because it was appointed for the completion of God’s creation task. This
understanding of the definite article is confirmed by the following verses. They emphasize that with
the close of the sixth day, God’s creation of heaven and earth and all that they contain, as a very
specific divine task, was completed.

这一看法，它们凸显了以下事实：当第六日结束的时候，上帝创造天地以及其中的所有——
这项特定的、神圣的工作，就此完工了。

We should not pass lightly over this emphasis upon God’s work of creation as a very specific
divine task which was definitely completed by the seventh day. Rather, we ought to note how
precisely Scripture here distinguishes between God’s work of creation, which had come to an end
with the close of the sixth day, and his work of preservation, in which God has been active ever
since “sustaining all things by his powerful word” (Heb 1:3). It is in the failure to recognize this
distinction that the basic fallacy of evolutionism creeps in. Its advocates quite properly observe the
processes that are ordinarily operative in the universe now, while God is active in his work of
preservation. Yet evolutionists very improperly conclude that these same processes were operative
when this universe came into existence, while God was active in his work of creation. That is an

unwarranted conclusion. We know nothing and can know nothing about the nature of the processes
which were operative while God was active in his work of creation, beyond what is revealed in his
Word. These processes were not subject to human observation, nor can they ever be made subject
to such observation.

类亦无法通过观察得知。

of creation. According to the main clause of verse 2, the first thing mentioned is that God rested,

this very day, and I, too, am working.” Ever since the close of the sixth day, God has continued to
be intensively active in his work of preservation. Without it, heaven and earth would go out of
existence.
而当我们谈论上帝时，我们知道上述概念只适用于“‫”מִ כָל־מְ לַאכְ ּתֹו אֲשֶ ר עָשָ ה‬，即

若没有他的保全之工，天地也就早已不再了。
What God did do on the seventh day was desist from any further activity in his creation work.
This does not imply a lack or loss of interest in this work or that God was happy to be done with it.
These implications may often be present when it is said of us that we have ceased working at tasks
in which we have previously been active. God desisted from any further activity in his work of
creation because everything belonging to that task was done and because he saw that everything he
had made was very good. As far as creating was concerned, there was nothing more that needed to
be done. God’s creative blessing spoken upon man and beast was in effect. It is still effective; it has

brought us into existence.
上帝歇了他的创世之工，这也正是他在第七日所做的事。这并不表明他不再关心他已完
成的工作或者从此他就对此爱答不理了。当我们停下先前积极从事的手头任务时，我们常常
会表现出以上两种反映。但是上帝之所以歇了他的创世之工，是因为他已造齐了一切，并且
他又看这一切甚好。就创世而言，他已无需再做任何事。上帝在创造人类以及动物时所发出
的祝福是有效的，并且现在仍然有效，我们正是因此而来到了这个世上。

seventh day. In Exodus 31:17 we are told (AV): “For in six days the Lord made the heavens and the

but also ‫וַיִ נָ פַש‬. On the seventh day God abstained from further creative activity and, in doing so,
was at the same time refreshed. In Exodus 20:11 we are told of God who in six days had “made the
heavens and the earth, the sea, and all that is in them, but he rested on the seventh day.” Here still
another verb, ‫וַ יָ נַח‬, is used for God’s resting. It seemingly includes both ceasing from work and
being refreshed.

得知：“因为六日之内耶和华造了天地，第七日便安息了，也得了更新。”这里用到了两个

When these same Hebrew verbs say of human beings that they rested and were refreshed, the
first thoughts conveyed are that their strength was renewed, which had been heavily taxed by the
labors from which they desisted, and that the weariness occasioned by their past labors was
overcome. It is obvious, of course, that such thoughts are wholly excluded when it is said of God
that he rested and was refreshed. Isaiah 40:28 forcefully reminds us: “Do you not know? Have you
not heard? The Lord is the everlasting God, the Creator of the ends of the earth. He will not grow
tired or weary.…” When it is said of God that he rested, that he was refreshed, these are
anthropomorphisms. Even when it is said of man that he rests from his labors and is refreshed, this
generally means more than that he is regaining his strength and overcoming his weariness. It
generally also means that he is finding joy and satisfaction in the fruits of his past labors. Only the
latter is meant when we are told of God that he rested and was refreshed on the seventh day. He
found great joy, delight, and satisfaction in everything that he had made, especially in man, whom

当以上这些希伯来动词用来描述人类歇息以及得更新时，首先，它们表示当人停下劳累
之后，他们的体力得到了恢复，工作带来的疲惫感也消失不见了。当然，论及上帝安息又得
了更新，这些含义则明显不正确。以赛亚书 40:28 对我们的提醒掷地有声：“你岂不曾知道
吗？你岂不曾听见吗？永在的神耶和华，创造地极的主，并不疲乏，也不困倦......”即便当

我们说某人歇了他的工作并得了更新时，通常这也远不止表明他重新得了力且又不再感到疲
惫，通常这还表示他从过去的劳累中得到了喜乐和满足。而当圣经告诉我们上帝在第七日安
息又得了更新时，只有上述第二种含义才适用于他。从他所造的每样事物之中，尤其是从他
按他自己的形像所造的、叫他们可以与他相交的人类当中，他得到了莫大的喜乐、愉悦与满
足。
This understanding of God’s rest and refreshment is verified by the additional actions that God
took on the seventh day. According to verse 3, God blessed this seventh day after creation and
hallowed it. We have had previous occasion to note that God’s blessing is an effective action. When
God blesses, he effects blessings. When God blesses living beings, they become the recipients of
his blessings. The effect is somewhat different when God blesses lifeless things. Then his act of
blessing makes these inanimate objects the sources of benefits for living creatures, most especially
sources of benefits for man that they may redound to his welfare and blessing. Confer Genesis 27:27,
where God is said to have blessed fields, and Exodus 23:25, which holds out God’s promise to bless
bread and water. With this understanding, we too pray: “Heavenly Father, bless this food, to your
glory and our good.” When God not only blessed (‫ )וַיְ בָ ֶרְך‬the seventh day but also hallowed
(‫ )וַיְ קַ דֵ ש‬it, he set it aside as a source of blessing and benefit for man. For under man’s dominion
God had placed the earth and all that it contained.
上帝在第七日另又做了些事，这点也证实了以上看法。从第 3 节中我们看到，上帝祝福
了第七日，并将它分别为圣。我们已在前文中注意到，上帝的祝福本身就是有效果的行动。
当上帝发出祝福的时候，他就会让他的祝福生效。当上帝祝福有生命之物，他们就成了他祝
福的承受者；当上帝祝福无生命之物时，结果则会略有不同。无生命之物于是就成了叫有生
命之物得益处的一股源泉，尤其对于人类而言，他们可以因此福祉加增，蒙受许多祝福。以
创世记 27:27 为例，上帝祝福田地，又以出埃及记 23:25 为例，上帝应许祝福粮与水。带着
这样的见解，我们也祷告说：“天父啊，请祝福这些食物，叫你自己得荣耀，又叫我们得益
处。”上帝不但祝福了（‫）וַיְ בָ ֶרך‬第七日，并将这日分别为圣（‫）וַיְ קַ דֵ ש‬，他将这日分别出
来让它成为一个祝福的源头，叫人类得各样的益处。因为上帝已将全地以及其中的一切交给
人来治理。
To explain why God blessed and hallowed this day, we are told “for on [this day] God rested
from his entire work which he had created by making.” Moses here uses the specialized verb ‫בָ ָרא‬
for the entire activity which God had finished and from which he was now desisting. Throughout
this work, God had acted in a way that was truly extraordinary and epoch making, in a way in which
only the eternal, almighty, and all-wise God could work. It had been work which God had carried
out “by making” (‫)לַ עֲשֹות‬, by individual divine acts described in the account of the six creation
days (cf. GK,114o for the gerundival use of the infinitive ‫)לַ עֲשֹות‬. Through this marvelous

Even as God rested on the seventh day, even as God found joy and satisfaction in all that he had
made, so man likewise was to find blessed rest in God’s created works and in the gracious God who
had made it all for him.44 With joy and delight, with praise and thanksgiving to God, man and woman
were to see and experience on the seventh day how good God had made everything for them and
how good he had made them. In this way man was to enjoy blessed fellowship with his God, joy
and bliss in all of God’s works on the seventh day and on every subsequent day.
正如上帝在第七日安息了，正如上帝从他所造的一切之中得到了喜乐和满足，同样，人
类也得以从上帝的创世作为以及这位为他们造了一切的恩慈的上帝那里得了蒙福的安息。44
带着喜乐与愉悦，带着对上帝的赞美和感恩，男人和女人在第七日看到又经历到了上帝的美
善——他为他们所造的这一切何等美好，为他们造了这一切的他更是何等美善。如此，人类
便得以享受与他们这位上帝之间的美好相交，在第七日因神的一切作为而满心喜乐，享受其
中的福份，并且随后的每一天都可以如此。
It should, therefore, not surprise us that the seventh day is not closed in the same solemn way
in which the other six creation days were individually closed. The divine acts performed on each of
those six days were unique, never to be repeated on any other day. Not so the blessing of rest which
God showered on the seventh day as a source of joy and delight, of blissful fellowship with God for
mankind. This blessing was likewise to mark every subsequent day for man. The seventh day was
the dedication day of heaven and earth and of the joy and bliss which man was to find in God’s
creation and in the God from whom it had all come. It is not the seventh day that continued on.
There is no scriptural basis for this assertion. With the ordinal ‫הַ שְ בִ יעִ י‬, the seventh day is lined
up with the six days that preceded it. It must in essence have been like them, consisting of the same
light and darkness periods which, ever since the fourth day, were determined by the heavenly light
bearers. It was the blessing of rest of the seventh day that was to continue.
因此，对于第七日没有和其它六个创世之日那样一一庄严结束，我们并不需要感到惊讶。
六日中的每一日中，上帝的创世作为皆为独一无二，并没有重样的。然而，神在第七日赐予
人类的安息之福却与之前不同，它是一股为人带来喜乐和愉悦、叫人得以与神在福乐之中相
交的源泉。同样，这祝福也标志着人类在今后的每一日都要享受这等福份。这第七日是庆祝
天地受造完成的大喜之日，也是一个充满福乐的日子，人类在上帝的创造以及这位创造万有

This is the thought that is emphasized in Hebrews 3:8–4:11. There, a rest for God’s people is
spoken of as still remaining. This rest is still being offered by God. Blessed are those who come to
enter it. This rest which God still desires human beings to enter is not spoken of as a new blessing.
It goes back to the completion of creation, when it was said, “On the seventh day God rested from
all his work” (Heb 4:4). It is none other than the rest of blessed joy and delight in God and in his
works into which Adam and Eve were drawn on the seventh day. We know, of course, that when
Adam and Eve fell into sin they, and all mankind with them, lost this rest. But the writer to the
Hebrews states that through the gospel, God again and again offers this rest to sinful human beings.
Many have failed to enter this rest because of unbelief. This rest into which we sinners enter through
faith in the gospel is nothing less than blessed fellowship with God which will reach its
consummation in heaven.
这正是希伯来书 3:8–4:11 所强调的观点。论到安息，其中说到，“必另有一安息日的安
息，为神的子民存留。”这安息如今仍由上帝所赐，而进入这安息的人是有福的。如今，上
帝依然渴望人类进入这安息，并且，这安息也并非什么新的祝福，而是仍要追溯到创世完工
之时，“论到第七日，有一处说，到第七日神就歇了他一切的工”（来 4:4）。这安息不是
别的什么，正是上帝在第七日赐给亚当和夏娃的那份充满福乐的安息，那份因上帝及其作为
而叫他们感到愉悦的安息。当然，我们也知道，当亚当和夏娃堕落犯罪的时候，全人类就和
他们一起失去了这份安息。但是，希伯来书的作者却告诉我们，藉着福音，上帝一次又一次
地将这份安息赐给了罪人。只是，许多人因为不信就无法进入这份安息。我们这些罪人藉着
信福音所进入的这份安息并不是别的什么，正是与上帝之间那份蒙福的相交，并且，它要在
天堂得以完全。
What needs to be realized and kept in mind is that in Genesis 2:2, 3 nothing is said about the
seventh day of every week, about every regularly recurring seventh day. Moses speaks only of the
seventh day that followed the six days of creation.
另外还有一点我们需要记住，创世记 2:2,3 并未指明，其中论及的第七日就是如今每周
都会重复出现那个的第七日。摩西所说的无非就是六个创世之日之后的第七日。
When God commanded the Old Testament weekly Sabbath (Ex 20:8–11), he indeed pointed to
his rest on the seventh day following creation and to his blessing and hallowing of this seventh day.
He pointed to it as a reason for the Sabbath law which he gave to Israel. He does not say that the
Old Testament weekly Sabbath was already instituted at creation. Israel’s Sabbath was to remind it
of the blessed rest which man had lost by sin. It was to foreshadow the promised Messiah’s
restoration of this rest for mankind. Christ fulfilled the Old Testament Sabbath law and abrogated it
when, through his redemptive work, he established the gospel of the forgiveness of sins (Col 2:17).45

创世记 1:1-2:3 概要
The eternal, almighty, all-wise God created all things in perfection to his glory and for the
benefit of man, whom he made in his own image to be the special object of his love and whom he
drew into his own Sabbath rest.
永恒、全能、全智的上帝造了万有，一切尽都完美无瑕，这是为着他自己的荣耀，也是
