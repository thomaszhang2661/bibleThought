Martin Luther called the Old Testament “ein Christbuch,” a book about Christ. He knew that
although the full light of God’s New Testament revelation does not shine on the pages of the Old
Testament, the entire body of Christian teaching is found there. The apostle Paul, who articulated
the body of Christian doctrine more clearly and more completely than any other apostle, said as
much when he told King Agrippa: “I am saying nothing beyond what the prophets and Moses said
would happen—that Christ would suffer and, as the first to rise from the dead, would proclaim light
to his own people and to the Gentiles” (Ac 26:22, 23).
马丁·路德称旧约为“ein Christbuch”，即，一本讲论基督的书。他知道，尽管上帝新
约的启示并未全然光照在旧约的书页之上，但基督教的主体教导却已得见其中。相较于其他
任何一位使徒，使徒保罗对于基督教主体教义的阐释都要来得更为清晰与完整。他对着亚基
帕王如此说到，“我......所讲的，并不外乎众先知和摩西所说，将来必成的事。就是基督必
须受害，并且因从死里复活，要首先把光明的道，传给百姓和外邦人”（徒 26:22,23）。

In the minds of a whole generation of pastors in the Wisconsin Evangelical Lutheran Synod,
the book of Genesis is intimately associated with the name Lawrenz. Carl J. Lawrenz was called to
the faculty of Wisconsin Lutheran Seminary in 1944 and taught Old Testament and Symbolics until
his retirement in 1982. For most of those years, he was president of the seminary and chairman of
the Old Testament department. For many years, he was also the dean of students. He served the
church at large as chairman of the Commission on Interchurch Relations and as chairman of the
Commission on Higher Education. But it is likely that he is most fondly remembered as the man
who opened up the Old Testament, specifically the book of Genesis, to a generation of pastors. The
undersigned was a member of the first Genesis class that Professor Lawrenz taught when he came
to the seminary. His knowledge of the Scripture and of the Hebrew language was thorough, and his
acquaintance with the literature of the field was impressive. But what impressed his students most
of all was his childlike faith in the Savior, whom he had learned to know from the pages of the Old
and the New Testaments.
在威斯康辛福音路德会整整一代牧师的心目中，创世记这册书总与劳伦斯这个名字紧紧
相联。卡尔·约翰·劳伦斯于 1944 年受呼召进入威斯康辛路德宗神学院，教授旧约与象征
学，直至 1982 年退休。在此期间，他长期担任着神学院院长与旧约系主任的职务，且担任
教导主任的年头也不在少数。他曾一度以担任跨教会关系委员会主席与高等教育委员会主席
的方式服侍着教会。但也许，最令人怀念的仍是他向一整代的牧师细致地讲解旧约这件事，
特别是创世记。该前言底部的署名人正是劳伦斯教授的一位学生，也是他刚到神学院所教第
一节创世记课堂上的那位学生。劳伦斯拥有通透的圣经和希伯来语知识，且对该领域的文学
作品也甚精通。但最让他的学生难以忘怀的却是他那颗孩童般倚靠救主——就是旧约和新约
书卷向他所启示的这位救主——的信心。
Toward the end of his long ministry, Prof. Lawrenz was asked by Northwestern Publishing
House to write a professional commentary on the book of Genesis. He began the work, but the
pressure of his other duties did not permit him to finish the assignment. He was able to complete
only the introduction and the commentary on the first four chapters before his death in 1989. The
undersigned, Prof. Lawrenz’ student and later his colleague on the faculty, was called to the
seminary in 1969 and taught in the Old Testament department there for 25 years. Included in his
teaching assignment for all of those years was the exegesis of the book of Genesis. The only
textbook for that class was the Hebrew Bible.
在劳伦斯教授漫长教牧生涯的末期，他受到了西北出版社的邀请，准备就创世记撰写一
部专业的注释书。他开了个好头，但期间因手头其余工作给他的压力，终究未能完成这项任
务。他于 1989 年去世，在此之前，他只完成了引言与前四章的注释。该前言底部的署名人
系劳伦斯教授的学生，后与他一同在神学院任教，于 1969 年受呼召进入神学院，在旧约部
门任教 25 载。那些年来，他的教学任务之一就是教授创世记的解经课。那门课的唯一教科
书就是希伯来语圣经。
After Prof. Lawrenz’ death, the undersigned was asked to extend the unfinished Genesis
commentary from Genesis chapters 5 through 11—in order to complete the exposition of the first
five of the ten toledoth, the ten “accounts” that make up the book of Genesis. The first five accounts
trace God’s saving activity in the original world. They tell of the origin of the universe and of the
human race and of nature—not like a textbook of science, but like the inspired record of a loving
and powerful Creator at work.
劳伦斯教授死后，为完成十段 toledoth（即，构成创世记的十份“记载”）中的前五段，
这位署名人受邀扩写尚未完成的创世记第 5-11 章的注释。前五份记载追溯上帝在早期世界
中的救恩作为。它们述说着宇宙、人类以及自然的起源，它们并不是什么科学课本，而是由
一位慈爱、大能、活跃的创造主所默示的记载。
These first five accounts relate a considerable amount of ancient history, some of which is
recorded nowhere else. Again, however, these accounts do not do this like a textbook of history.
Moses may survey long periods of time very briefly, while treating shorter periods of time in great
detail.
这前五份记载所涉古代历史甚广，其中一些无法见于别处。然而，我们必须再次强调，
这些记载绝非历史课本一般。摩西可能就较长时间跨度的历史内容做了些简略的查考，但他
对较短时间跨度的历史却作了详尽的描述。
On this background of nature and history, Moses introduces the human race. The first 11
chapters of Genesis make it very clear that I am not the captain of my fate or the master of my soul,
but that I am a creature of God. He designed me to live not alongside him as a rival, but under him,
in a master-and-servant relationship. The Christian church is not a democracy, but an absolute
monarchy. Tragically, however, the first chapters of Genesis record that the unbelievable, the
incomprehensible happened: the creature broke with his Creator and, subsequently, with his brother.
Genesis chapters 1–11 documents how this played out, not only in the lives of individuals, but in
the lives of nations as well.
在这自然及历史背景之下，摩西向我们介绍了人类。创世记前 11 章的内容让我们清楚
看到：我并非我命运的掌舵者，也不是我灵魂的主宰者，而是上帝的创造物。他造我并非要
让我与他比肩，做他对头，而是要让我在他之下，他是我主，我是他仆。基督的教会并非一
种民主政体，而是一种绝对的君主制。然而，可悲的是，创世记的前几章却记下了一些让人
难以置信、令人费解的内容：受造物与其创造主决裂，并随之与其兄弟决裂。创世记第 1-11
章将这些事的原委记录在案，它们不仅讲述着个体的生命，也讲述着民族的历史。
This commentary also introduces the sixth toledoth, “The Account of Terah,” and with it the
second half of Genesis, which traces God’s saving activity among the patriarchs.
这本注释书也向我们介绍了第六段 toledoth——“他拉的历史”，而紧随其后的正是创
世记的第二部分内容——为我们追溯上帝在族长中间的救恩作为。
God and his gracious plan for his fallen creatures are the very heart of Moses’ narrative. Again
and again in his inspired record, Moses emphasizes how God has moved through all of history to
reach out to people—in judgment and in mercy. God speaks in nature, and he speaks in history. His
words of mercy and judgment touch the lives of individual people and direct the history of nations.
Genesis chapters 1–11 shows us the beginning of the church; it also portrays vividly the differences
between the unbelieving world and the faithful remnant—their essential natures, as well as their
ultimate destinies.
上帝以及上帝为其堕落的创造物所预备的恩慈计划就是摩西所记内容的重中之重。在摩
西受神默示所写下的这些内容当中，他一次又一次向我们强调，贯穿历史，上帝如何按他的
审判和怜悯对待人类。上帝藉自然界说话，上帝也藉历史说话。他口里所出的话语既有怜悯
又有审判，这些话语触摸各人的生活，引导列族的历史。创世记第 1-11 章既向我们展示了
教会的开端，也将这个不信的世界与信徒余民之间的迥异——他们的根本性以及他们最终的
命运——活化在我们眼前。
To study these chapters is not an academic exercise, but an act of worship. Both authors of this
commentary have attempted to let you hear God speaking to you from the first pages of his Book.
As you listen to him, you will be convinced that these chapters are not the clumsy work of some
ancient editor who cobbled together parallel (and sometimes conflicting) ancient sagas and
documents that came into his possession centuries after the time of Moses. The human author of
Genesis was a highly educated man (Ac 7:22) and, above all, a man with his ear attuned to the Spirit
of God. If he chose to quote from a pre-existing document (cf. Ge 5:1), that’s one thing. But to deny
Mosaic authorship because of alleged numerous sources one claims to have uncovered throughout
the Pentateuch is to do violence to the Scripture. It brands Moses as a secondhander, and a bumbling
one at that. And it labels Jesus Christ as someone who was not telling the truth when he said,
“[Moses] wrote about me” (Jn 5:46).
学习这些章节并非某种学术性的操练，而实在是一项敬拜之举。本注释书的两位作者都
想让你在上帝这卷书的第一部分内容中听见他向你说话。当你聆听他说话之时，你也会深信，
这些章节绝非出自某位来自摩西之后几个世纪的古代编辑之手，也不是他笨手笨脚地将他那
时代（有时候互相矛盾）的古代传说及文件拼凑而成的杰作。创世记的人类作者才高八斗（徒
7:22），并且，最重要的是，他的耳朵聆听神的灵说话，与他同频。若他选择引用某份在他
以前早已存在的文件（参创 5:1），这是一回事。但是，若因某人声称自己在五经中发现的
所谓的大量资料来源而否认摩西的作者身份，那就是另一回事了，这是在破坏圣经。这给摩
西贴上了二手货的标签，而且还是一个笨手笨脚的二手货。这给耶稣基督贴上了宣讲虚谎的
标签，因他曾说，“（摩西）曾指着我写”（约 5:46）下了那些话。
To summarize: Much of the contemporary literature on Genesis assumes there is no such thing
as divine revelation. For contemporary liberal scholarship, Mosaic authorship is not even an option.
The two authors of this commentary have written, without qualification and without apology, from
the conviction that the ancient history contained in Genesis is not an edited collection of ancient
sagas and literary fragments passed down from generation to generation. The book of Genesis is not
Jewish national literature, the product of human genius, to be studied in purely literary terms.
总的来说，当代许多讲论创世记的文学作品都认为压根儿就没有神启这回事儿。对于当
代自由派的学术而言，摩西的作者身份连个选项都算不上。本注释书的两位作者不需要他人
授予什么资格，也不需为自己作出任何辩护，他们在书写此时坚信，创世记所涵盖的古代史
并非由一代代流传下来的古代传说及文学片段编辑收集而成。创世记不是一部叫我们从纯文
学的角度去研究的、犹太民族的文学作品，它并非人类天才的产物。
It is, rather, the inspired Word which the eternal God in pure grace speaks to his human creatures
today, just as he has always spoken.
反而，它是神所默示的话语，今天，上帝仍以其纯粹的恩典将这些话说给他所造的人类
听，正如他总是如此说话。

Introduction
引言
Neither in the Old nor in the New Testament is the portion of Scripture we know as Genesis
ever referred to as a separate biblical book. Anything stated in Genesis is simply recognized as being
presented in the Old Testament’s first portion, whose scriptural name in its simplest form is ‫הָ ּתֹורָ ה‬
(hattorah). This name means “the teaching, the instruction” and is commonly translated “the law.”
When St. Paul in Galatians 4:21 refers to the Genesis account of Abraham’s two sons and their
mothers, he writes: “Tell me, you who want to be under the law, are you not aware of what the law
says?”
无论是在旧约还是在新约中，我们所知道的创世记都不是一部独立的圣经书卷。旧约第
一部分的内容以其最简洁的形式被称为‫（הָ ּתֹורָ ה‬hattorah，妥拉），而创世记正是其中的第
一册书。‫הָ ּתֹורָ ה‬意为“教训，指示”，通常被译为“律法”，当圣保罗在加拉太书 4:21 中
提到创世记中亚伯拉罕的两个儿子和他们的母亲时，他如此写到：“你们这愿意在律法以下
的人，请告诉我，你们岂没有听见律法所说的吗？”
Genesis first occurred as the title of the first portion of the Torah in the Septuagint (LXX), the
ancient Greek translation of the Old Testament. In the LXX the entire Torah was called ἡ πεντάτευχος
sc. βίβλος, the five-volumed book. At the same time, the LXX gave titles to each of the five parts.
These names were taken, either in translation or in Latinized form, and were used in the Vulgate of
St. Jerome. By way of the Vulgate, they appeared in the English Authorized Version as Genesis,
Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy. Luther also used the name Genesis in his Latin
exposition, although in his German Bible translation he contented himself with enumerating the five
books of Moses.
创世记曾以妥拉第一部分的标题首次出现在古代旧约希腊语译本——七十士译本（LXX）
之中。在七十士译本中，整部妥拉被称为 ἡ πεντάτευχος sc. βίβλος，即，含五册书的经书。与
此同时，七十士译本为这五册书中的每一册都附上了相应的标题。这些名字以相应的译文或
拉丁语形式被人采纳，且被用于圣耶柔米的武加大译本中。通过武加大译本，它们以创世记、
出埃及记、利未记、民数记和申命记这些名字出现于英文钦定本中。尽管路德在他的德语译
本中只是将摩西的这五册书列了出来，但他在他的拉丁语注释中也采用了创世记这个名字。
The five divisions are very natural ones which were undoubtedly apparent to the hearers and
readers of the Pentateuch from the beginning. A long gap of time intervened between the accounts
of Genesis and Exodus. The narrative with which Genesis ends—the patriarchal family’s settlement
in Egypt—becomes that of the deliverance of the Israelites as a numerous people from Egypt when
it continues in Exodus. The Leviticus section consists almost exclusively of laws, setting forth the
ceremonial holiness of God’s chosen people. As the narrative is once more resumed in the Numbers
section, it relates the long wilderness sojourn involved in Israel’s journey from Sinai to the plains
of Moab. Deuteronomy presents the repetition and completion of the theocratic law code in the three
farewell addresses of Moses on the plains of Moab as Israel was about to enter Canaan under the
leadership of Joshua.
毫无疑问，这五册书原先的划分对于摩西五经的听众和读者而言从一开始就显而易见。
创世记和出埃及记之间的历史间隔很长。创世记以族长的家人最后在埃及的定居结尾，而在
接下来出埃及记的叙述中，这些人则繁衍成了一个庞大的民族，且最终得救脱离了埃及的奴
役。利未记则几乎完全在谈论律法，阐明神的选民当在仪式上保持圣洁。接着，民数记恢复
了叙事，讲述以色列人从西奈山到摩押平原的漫长旷野之旅。最后，当以色列人正准备在约
书亚的带领下进入迦南时，申命记则以摩西在摩押平原的三次告别演说重申且完成了那部神
权法典。
The Jews also recognized these five natural divisions, although they remained very conscious
of the Torah as a unit—the fundamental unit of God’s Old Testament revelation. They contented
themselves with referring to the divisions by means of the initial word of each section. The rabbis
spoke of the Torah as hammishshah humshe hattorah, the five-fifths of the law, and of the first
division as the sepher bere’shith.
尽管犹太人一直非常谨慎地将妥拉视为上帝旧约启示内容中的一整块基本单元，但他们
也确实认得这五册书原先的划分方式。他们认为只需用每册内容的起始词来区分这五册书便
足矣。犹太拉比将妥拉称为 hammishshah humshe hattorah，即，五分之五的律法，且将其中
第一册称为 sepher bere’shith。
The Septuagint title Γένεσις, Genesis, which appears in the Codex Alexandrinus as Γένεσις
κόσμου, can be translated as “origin, beginning.” As far as the content of Genesis is concerned, this
makes a very appropriate title for the first division of the Pentateuch. This division is indeed a
presentation of very vital beginnings. We are not to think only, as the Codex Alexandrinus apparently
did, of the beginning of the whole universe. Genesis also very specifically presents the beginning
of this earth and of all that it contains, the beginning of the human race, the beginning of sin, the
beginning of the dire consequences of sin, the beginning of God’s manifestation of his saving grace
which would culminate in the Protevangel, the beginning of the enmity between the serpent’s seed

Other scholars take the Septuagint’s consistent translation of ‫ ּתֹולְ דֹות‬toledoth, with the
genitive singular or plural of γένεσις, genesis, to account for the use of this Greek term as the title
of the first division of the Pentateuch.1 Yet genesis, meaning “origin,” or “beginning,” is not an
acceptable translation of toledoth. The basic meaning of toledoth is “descendants.” In the structure
of Genesis, the word is used in a broader sense ten times to indicate the divisions of the material
that is being presented. Nine of these toledoth (numbers 2–10) are named after persons. In each of
these cases, the toledoth does not tell us of the origin or birth of the person after whom it is named.
It tells us instead what happened to him, what developed from him, and what he did in relation to
the carrying out of God’s plan of salvation. Thus toledoth may well be translated as “history” or
“account.”2 The one remaining toledoth, the very first one, is that of heaven and earth. Again, it is
the history of what happened to heaven and earth after God created them.

The text of Genesis itself contains no statement as to its inspired author. We hasten to add that
this does not in any way leave the matter of the book’s authorship unsettled. The opening statement
of this introduction has already emphasized that in the Old as well as in the New Testament all the
material found in Genesis is spoken of only as part of the Torah. And both the Old and New
Testaments clearly ascribe the Torah to Moses.
创世记文本本身并未就其受神默示的作者身份作出声明。但我们得立刻补充说，这并未
对这册书的作者身份造成任何影响。本书的引言开头早已强调，无论是旧约，还是新约，它
们都不过将创世记中的所有内容称为妥拉的一部分而已。旧约和新约都将妥拉的作者身份归
于摩西，这点再清楚不过了。
This is done already in the amplified names which some of the Bible writers give to the Torah.
These amplified names are of two kinds. It is noteworthy, on the one hand, that the Torah is spoken
of by such amplified names as “the Law of God” (Ne 10:28, 29); “the Law of the Lord” (1 Ch 16:40;
2 Ch 31:3; 35:26; Ezr 7:10; Lk 2:23, 24); “the Book of the Law of God” (Jos 24:26; Ne 8:18); “the
Book of the Law of the Lord” (2 Ch 17:9; 34:14); “the Book of the Law of the Lord their God” (Ne
9:3). All these amplifications of the name Torah stress its divine author. They assure us that the
Pentateuch, which includes Genesis, is nothing less than the inspired and inerrant Word of the Lord
our God.

In the Old Testament Canon, which our Savior Jesus Christ recognized as “the Scripture which
cannot be broken” (Jn 10:35), the Torah formed the first and fundamental part. This Torah was
clearly ascribed by Jesus to Moses. According to Luke 24:44, after his resurrection, Christ told his
disciples: “This is what I told you while I was still with you: Everything must be fulfilled that is
written about me in the Law of Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms.” Thereupon Luke follows with
the comment, “Then he opened their minds so they could understand the Scriptures.” Here Luke
indicates that with the terms “the Law of Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms,” Jesus was referring
to the Old Testament Scriptures according to the three divisions in which they were assembled at
the time. This is still the arrangement of the various books as we have them in our printed Hebrew
Old Testaments: Torah, nebhi’im, kethubhim. The latter division, simply meaning “writings,” begins
with the Psalms and ends with 2 Chronicles.
我们的救主耶稣基督视旧约正典为“经上不能废的话语”（约 10:35），而妥拉则正是旧
约正典的起头与基石。耶稣明确将妥拉的作者身份归于摩西。根据路加福音 24:44，复活后
的基督对他的门徒这样说到：“这就是我从前与你们同在之时，所告诉你们的话，说，‘摩
西的律法，先知的书，和诗篇上所记的，凡指着我的话，都必须应验。’”路加随即说到，
“于是耶稣开他们的心窍，使他们能明白圣经。”路加在这里表明，耶稣使用“摩西的律法、
先知的书和诗篇”这几个词来称呼旧约圣经，而这种区分法也正是当时人们收录旧约经卷时
所采用的方法。我们目前所拥有的印刷版希伯来语旧约圣经仍按这种方法排列各卷经书，即：
妥拉，先知，圣写（Torah, nebhi’im, kethubhim）。其中最后一部分圣写，原意无非就是“作
品”，其书卷始于诗篇，终于历代志下。
That Jesus thought and spoke of the Old Testament in terms of this arrangement of the Canon
is evident also in Luke 11:49–51. Here Jesus warned the Jewish leaders of the judgment awaiting
those who, in their hostility to God’s own Son, were bringing all the past opposition against the
faithful witnesses of God’s saving Word to a climax. He pointed out that opposition to God’s saving
message spanned the entire Old Testament from the martyrdom that Cain inflicted upon Abel,
recorded in an early chapter of the Torah (Ge 4:8ff.), to the shedding of Zechariah’s blood between
the altar and the sanctuary, reported in the last book of the kethubhim (2 Ch 24:20–22).
耶稣正是按这种正典排列次序思考并谈及旧约的，路加福音 11:49-51 也向我们清楚表明
了这一点。在这里，耶稣向犹太领袖发出警告：那些敌对神的儿子、又敌对忠心见证上帝救

Negative Old Testament criticism denies the Mosaic authorship of the Pentateuch. It holds that
the Pentateuch was gradually compiled from a number of literary strands. It theorizes that each of
these literary strands was put into written form many centuries after the time of Moses. Much of the
isagogical and exegetical literature on the Pentateuch during the past two hundred years has been
written from the viewpoint of these assumptions.
针对旧约的消极批判法否认摩西作为摩西五经的作者身份。该批判法认为摩西五经是由
一系列文学作品逐渐串接起来并最终编辑而成的。它的理论是，这些被串接起来的每部文学
作品都是在摩西时代许多个世纪之后才被写成书面形式的。在过去两百年里，许多关于摩西
五经的神学导论及解经作品都将这些假设观点当作了它们的讨论前提。
There has never been, nor is there now, agreement on the number of these sources, on the
portions of the Pentateuch which are to be assigned to each, on the time of their composition, or on
the precise process by which they were supposedly woven together to form the present Pentateuch.
At best one can say that there is a general consensus among those who espouse source criticism that
the Pentateuch is a fusion of four main documents labeled Yahwist (J), Elohist (E), Deuteronomist
(D), and Priestly (P). Source critics furthermore agree that, beginning with the 10th or 9th century
b.c., these four sources were independently composed and subsequently interwoven with one
another, the final fusion and redaction taking place after Judah’s return from the Babylonian
captivity. Negative Pentateuch criticism is therefore commonly identified with the JEDP
hypothesis.8

这些人受到他们的引导而得出结论，认为他们的这种假设一定建立在极为牢靠的根基之上。
It is very important, therefore, to realize that it was not overwhelming evidence which led Old
Testament scholars to assume that post-Mosaic sources make up the books of the Pentateuch. Rather
it was the anti-supernaturalistic presuppositions with which they approached the Pentateuch. This
was acknowledged early in the history of negative Pentateuch criticism by one of its ardent
advocates, W. M. L. De Wette. He expressed it very succinctly: “If it is conclusive for the educated
mind that such miracles (as are contained in the Pentateuch) did not really take place, one asks
himself whether they perhaps appeared as such to the eyewitnesses and participants; but that, too,
must be denied.… Thus the conclusion is reached that the narrative was not contemporaneous, nor
was it taken from contemporaneous sources.”9

因此，我们势必要认识到一点，即，旧约学者之所以认为摩西五经是由那些晚于摩西时
代的资料来源组合而成，并非因为他们证据确凿，而是因为他们是在用反对超自然观的前设
看待摩西五经。在针对摩西五经采用消极批判法的早期，该批判法的热心倡导者之一——威
廉•马丁•莱布雷希特•德维特早已承认他们所使用的这种前设。他清楚说到：“毫无疑问，
凡受过教育的人定然不会认同这类神迹（如摩西五经中所包含的）的真实性，不过我们也可
能问自己，这些神迹是否曾经发生在那些目击者和参与者的眼前呢？然而，这也绝无可能......
因此，结论就是，摩西五经中的叙事绝不属于那个时代，也绝非取自那时代的资料来源。”

To get the full import of De Wette’s statement, it is worthwhile to reflect on it in terms of a
concrete Pentateuch account like that of God’s proclamation of the Decalog on Mount Sinai (Ex
20:2ff.; Dt 5:22–24). In the latter passage Moses reminded Israel: “These are the commandments
the Lord proclaimed in a loud voice to your whole assembly there on the mountain from out of the
fire, the cloud and the deep darkness.” There can be no doubt that Moses is here assuring a miracle.
If Moses really said this as an eyewitness concerning eyewitnesses, there is no way of explaining
away the miracle. The “whole assembly” of which Moses was speaking consisted of more than half
a million men, besides women and children. Even people who were not scientifically trained would
have known whether or not they had heard the Ten Commandments proclaimed to them with a loud
voice from the darkness of Mount Sinai. According to the text, Moses, without fear of contradiction,
stated that they had heard it.

“这些话是耶和华在山上，从火中，云中，

这里提到的神迹。而关于摩西在这里提到的“全会众”，其中仅男子的数目就超过 50 万，

他也认为没有人会对此存有疑问。
De Wette balked at accepting miracles of this kind, with which the Pentateuch abounds. For
him it was a foregone conclusion that God governed and directed the affairs of this world through
the natural means which he had established. In other words, God did not reveal his will to mankind
directly and supernaturally, nor did he intervene in a miraculous way in human affairs. Proceeding
from such firm anti-supernaturalistic presuppositions, De Wette saw only one alternative: The
Pentateuch accounts must come from later Judean and Israelite writers. He felt that such writers,
who occupied themselves with the traditions of their people and projected themselves into their
people’s heroic past, could be expected to accept the miraculous element which is prone to become
entwined with the golden age of any people.
德维特拒绝接受这类神迹，然而它们却遍布摩西五经。对他而言，他的预设就是上帝藉
他所设立的自然规律掌管和引导这世界的事务。换句话说，上帝未曾以直接和超自然的方式
向人类启示他自己的旨意，他也从未以施行神迹的方式介入人类事务。从这种顽固的反超自
然观前设出发，德维特只看到了一条出路：摩西五经所记载的内容定然源自晚期的犹大及以
色列作者。他认为，既然这类作者专注于弘扬他们百姓的传统，又将自己身临其境于他们百
姓的英雄过往之中，那么他们将神迹元素纳入书中也就不足为奇了。对于任何一个民族而言，
这些元素往往都会与他们的黄金时代交织在一起。

During the period of European enlightenment, which fostered strong anti-supernaturalistic
presuppositions, Old Testament scholars vied with one another in trying to detect and separate the
documentary sources which were supposedly woven together in the Pentateuch. For a hundred years,
from the initial amateur efforts of Jean Astruc in 1753 to those of Hermann Hupfeld in 1853, this
was done by a laborious literary analysis of the Pentateuch text. One hypothesis followed another
as the obvious weaknesses of each preceding hypothesis were pointed out by friend or foe of the
critical procedure. Upon the initial two-document hypotheses, there followed in a series the
fragment hypothesis, the supplement hypothesis, the crystallization hypothesis, and, finally,
Hupfeld’s modified document hypothesis. Some of these hypotheses had a number of advocates
who each offered variants.

subject matter being presented. He also knows that any account of much length which offers

与之前那些批判者从文学分析入手对待摩西五经叙事的做法不同，威尔豪森则从他对摩

But these three codes belong together. The Book of the Covenant was Israel’s God-given
constitution, setting forth its fundamental obligations as God’s people and the basic rights which
were to be protected in the covenant nation. The so-called Priestly Code was God’s subsequent
elaboration of this constitution. It was also given by God to Israel through Moses, for the most part
at Mount Sinai and in a lesser measure during the wilderness journey. The Deuteronomic Code was
embodied in the admonitory farewell addresses of Moses. It was a repetition of the theocratic
legislation for the new generation which was preparing to enter Canaan. In part it was an adaptation
of some of the legislation’s precepts to the new conditions which the Israelites would face in their
new homeland. Since in this review of God’s Law Moses addressed the entire people, he quite
naturally did not repeat all the ritual details, which were the particular responsibility of the
priesthood to teach and carry out.
然而，这三部法典却属于彼此。约书是上帝赐给以色列的宪法，其内容阐明了以色列作
为上帝百姓应尽的基本责任以及这个与上帝立约的民族所享有的各项权利。所谓的祭司法典
则是上帝随后对这部宪法所作的详述。同样，上帝藉摩西将它赐给了以色列人，其中，他在

记法典被收录于摩西的劝勉和告别演说之中。神权律法在其中得到了重申，旨在为新一代以
色列人进入迦南作好准备。因以色列将在他们新的居所面临新的挑战，所以就某种程度而言，

申这些律法时，他并未重申所有关乎仪式的细节，因教导和施行这些细节是祭司的特殊职责
所在。

development, Wellhausen chose to separate what belonged together. He ignored the relation to one
another which Scripture assigns to the three legislative codes. To serve his evolutionary theory of
history, he assigned them to three different periods in Israel’s development as a nation.
威尔豪森如痴如醉地想要证明他那套讲论以色列宗教发展的教义理论，为此他选择将那
些彼此一体的内容刻意分开，对于圣经赋予这三份法典彼此之间的关系他也视而不见。为契

Wellhausen thought of the Israelites at the time they entered Canaan as uncultured desert
nomads. In the Book of the Covenant he therefore claimed to find the simple legislation which he
considered appropriate for Israel’s early history as a nation. He maintained that the brief, basic
precepts concerning worship (Ex 20:22–24) in the Book of the Covenant correctly characterized
Israel’s early religious practices. Interpreting verse 24 wholly out of its context, Wellhausen
contended that during this period the Israelites brought offerings to very simple altars of earth,
undressed stones at various cultic places throughout the country. He held that such offerings were
still spontaneous gifts on the part of the worshipers, that there was as yet no fixed ritual of sacrifices,
no central sanctuary, and no prescribed priesthood. This, Wellhausen asserted, was the normal state
of affairs until Josiah’s religious reform in 621 b.c. He boldly claimed that up to this time period,
there was no indication in the Old Testament historical books of laws which demanded one central
sanctuary or which gave precise regulations concerning prescribed sacrifices or a restricted
priesthood. When in reply numerous references to the full Mosaic Law were pointed out to him, his
invariable answer was that these references were insertions on the part of Deuteronomic and Priestly
redactors who reedited these historical books at a later date.

历史，尽管他承认其中的原始万物有灵论及拜物主义几乎已被伊罗兴典和耶和华典的编辑清
除净尽，他却依然宣称我们仍可以发觉它们的痕迹。威尔豪森甚至依然认为，JE 中的宗教
观更接近于单一主神教，而非完整的道德一神教。这也就是为何威尔豪森不愿意承认：出埃
及记 24:12 及 34:29 提到的两块石头上的命令就是那十句话（decalog）。13
According to Wellhausen, his third Pentateuch source, Deuteronomy (D), did not come into
existence until 621 b.c. during the reign of King Josiah of Judah. Second Kings 22, 23 informs us
how the Book of the Law (sepher hattorah) was found in the temple while it was being repaired.
On the basis of the rediscovered Torah, Josiah then inaugurated a thorough reformation in Judah,
abolishing idolatry and restoring the true worship of the Lord. Without warrant from the
aforementioned Scripture portions, Wellhausen, however, chose to identify the Book of the Law
with Deuteronomy. He contended that it had just been written for the express purpose of centralizing
Yahweh worship at Jerusalem in the strictly monotheistic form in which it had been promulgated by
the great prophets, especially Isaiah and Jeremiah. According to Wellhausen, Deuteronomy was
subsequently combined with the earlier JE document by Deuteronomic redactors, who at the same
time introduced various Deuteronomic insertions and revisions into Israel’s historical books.
根据威尔豪森，他的第三份摩西五经来源，即申命记典（D），直到公元前 621 年犹大
约西亚王执政期间才得以问世。列王纪下第 22、23 章告诉我们，当圣殿被维修时，有人在
其中发现了律法书（sepher hattorah）。基于这卷重新被人发现的妥拉，约西亚就在犹大展
开了一场彻底的革命，废除偶像，让百姓再次真正敬拜耶和华。然而，在没有上述经文段落

授权的情况下，威尔豪森便将律法书等同于申命记。他主张，申命记之所以被写下，是为明
确要求将敬拜耶和华的场所集中于耶路撒冷，且要严格按照一神教的形式，也就是按照几位
大先知（尤其是以赛亚和耶利米）曾宣讲的那样。根据威尔豪森，申命记典的编辑者们随后
就将申命记与早先的 JE 来源编辑在一处，与此同时，这些人还将各种申命记典插叙及修订
添入那些以色列历史书卷之中。
Wellhausen linked the publication of the final Pentateuch source, the Priestly Code (P), to the
events reported in Nehemiah 8–10, which he dated at 444 b.c. He contended that during the exile
the priests had given much thought and attention to the conduct of worship as it had been carried
out in the temple, especially after the Deuteronomic reform, and how it might even be improved
when the temple would again be restored. The exiled priest and prophet Ezekiel, he maintained, had
begun to put his reflections into writing as we have them in chapters 40–48 of his book of prophecy.
Other priests had attached themselves to Ezekiel and had drawn up the so-called Holiness Code, as
we now have it in Leviticus 17–26. “Thus there grew up in the exile from among the members of
this profession a kind of school of people who reduced to writing and to a system what they had
formerly practiced in the way of their calling. After the temple was restored, this theoretical zeal
still continued to work, and the ritual when renewed was still further developed by the action and
reaction on each other of theory and practice.”14 Wellhausen considered the Priestly Code to have
been the final result of this labor of many years. He proposed that under Ezra and Nehemiah, as
recorded in Nehemiah 8–10, the formal publication and adoption of the Priestly Code as the binding
Torah for Israel took place. Priestly redactors, he maintained, subsequently wove the Priestly Code
into the JE document and, together with Deuteronomy, our present Pentateuch was thereby formed.
Again, the text of Nehemiah 8–10, which Wellhausen used for his hypothesis, does not present the
Book of the Law, which was read to Israel, as something new. It should be kept in mind that
Deuteronomy and the Priestly Code, as conceived by Wellhausen, would actually be pious forgeries.
For in the Pentateuch, both Deuteronomy and the so-called priestly legislation are presented as given
by God to Israel through Moses. Wellhausen’s JEDP, however, found widespread acceptance both
throughout Europe and in America.15

Addendum
附录
by John C. Lawrenz

约翰·卡尔·劳伦斯
The critical denial of Mosaic authorship moved from German academic circles to England and
then to America in the late 1800s. The English scholar Samuel Davidson adopted the supplementary
hypothesis in 1862. Nineteen years later, despite a professed reverence for the Word of God and a
reputation for personal piety, the Scottish theologian W. Robertson Smith brought Wellhausen’s
views to the English-speaking world with boldness and thoroughness in his Introduction to the Old
Testament. A British namesake, one who traveled widely in the Middle East and is best known for
his groundbreaking work in the historical geography of the Holy Land, George Adam Smith,
assumed pre-Mosaic sources (Modern Criticism and the Teaching of the Old Testament, 1901). Two
English-speaking Hebrew lexicographers, one on each side of the Atlantic, lent their prestige to
negative higher criticism as well. They were the Oxford don Samuel Rolles Driver (An Introduction
to the Literature of the Old Testament, 1891) and the American scholar Charles A. Briggs (Biblical
Study, 1883; Messianic Prophecy, 1886; and Higher Criticism of the Hexateuch, 1893).
19 世纪晚期，否认摩西作者身份的批判之风从德国的学术圈儿吹到了英国，随后又吹到
了美国。1862 年，英国学者撒母耳·戴维森接受了补编假说。19 年之后，苏格兰神学家威
廉·罗伯逊·史密斯尽管自诩敬畏神的话语又以其敬虔而闻名遐迩，却在他的《旧约导论》
中大胆又彻底地将威尔豪森的观点引入那些以英语为口语的国家之中。乔治·亚当·史密斯，
这位与他同姓的英国人曾周游中东各地，他在圣地历史地理学上的开创性工作更是广为人
知，但他却提出了“资料来源早于摩西时代”的假说（《当代批判与旧约教导》，1901 年）。
同样，大西洋两岸的两位以英语为母语的希伯来语词典编纂者也因他们的消极高等批判而名
声大噪，他们分别是牛津大学的教师撒母耳·罗尔斯·德赖弗阁下（《旧约文学导论》，1891
年）以及美国学者查尔斯·奥古斯都·布里格斯（《圣经研究》，1883 年；《关于弥赛亚的
预言》，1886 年；《关于旧约首六卷的高等批判》，1893 年）。
By and large the English-speaking critics of the 19th century embraced the technical aspects of
the German theories while attempting at the same time to assert that the Bible was in some
marvelous way a revelation of God, capable of working and sustaining faith. In doing so, they set
the tone for the years that followed. A person may find rank unbelievers as well as sincere Christians
among those who believe and teach that Moses did not write the five books attributed to him. Today,
negative higher criticism remains a given rather than a hypothesis, this despite the fact that its four
pillars have been revealed as unscholarly by such Jewish scholars as Ezekiel Kaufmann and
Umberto Cassuto and such evangelical giants as Ernst Wilhelm Hengstenberg, Robert Dick Wilson,
Edward Young, and William Henry Green.
19 世纪那些以英语为母语的批判者大多一边欣然接受德国理论派的“工艺”，一边又宣
称圣经尚有奇妙之处，认为圣经算是神的启示，也能创造并坚固信心。这也成了之后很长一
段时间内的某种基调。这些相信又教导摩西并非摩西五经作者的人当中也许不乏极端的非信
徒，但其中却也有一些真诚的基督徒。如今，尽管消极高等批判法的四大支柱早已被——犹
太学者以西结·考夫曼和翁贝托·卡苏托，福音派巨人恩斯特·威廉·亨斯滕贝格、罗伯特·迪
克·威尔逊、爱德华·杨以及威廉·亨利·格林等人——揭露为伪学术，但是消极批判法早
已成了某种规矩而非假说。
What can we say today about Wellhausen’s assumptions?
那我们如今又该如何看待威尔豪森的假说呢？
Wellhausen’s first proof for multiple sources was different names for God. Wellhausen did not
have ready access to the wealth of ancient literature that we have today. Sophisticated writing went
on in the ancient world long before Moses. We find that long before Moses non-biblical authors
gave their pagan gods multiple names. The Babylonian chief god Marduk, for example, is given no
less than 50 names in the concluding portion of Enuma Elish, Babylon’s version of the creation
story. If the first chapter of Genesis opens our eyes to the sweep of God’s majestic creation of all
things, should it be strange that Moses chooses a Hebrew name for the Creator that stresses both
majesty and power? Neither should it be strange that God’s personal name Yahweh (Lord) is revealed
and used in Genesis 2 where the inspired Moses describes how the Creator personally, specifically,
and lovingly shaped the perfect, Godlike humanity of our first parents.
在威尔豪森看来，第一项支撑摩西五经由诸多资料来源构成的证据就是它采用了一些不
同的名字来称呼上帝。在威尔豪森那个时代，人们无法接触到许多我们如今已经拥有的古代
文学作品。复杂的文字内容在远早于摩西之前的时代便已存在。我们发现，在远早于摩西的
时代，那些非圣经作者也会给他们的异教神明取上好些名头。例如，巴比伦主神马尔杜克在
巴比伦版创世故事《埃努玛-埃利什》的结尾处就曾被冠以不少于 50 个名头。如果创世记的
第 1 章让我们看到了上帝创造万物的宏伟及大能，那么摩西选择用一个强调威严和能力的
希伯来语名字来称呼上帝又有何不妥呢？同样，受神默示的摩西在创世记第 2 章用“耶和
华”这个上帝专属名来称呼他，向我们展现创造主如何亲手满有慈爱地造了那两位完美的、
与神相似的、我们的始祖。
The second and third pillars of Wellhausian criticism, as he established them, were repetition
and internal contradiction. Honest scholars admit today that ancient storytellers had a different
palette from which to paint their stories than we have today. Today we employ punctuation,
underlining, bold, and italics, as well as such sophisticated devices as color, font style, layout, and
varying print size. Most of these were not available to Moses or the other ancient writers. Instead
they used word choices that opened and closed subsections. They imbedded words that
foreshadowed later topics and used words that echoed truths that had been previously stated. They
arranged chunks of text to build up a story, and then built the story back down in reverse order (the
chiastic AB:BA form). It was necessary for similar material to be repeated, and this was done in
varied ways, each way showing creativity rather than contradiction. On occasion, repetitions were
simply the equivalent of an exclamation point.
威尔豪森所建立的批判法的第二及第三根支柱就是他认为摩西五经内容重复又充满内
在矛盾。当今的学者若是有些诚实，也都承认古代的故事作者说起故事来与我们有些差别。
如今我们会使用标点符号、下划线、粗体、斜体以及诸如颜色、字体、排版、不同的打印尺
寸这些复杂的手段，但摩西或其他古代作者则多数都不会使用这些花招。相反，他们选用一
些特别的词汇来开启或结束分段。他们会埋设一些词语来预表后文出现的话题，也会使用一
些词语来回应前文陈述过的真理。他们先用一整块内容来构建一段故事，再以相反的次序返
回构造这段故事（即交叉结构，AB:BA 型）。相似的内容有必要以不同的方式被重复提及，
且其中的每种方式都是在展现其独创性而非前后矛盾。再者，重复的手法有时不过等同于现
在的感叹号而已。
Copies of the Bible were also few in number. As a result, the words of Moses were memorized
by the priests who then taught the people (see 2 Ch 15:3). Similarly, heads of families taught their
children (Dt 4:9) to memorize and recite Scripture out loud. A certain level of repetition is an aid to
memory.
圣经的抄本数量稀少。因此，祭司们会熟记摩西的话并教导百姓（历下 15:3）。与此相
仿，家庭带领人也会教导他们的儿女（申 4:9）熟记并大声背诵圣经。一定程度的重复有助
于记忆。
Finally, even apart from divine inspiration, no committee has ever composed literature such as
this that has captured the imagination of a people or shaped a people’s subsequent literary history.
We know the time and circumstances that gave us the writings of Virgil, Dante, Shakespeare, and
Goethe. We credit each man with his own work. For a while it was fashionable to treat Homer, who
lived not long after Moses, in the same way that higher critics treat the Bible. No longer. We know,
of course, that the Bible belongs to a class even higher than that of the world’s literary masterpieces;
the whole of Scripture is God-breathed. The Holy Spirit’s chosen “committee” were prophets and
apostles. He selected the writers, and they wrote. A wondrously gifted man wrote Genesis. It was
not crafted by an anonymous committee of cut-and-paste editors. Moses shows respect for ancient
storytelling on its own terms. His style of writing fits his era. He pays admirable attention to internal
structure and detail. He was not a committee at work. True to himself, he used repetition skillfully
as a tool. His selection of words reveal artistry, not internal contradiction.
最后，且不论神的默示，也从未有哪个委员会能够创作出这样能够引起一个民族遐想或
塑造一个民族后来文学史的文学作品。我们知道维吉尔、但丁、莎士比亚和歌德的作品诞生
的时代和环境，我们将他们各人的作品归于各人。一段时间内，用高等批判法对待圣经的方
式来对待摩西时代之后不久的那位荷马也成了一种风尚。但是，这种风尚已然消逝。当然，
我们知道，圣经却远远高于世界文学名著；整本圣经都是神所默示的，而圣灵所拣选的“委
员会”则是先知和使徒。他选好了作者，于是他们就开始书写。一位极富恩赐的人写下了创
世记。这卷书并非由一个匿名的“剪切-粘贴编辑委员会”精心制作。摩西用他自己的方式
向古代叙事方式展示了应有的尊重。他的写作风格符合他所处的时代。他对文本内部结构和
细节的关注令人钦佩。他不是某个委员会。他按照自己的方式熟练地将重复的表达方式用作
一种工具。他的选词所显示的是艺术性，而非文本内部存在的某种矛盾。
The fourth pillar upon which Wellhausen built his theory was that certain vocabularies and
certain styles reveal texts written at different times by different men. The P source, for example, is
said to be rich in genealogies, to love cultic ritual, and to revel in legalese. Source J, on the other
hand, excels in storytelling and is rich in everyday words, direct speech, and narrative style. Cassuto
and other scholars have pointed out that circular reasoning is at work in drawing such conclusions.
Word choices and sentence structures vary naturally depending on the subject being presented.
Different topics require distinct vocabularies. Who would quote a conversation and sketch a family
tree in the same way? Yet, when Moses describes sacrifices in Genesis, would we not expect him to
use the same priestly vocabulary with which he wrote Leviticus? Any parent uses one style when
setting out house rules, and another style when relating what happened at the office, and still another
style when praying out loud at the table.
威尔豪森用来建立其理论的第四根支柱则是，文本所使用的某些词汇和风格表明文本是
由不同作者于不同时期写成。例如，据说 P 来源包含了大量家谱，喜爱宗教仪式，陶醉于法
律用词。另一方面，J 来源则擅长叙事并富含诸多日常用语、直接引语以及叙事风格。卡苏
托和其他学者都已指出，得出这类结论都不过是在采用循环论证而已。文本的选词以及句子
的结构基于所呈现主题的不同自然也就会呈现出多样性。不同的话题需要不同的词汇来支
撑。谁又会在引用对话和勾勒家谱的时候采用相同的话术呢？那么，当摩西在创世记中描绘
献祭活动的时候，难道我们就不应该指望他会使用那些他在利未记里描绘祭司活动时所使用
的词汇吗？对于任何一位家长而言，当他在制定家庭规则的时候，他都会使用一种话术，当
他在处理工作事务的时候，他又会使用另一种话术，而当他在餐桌上祷告时，他的说话方式
肯定又会有所不同。
Critics have also tried to isolate words from non-Hebrew sources and use them to prove later
editing. They have argued that certain grammatical constructions are datable. If “old” and “new”
constructions appear in the same chapter, this, they say, proves multiple authorship. Certain
historical asides, such as the comment “to this day” in Genesis 22:14, they also say are fingerprints
left by a later editor.
批判者也尝试将非希伯来语的资料来源单独隔裂出来并用以证明后期编辑的说法。他们
提出，其中的一些语法结构有待商榷。他们声称，如果同一章节中同时出现“新旧”语法结
构，那么这就证明了摩西五经由多位作者写成。他们还声称，诸如“直到今日”（创 22:14）
这种历史旁白类的评注也是由某位后期编辑者留下的指纹。
Care must be taken not to overemphasize our knowledge of the vocabulary (Hebrew and
otherwise), grammar, and historical awareness of a man of Moses’ stature. In the New Testament
Stephen describes Moses as “educated in all the wisdom of the Egyptians” and “powerful in speech
and action” (Ac 7:22). Any prince in New Kingdom Egypt (ca. 1550 to ca. 1180 b.c.) was expected
to master every intellectual, literary, military, and practical science of the day. Why could Moses not
have selected words from more than one language base? Who is to say that some grammatical
constructs, rare in Hebrew, would not have been picked up from an extensive exposure to archives
drawn from other lands? Honest archaeologists work with the “compounded 3 percent rule.” Briefly
stated, what we moderns know for sure from archaeology depends on a 3 percent publishing of 3
percent of things actually dug from the ground. Additionally, what the archaeologist finds represents
but 3 percent of what survived from ancient times. And, archaeologists have done their work in only
3 percent of known sites. Certain Aramaic words, once thought to prove that a text was written after
Israel’s exile in Babylon, have now turned up in non-biblical inscriptions from hundreds of years
before the exile. A corollary to the compounded 3 percent rule is “absence of evidence is not
evidence of absence.”
我们必须谨慎，不要对自己在词汇（希伯来语以及其它语言）及语法的认识上自视过高，
也不要觉得自己对摩西这样一位人物知根知底。在新约中，司提反称，“摩西学了埃及人一
切的学问，说话行事，都有才能”
（徒 7:22）。在埃及新王国时期（约公元前 1550-1180 年），
王子都受到期许，需要精通当时各种知识及文学、军事及实践科学。摩西若是选用多种语言，
这又有何不可呢？谁敢说，一些希伯来语中很少见的语法结构不是由于大量接触那些从其它
国家收集而来的档案资料而获得的呢？诚实的考古学家按照他们的“复合 3%”原则开展工
作。简而言之，我们当代人从考古学中所获得的确切知识都是基于 3%的文物出版，而这些
文物又只占所有从地下挖出的遗存中的 3%。此外，考古学家所发现的东西也不过只能代表
那些古代遗存的 3%。再者，考古学家目前的工作地点也只限于 3%的已知遗址。有些亚兰语
词汇从前被视为证明某段文本写于以色列被掳巴比伦之后的证据，现在却又在被掳前几百年
的一些非圣经碑文中展露身影。故此，复合 3%原则带来的必然结论就是，“缺乏证据不等
于没有证据”。
Since all four of the pillars of negative higher criticism are flawed, it is not surprising that
consensus among higher critics themselves has broken down. The JEDP hypothesis dominated the
critical scene as recently as 1970. Since that time, theories have multiplied, resulting in a chaos of
conflicting views on the division and dating of texts. Critics confess that old assumptions have no
validity unless combined with other criteria. The tendency today is to study larger units of text and
treat their structure, rhetorical effects, and messages under such headings as “discourse analysis” or
“narrative analysis.” At the extreme end of those who treat the Bible as non-historical fiction are
men like John Van Seters, Thomas L. Thompson, and Hans Pieter Lemcke. Rolf Rentdorff, a
contemporary higher critic, has this to say: “Old Testament scholarship at present is ‘in crisis.’ The
Wellhausen paradigm no longer functions as a commonly accepted presupposition for Old
Testament exegesis. And, at present, no other concept is visible that could replace it. At the moment
there is no new model that could be expected to achieve common acceptance as a paradigm, and
there will probably be none in the near future.”
既然消极高等批判的四大支柱都有裂痕，也就难怪高等批判者彼此之间无法达成共识了。
直到 1970 年，JEDP 假说仍是批判界的主流。而自那以后，各种理论便开始纷纷涌现，继而
导致了对于文本分段及成书日期的各种互相矛盾的看法，场面极度混乱。批判者承认，原先
的那些假说若不和其它准则互相结合，就已经失效。今天的趋势就是研究更大篇幅的文本单
元，并在诸如“篇章分析”或“叙事分析”这类标题下研究它们的结构、修辞效果以及各类
信息。在那些将圣经视为非历史性小说的人物当中，约翰·范·塞特斯、托马斯·L·汤普
森以及汉斯·彼得·莱姆克就是几例极端。当代高等批判者罗尔夫·伦多夫如此说到：“旧
约学术界现在‘危在旦夕’。威尔豪森提出的样板如今已经不再作为旧约解经的预设而受到
广泛的接受。而我们目前也没有看到其它可以取而代之的理论。现在，没有新的样板符合大
家的预期并受到广泛的接受，并且近期也不会有。”
If Wellhausen’s paradigm died, many critical approaches have found nourishment from its
rotting corpse. One school followed another through the 1900s. Hermann Gunkel picked up on the
evolutionary aspect of critical theory. He and his followers postulated an early oral history for the
biblical text that he believed came to its present written form quite late. Bible stories are mere myths
or sagas that compare favorably with the old Nordic and Germanic legends. This school of Form
Criticism invited the reader to search for the truths behind the stories while treating biblical facts
with extreme skepticism. Sociological insights were used to analyze and reconstruct the Israelite
society of old. Yet form critics have not agreed on how oral and literary forms relate to one another,
thus leaving their considerable body of scholarly work short of compelling.
威尔豪森的样板一旦死去，那么许多批判法就会从它这具腐烂的尸体中得到滋养。贯穿
20 世纪，不同的学派一个接一个涌现在我们眼前，可谓“百花齐放”。赫尔曼·衮克尔拾起
了批判理论的进化观。他和他的跟随者提出假设，认为圣经文本的早期口述历史较晚才以书
面形式呈现，圣经故事也不过就是些比北欧和德国传说更受欢迎的神话传说而已。该形式批
判学派邀请读者一同寻找故事背后所隐藏的真理并秉持极端的怀疑态度看待圣经事实。他们
使用社会学知识来分析与重构古代以色列社会。然而，对于口述形式和文学形式之间的关系，
形式批判家们彼此之间并未达成共识，因此，他们大量的学术工作都缺乏说服力。
Albrecht Alt, Martin Noth, and Gerhard von Rad took biblical texts more seriously, yet they too
believed that the Masoretic text we have today was but the end product of a long, slow, and
continuous process of editing by anonymous reworkers of the text called redactors. The TraditioHistorical school has attempted to recapture the core truths of Israel’s early history by peeling its
onion away, one redactional layer at a time. Though much of this is highly speculative and open to
debate, the idea that redaction took place at all stages of the development of the text is now broadly
accepted.
相比之下，阿尔布雷希特·阿尔特、马丁·诺斯和格哈德·冯·拉德对待圣经文本的态
度则更为严肃些，但他们也相信，我们今天看到的马所拉抄本，只是一份经过漫长、缓慢、
持续不断的编辑过程之后才形成的最终产物，其编辑人员则是一群匿名的再加工者。传统历
史学派试图通过剥洋葱的方式，一次剥掉一层“编辑表皮”，重新找回以色列早期历史的核
心真相。尽管其中大部分说法在很大程度上只是猜测并存在争议，但是，认为圣经文本于其
各发展阶段被逐渐编辑而成的观点现在已经受到了广泛的采纳。
Form Criticism and Traditio-Historical Criticism were creations that sprang from academic
reflection. Field archaeology offered a counterweight as it developed and refined itself over the past
century. The most influential archaeologist of the 1900s was the American William F. Albright. He
was one of the first to excavate following rigorous and systematic standards. Albright and his pupils
believed that material artifacts confirmed the essential believability of the Bible in many of its
historical and factual details. The Albright school, however, was not afraid, ignoring the 3 percent
rule, to let archaeological insights judge the text. Israeli archaeologists, because of their familiarity
with both language and country, opened a fresh period of reexamination following the formation of
the State of Israel. Yet they have imitated Albright by making archaeology the arbiter of what the
Bible says. A second generation of Israeli archaeologists, led by Israel Finkelstein, has closed ranks
with those who believe little, if anything, can be known from the Bible about the peoples who lived
in the Holy Land during biblical times.
形式批判和传统历史批判都是学术反思的产物。而实地考古学则在过去一个世纪里不断
发展和完善的过程中为它们提供了某种平衡。20 世纪最有影响力的考古学家是美国人威
廉·福斯威尔·奥尔布赖特，他是最早在严格系统标准下挖掘考古的人之一。奥尔布赖特和
他的学生们相信，历史文物证实了圣经在许多历史和事实细节上的基本可信度。然而，奥尔
布赖特学派在无视 3%原则的情况下无惧采用考古学的见解来判断圣经文本。一些以色列考
古学家，由于他们对自己的语言和国家都颇为熟悉，便在以色列国成立后开启了一段重新审
视的全新时期。然而，他们也模仿奥尔布赖特，容让考古学成了圣经所言的仲裁者。有些人
认为我们几乎无法从圣经中得知圣经时代圣地百姓的生活状况，其中，以以色列·芬克尔斯
坦为首的第二代以色列考古学家正是他们的忠实伙伴。
New Criticism, as today’s version of higher criticism is called, places the focus squarely on the
text, setting aside the author’s intent as unknowable. So-called structuralists still cling to the
conviction that the text of the Bible can be objectively analyzed. Their deconstructionalist
counterparts debunk anything objective. They promote the anti-scientific, post-modern conclusion
that there is little or no objective knowledge to be recovered about anything from those who wrote
in the past.
新批评法，也就是今天所谓的高等批评法，将焦点直接置于文本之上，却将作者的意图
搁置一边，因为该批判法认为后者并不可知。那些所谓的结构主义者仍然坚信他们能够客观
地分析圣经文本。他们的对头——解构主义者——则会揭穿任何客观的事物，宣扬一种反科
学的后现代主义论调，即我们几乎无法从过去的作家那里获得任何客观的知识。
In an effort to give some shape to today’s critical confusion, terms like historicality are used to
salvage a sense that the Bible has something to say about Israel’s past. The hallowed Lutheran
principle that clear texts should be used to expound less clear passages exists as “intertextuality” in
the critical mind. Semiotics is the new science of searching words and texts to find the concepts
expressed through them. Semiotics often wanders far from any factual baseline as each scholar leads
his own expedition in search for meaning. More and more, the motifs of folklore and the oral
traditions of other societies have been brought to bear on the history and religion of ancient Israel.
Often the most primitive of modern peoples are cited as keys to unlock understanding. Political
insights have also been in vogue. George Mendenhall explained the emergence of the Israelite
people as a “peasant revolt.” Norman Gottwald used the paradigm of Marxist class struggle to
explain the same phenomenon. Similar approaches turn the Bible into a proving ground for modern
social trends. Texts are analyzed to bolster such things as radical feminism, liberation theology, and
the gay and lesbian agenda.
当今的批判主义给人造成了不少困扰，而正是为了修正这种困扰，历史性这类术语才应
运而生，被用来挽救人们对圣经的感知，即圣经确实记载了一些关于以色列的历史。路德宗
的神圣原则——即我们应使用清晰的经文解释模糊的经文——在批判者的头脑中不过就是
“文本互涉”。符号学是一门通过搜索文字和文本来寻找那藉它们所表达的概念的新兴科学。
符号学经常大大偏离事实底线，因为每位学者都在凭借自己的探索寻找意义。民间传说的主
题和其它社会的口头传统对古代以色列的历史和宗教施加了越来越多的影响。现代人那些最
粗糙的想法常常被视为打开理解之门的钥匙。把政治见解掺和进来也成了某种时尚。乔治·门
登霍尔将以色列民族的兴起解释为“农民起义”，诺曼·戈特瓦尔德则将马克思主义的阶级
斗争用作样板来解释该现象。与它们类似的那些说法都将圣经变成了论证现代社会走向的试
验基地。有些人挖空心思分析圣经文本，却是为了支持激进的女权主义、自由神学以及男女
同性恋议程。
In the end, all negative higher criticism, whoever its author, unites in blocking the road back to
Moses and biblical inerrancy. To negative higher critics, the Bible must be full of human errors.
Scripture is no better than mankind itself and usually much worse than the enlightened intelligentsia
of today. Any method to expose biblical “error” is deemed appropriate and scholarly. Quite often
the search is everything. In such a context, God’s truth is lost and what Wellhausen set in motion
still ruins.
最后，所有的消极高等批判，无论发起者是谁，都在一起阻碍着那条回到摩西作者身份
和圣经无误之上的道路。对于消极高等批评者而言，圣经必定处处都是人为的错误。圣经不
如人类本身，比起今天那些开明的知识分子更是差远了。任何揭露圣经“错误”的方法都被
视为合宜且具学术性的妙招。很多时候，探究就是一切。在这样的背景下，上帝的真理丢失
了，而威尔豪森所发起的运动却仍在产生着破坏。
The “assured results” of negative higher criticism remain false promises for all who believe that
God’s saving knowledge in Christ Jesus began with the book of Genesis. Jesus said something very
wise to the living, unbelieving brothers of the rich man who sat in hell while Lazarus rested on
Abraham’s bosom. Jesus said, “They have Moses and the Prophets; let them listen to them” (Lk
16:29). Jesus told his disciples, “Everything must be fulfilled that is written about me in the Law of
Moses, the Prophets and the Psalms” (Lk 24:44). Up until and through the Reformation, Christians
took what Moses said in Genesis at face value. Why? Because Jesus did. Anyone who wishes to
read Genesis with profit must do the same.
对于所有相信上帝在基督耶稣里赐下的救恩知识始于创世记的人来说，消极高等批评的
“确切结果”依然不过是些虚假的应许。当拉撒路躺在亚伯拉罕怀里的时候，耶稣指着那位
蹲在地狱里的财主的、那些尚且活着却又不信的弟兄们充满智慧地说到：“他们有摩西和先
知的话可以听从”（路 16:29）。耶稣对他的门徒则说到：“摩西的律法、先知的书和诗篇
上所记的，凡指着我的话都必须应验”（路 24:44）。在宗教改革之前以及整个宗教改革过
程中，基督徒都只按字面义相信摩西在创世记中所说的话。为什么？因为耶稣也那样。任何
人若想在阅读创世记的过程中获益，他也必须如此。
