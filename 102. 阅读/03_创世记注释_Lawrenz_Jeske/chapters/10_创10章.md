# 创世记第 10 章注释

创世记第 10 章The opening words of this chapter announce that Moses here begins another of the majordivisions of Genesis—the fourth of the ten toledoth. These are the ten accounts, ten mini-histories,which together relate The Early History of God’s Saving Activity.本章开篇便向读者宣布，摩西在此开启了创世记的又一段主要历史——十段 toledoth 中的第四段。这十段 toledoth 就是创世记的十段记载，十段微历史，它们共同编织成了上帝救恩作为的早期历史。The Fourth Toledoth: The Account of the Sons of Noah (10:1–11:9)第四段 Toledoth：挪亚儿子的历史（10:1–11:9）There are a number of factors which divide the nations of the world today. Racial antagonisms,religious differences, radically different political ideologies, barriers of language—these have atendency to pit nation against nation, to divide nations. Genesis 10, usually referred to as the “Tableof Nations,” is a needed and wholesome reminder of what unites all nations. It teaches that the entirehuman race has a common ancestry.当今世界各民族之间分歧颇多。种族间的彼此对立、宗教上的差异、截然不同的政治理念、语言障碍——这些往往都容易导致民族之间的相互对立，造成民族之间的分歧。通常被称为“列族表”的创世记第 10 章，是对我们有益的重要提醒，提醒着我们联结万族的纽带为何。这一章告诉我们，全人类拥有一位共同的祖先。This “forbiddingly genealogical” chapter consists of 70 names and doesn’t make for interestingreading. Luther admits that, evaluated superficially, the chapter “is seemingly barren and appears toserve no purpose.”1 But there is good reason why the Holy Spirit had precisely this informationinserted into the sacred record at precisely this point.这份“令人生畏的家谱”之章共列出了 70 个名字，读起来可不怎么有趣。路德表示，从表面来看，本章“似乎苍白无味，毫无意义。”1 但我们有充分的理由相信，圣灵必是特意将这部分特定的信息插入到这部分特定的圣经文本之中的。We have met Shem, Ham, and Japheth repeatedly in the preceding four chapters—as sons ofNoah, as occupants of the ark, as recipients of their father’s blessings and of his curse. But Genesis10 tells the subsequent history of these three brothers and describes God’s new roles for them. Theyare the ancestors of nations.
