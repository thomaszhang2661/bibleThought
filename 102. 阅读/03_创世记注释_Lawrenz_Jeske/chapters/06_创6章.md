By using this literaryfigure, Moses wants to emphasize the truth that God was aware of the dreadful and disgustingconditions on the earth he had created and that he took no action without being in possession of allthe pertinent facts in the case. “The Lord saw.” He saw not only the violent deeds of the race he hadcreated, he saw also the attitudes that motivated their deeds.通常而言，看见这个动词用在长眼睛的人身上倒是可以理解，但上帝是灵，他并没有眼睛。通过勾勒这种文学形像，摩西想要向我们强调一项事实，即上帝确实知道他所造的这片土地目前正处于怎样一种令人作呕的状态，以及其上正发生着怎样可怕的事情，并且，他也不会在没有得知所有相关事实的情况下采取任何行动。“耶和华看见。”他不但看见了他所造的人类作出的那些暴行，他也看见了推动这些暴行背后的动机。A second and perhaps even more surprising anthropomorphism is the statement that “the Lordrepented.” When the Bible predicates this action of a human being, it always indicates a doublechange in that person—a change of mind or purpose, followed by a change of action in keepingwith the change of mind. Can God change his mind? The very thought is frightening. “I the Lord donot change” God once announced through the prophet Malachi (3:6). Samuel told King Saul, “Hewho is the Glory of Israel … is not a man, that he should change his mind” (1 Sa 15:29). When, asin the passage before us, we are told that “the Lord repented,” we understand God is telling us he isabout to embark on a new course of action—without, however, changing his purpose announced toSatan in Eden (Ge 3:15). And this change of action is predicated of Yahweh, the Lord, theunchanging, faithful Savior-God. ## English Commentary

创世记第 6 章 “The Account of Adam” (Ge 5:1–6:8), the second of the ten accounts which make up the book of Genesis, began with some encouraging history for a believing child of God, listing ten of the Messiah’s ancestors. But as we are about to see, it closes on an unhappy note. The closing verses of this account (Ge 6:1–8) describe how the Sethite branch of Adam’s family despised its heritage and frittered it away, thereby calling down upon itself God’s awesome judgment of the flood. “亚当的历史”（创 5:1-6:8）——创世记十段历史中的第二段。这段历史先为我们列出 了弥赛亚的十位祖先，令上帝的信徒儿女深受鼓舞。但是，正如我们即将看到的，这段历史 的结尾部分（创 6:1-8）却叫人深感惋惜，那里谈及亚当所生的塞特一族如何轻视其基业并 将之白白浪费，从而招致上帝降下了大洪水的可怕审判。 Elohim (‫ )אֱֹלהִ ים‬is the almighty Creator-God, who designed his human creatures to live under him in a master-and-servant relationship. Genesis 6 records how this sovereign Lord and Master of the universe recognized that his creatures had rebelled against him and how he responded in judgment. This emphasis is most prominent throughout the flood narrative. But it is also consistent throughout the Scripture. “God cannot be mocked. A man reaps what he sows” (Gal 6:7). God’s white-hot holiness does not permit him to remain indifferent when rebellious creatures tell him: “Leave me alone!” The flood account in Genesis 6–8 illustrates, first of all, how a holy God responded to sin by pouring out his righteous anger on a disobedient human race hardened in unbelief. 伊罗兴（‫）אֱֹלהִּ ים‬是那位创造世界的全能神，他造了人类，叫他们居于他之下，与他形成 主仆关系。在创世记第 6 章中，摩西告诉我们这位宇宙的主宰如何意识到他的造物背叛了 他，他又如何以审判回应他们。该主题在洪水事件中尤为突出，且在整本圣经之中也具有其 一贯性。“神是轻慢不得的，人种的是什么，收的也是什么”（加 6:7）。当叛逆的受造物 向他犟嘴说“别管我”时，上帝那炽热的圣洁就不会容许他自己继续保持冷漠。创世记第 6 到第 8 章中的洪水事件首先向我们表明了一位圣洁的上帝如何回应罪恶——他将他公义的愤怒 全然倾泻在一群刚硬不信、顽梗悖逆的人类身上。 But there is a second and parallel thread running through the flood narrative and, specifically, throughout the eight verses which close out “The Account of Adam’s Line.” This is indicated by a second divine name Moses uses when describing God’s response to the apostasy of Seth’s descendants. Five times in verses 1–8 Moses uses the name Yahweh (‫)יְ הוָה‬, God’s Old Testament Savior name. (This name for the God who had made a solemn contract, a covenant of grace with the human race, is translated “Lord” in our English Bibles, in contrast to “Lord,” the translation for the Hebrew name Adonai [‫]אֲדֹ נַי‬.) Yahweh is the God of absolute independence, the great “I am” (Ex 3:14), and the God of absolute constancy (Ex 34:5–7). When reading the opening verses of Genesis 6, it is important for us to recognize that it is Yahweh, the Savior-God, who “saw how great man’s wickedness on the earth had become” (v. 5) and who announced: “My Spirit will not contend with man forever.… I will wipe mankind … from the face of the earth” (vv. 3, 7). 但在这段洪水记载中，还有另一条并行的脉络贯穿始终，尤其是在“亚当的历史”的最

后 8 节经文之中（即 6:1-8）。关于这一点，我们从摩西使用的第二个用来称呼上帝的名字 中就可以看出。当摩西在描写上帝如何回应塞特后裔的不信与悖逆之时，他就使用了这个名 字。‫יְ הוָה‬，这个在旧约中用来称呼救主上帝的名字，摩西在第 1-8 节中共使用了 5 次之多。

‫יְ הוָה‬是那位完全自主的上帝，那位伟大的“我是”（出 3:14），那位绝不改变的上帝（出 34:5-7）。当我们阅读创世记第 6 章的开头几节经文时，我们务必要晓得这一点，即，是‫יְ הוָה‬， 这位救主上帝“见人在地上罪恶很大”（第 5 节）并宣告说：“我的灵不再永远在他里面争 战......我要将所造的人......从地上除灭”（第 3,7 节）。 It’s highly significant that Moses alternates the use of the divine names, emphasizing two widely differing emotions—opposite emotions, from our point of view—in the heart of God. The judgment, which a holy and offended Creator sent on his rebellious creation, at the same time served in the interest of his saving grace. The judgment which brought God’s vengeance on obdurate sin and unbelief served also as a deliverance for God’s believing children. In perfect independence, but in absolute constancy, God tempered the exercise of his judgment; he held back his hand so that his children would be benefited. In spite of Satan and in spite of the loathing his straying children had for the Father’s Word and will, there would be a family of God. The church would survive; Yahweh would see to that. 尤其值得我们注意的是，摩西交替使用上帝的那些称谓，用它们强调上帝心中两种截然 不同的情感——从我们的角度来看，也是两种完全相反的情感。一位圣洁且被得罪的创造主 对那些悖逆他的受造物发出审判，但他的审判同时却又服务于他的救恩。上帝向顽固的罪恶 与不信施行报复，这是他的审判，但这审判对于那些相信上帝的儿女来说却是拯救。上帝对 顽固的罪恶和不信所施行的审判，也为上帝的信奉者带来了解脱。完全自主的上帝却又绝不 改变，于是他未急着发出审判；他收回自己那双快要施罚的手，好叫让他的百姓可以受益。 尽管撒旦已然猖獗，尽管那些背离天父话语和旨意的儿女令他厌恶，但地上仍会有一个家庭 属于上帝。神的教会将得保存；‫יְ הוָה‬必会确保此事。 It’s important that we understand this double truth about God’s judgment, a truth which will be valid down to the end of the world. In the New Testament Jesus pointed to the flood as the great prototype of the final judgment: “Just as it was in the days of Noah, so also will it be in the days of the Son of Man. People were eating, drinking, marrying and being given in marriage up to the day Noah entered the ark. Then the flood came and destroyed them all” (Lk 17:26, 27). 理解关乎上帝审判的这一双重真理对我们而言尤为重要，这真理必将一直有效，直到世 界末日。在新约中，耶稣将大洪水称为指向末日审判的重要预表：“挪亚的日子怎样，人子 的日子也要怎样。那时候的人又吃又喝，又娶又嫁，到挪亚进方舟的那日，洪水就来，把他 们全都灭了”（路 17:26-27）。 In his final judgment on the last great day, God punishes sinners and unbelievers while, at the same time, God delivers his believers. The beating heart of Lutheran theology is this distinction, this tension, between law and gospel. Only through this central teaching of the Scripture will the sinner be able to curb his sinful nature, while his regenerate nature finds the comfort God wants him to have. 当那大而可谓的最后审判日来临之时，上帝必将惩罚罪人与非信徒，但与此同时，上帝 也必将拯救那些信他的人。路德宗神学的心脏就是律法与福音之间的这一区分与张力。唯独

It’s unfortunate that some unhappy chapter divisions in our Bibles obscure this fact. This heading may very likely not be the one we would have chosen for a section which describes the nightmare of divine judgment which once engulfed the ancient world. But it is the heading which the Holy Spirit chose for this section of his book, and with good reason. In this third of the ten accounts which describe the early history of God’s saving activity, the emphasis is not on the global catastrophe or the geological convulsions or the human tragedy caused by the flood. This is Noah’s story: 摩西在这一章节开头写到： “这是挪亚的历史”。他用十个关键句为创世记定下了大纲， 而这句话正是其中的第三句。遗憾的是，我们手头的圣经版本并不总能正确地划分章节。此 处便是一例，并且这样错误的划分导致原本的行文结构变得让人难以辨认。上帝的审判吞没 了古代世界，而“这是挪亚的历史”这个标题在人看来则不太符合这场噩梦给人的印象。但 这恰是圣灵为他这册书的这部分内容所选定的标题，而且他有足够的理由这么做。创世记的 十段内容为我们展示的是上帝救恩作为的早期历史，而这第三段的重点也不在于这场全球性 的灾难、这次大地的震动或是这场洪水所导致的人类悲剧。以下便是关于挪亚的故事： • the details of God’s covenant with him, • 上帝与他立约的一些细节。 • a description of his character, and • 他所具有的品质， • the account of his miraculous preservation. • 上帝用奇妙的方式使他得保全之事。 Moses gives us a capsule description of Noah in three particulars. First of all, “Noah was a righteous man.” This is the first occurrence of the important Old Testament term ‫צַ דִ יק‬. Most commentators see the term here as no more than a reference to the fact that Noah lived an upright life in a godless and wicked society. That is not, however, how Moses defines the term. In Genesis 15:6 he says this about Abraham: “Abraham believed the Lord, and he credited it to him as righteousness.” In God’s accounting, Abraham was righteous not because of the upright life he lived, but only because God declared him to be righteous. ‫ צַ דִ יק‬is a forensic term, a courtroom term. God had promised Abraham that his great Descendant would do everything necessary to rescue a whole world of sinners from God’s curse, from self-serving lives, and from the lordship of Satan. Abraham believed that promise (although at the moment he and his beloved Sarah were childless),

and through this faith Abraham was pronounced righteous. God considered him to be one who had met God’s requirements; he was right with God. God had spoken his verdict for Christ’s sake, and his verdict on Abraham was “Approved!” The apostle Paul supplies this commentary: “The words ‘it was credited to him’ were written not for [Abraham] alone, but also for us, to whom God will credit righteousness—for us who believe in him who raised Jesus our Lord from the dead” (Ro 4:23, 24). 摩西从三个方面为我们简要地描述了挪亚这个人物。首先，“挪亚是一个义人。” ‫צַ דִ יק‬ 这个重要的旧约词汇在这里首次出现。大多数评论家认为此词不过表明了挪亚在一个不信和 邪恶的世界之中过着某种正直的生活而已。然而，这却并非摩西对此词的定义。在创世记 15:6 中，他用这个词这样描述亚伯拉罕：“亚伯拉罕信耶和华，耶和华就以此为他的义。”上帝 之所以将亚伯拉罕视为义人，并非因为他过着某种正直的生活，而唯独因为上帝宣告他为义 人。‫צַ דִ יק‬是一个法律术语，一个法庭用语。上帝曾向亚伯拉罕应许，他那位伟大的后裔定 将尽其一切所能拯救整个罪恶的世界脱离上帝的诅咒、脱离专为自我的生活、脱离撒旦的辖 制。亚伯拉罕信了这一应许（尽管当时他和他心爱的妻子撒拉尚未得子），并且正是藉着这 信，亚伯拉罕被上帝宣告为义人。因基督的缘故，上帝已经作出了他的裁决，而他对亚伯拉 罕的裁决是——“我认可他”！使徒保罗对此这样评论到：“算为他义的这句话，不是单为 他（亚伯拉罕）写的，也是为我们将来得算为义之人写的。就是我们这信神使我们的主耶稣 从死里复活的人”（罗 4:23,24）。 Now make the application to Noah. The epistle to the Hebrews equates Noah’s righteousness with Abraham’s in the words: “By faith Noah … became heir of the righteousness that comes by faith” (Heb 11:7). Like all Old Testament believers, Noah was saved not merely by a generic faith in a merciful God. Although that may sound good, it is not enough because it is not tied to Christ. Like Abraham, Noah was saved by faith in the woman’s Seed, who God promised would crush Satan’s head and free his slaves. Noah also believed God’s promise to destroy the unbelieving world and to preserve his descendants. In the dim corridors of the great craft, surrounded by destruction and desolation, they had opportunity to do meaningful, never-ending chores by which they might obey God’s commandment and meet the needs of waiting, powerless, grateful, dependent creatures. 从他交待给挪亚的这项任务中，我们可以看到上帝的良善与智慧。挪亚及其家人在方舟 里度过的这 12 个月，定会在精神和情感上都感到极为煎熬。他们脑海中不断浮现着这场突 如其来的可怕变故，这种思绪很可能极大地占据着他们清醒的时刻。因此，上帝给了他们一 些工作去做，虽然劳苦，但却于他们有益。破坏与荒凉环绕着他们，但在这艘巨型方舟的昏 暗走廊内，他们却有机会去忙着做一些有意义的事情，藉此遵行上帝的命令，让那些翘首等 待、只能依赖着他们、对他们满怀感激的动物得到基本的满足。 “Take every kind of food which is eaten and store it away; it will be food for you and for them.” This command does not preclude the possibility that God may have supplemented Noah’s efforts to feed the animals. We know that animals adapt their food intake to their needs. A brooding hen doesn’t eat as much as a chicken with the run of the barnyard. The enforced confinement and reduced light aboard the ark may very well have caused the animals to sleep more than normal. A hibernating bear eats very little or nothing during the winter months. The apostle Peter calls Noah “a preacher of righteousness” (2 Pe 2:5). He preached law and gospel not only with his mouth, but with his chalkline and level, his hammer and saw as well. Hebrews 11 gives us the secret of Noah’s remarkable life: his faith, his absolute trust in what God had promised. By including him in illustrious company like Abraham and Moses, the writer to the Hebrews emphasized that it was faith in God’s promise that led Noah to say what he said and to do what he did. “Noah, when warned about things not yet seen, in holy fear built an ark.… By his faith he … became heir of the righteousness that comes by faith” (Heb 11:7).
