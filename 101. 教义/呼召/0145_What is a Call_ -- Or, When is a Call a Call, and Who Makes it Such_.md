# 《什么是呼召？》中文逐句翻译

**原作者：Alfred H. Maaske**

---

## 第一页

**What is a call?: or, When is a call a call, and who makes it such?** 什么是呼召？或者说，呼召何时才算是呼召，又是谁赋予它这一意义？

**By Alfred H. Maaske** 作者：Alfred H. Maaske

---

In all our textbooks on dogmatics and other literature of a confessional nature, our Wisconsin Synod as well as our entire Synodical Conference has always stated that we, in conformity with our Lutheran Confessions, regard ordination as being a solemn public confirmation of the call. 在我们所有的教义学教科书以及其他信仰告白性质的文献中，我们的威斯康星会众（Wisconsin Synod）以及整个会众联合会（Synodical Conference）始终声明：我们遵从路德宗信仰告白，将按立（ordination）视为对呼召的一种庄严公开确认。

Therefore, even though the rite of ordination, in itself, is an adiaphoron, yet it is just as important that this rite be retained, as it is important that marriage ceremonies continue to be performed. 因此，尽管按立仪式本身属于"非要道事项"（adiaphoron，即可有可无之事），但保留这一仪式的重要性，与继续举行婚礼仪式的重要性是同等的。

In regard to marriage, it is the consensus that makes the marriage; in regard to the ministry, it is the call that makes the minister; but the respective ceremonies publicly acknowledge this. 在婚姻中，是双方的合意（consensus）成就了婚姻；在教会事奉中，是呼召成就了牧师；而各自的仪式只是公开地确认了这一事实。

The question now arises, however, "What is a call?" or "When is a call a call, and who makes it such?" 然而，现在出现了这样一个问题："什么是呼召？"或者"呼召何时才算是呼召，又是谁赋予它这一意义？"

---

## 合法的呼召（The Legitimate Call）

In the days when our Lutheran confessions were formulated and for at least three and one half centuries thereafter, a legitimate call was always understood on this wise: 在我们路德宗信仰告白形成之时，以及此后至少三个半世纪里，合法的呼召始终被理解为如下形式：

1) The man considered for service in the church, was trained, if necessary, by competent authorities of the whole church. 1）被考虑在教会中服事的人，若有必要，须由全体教会的合格权威机构对其进行培训。

2) He was then examined by competent authorities of the whole church, and declared fit for service in the church. 2）随后他须接受全体教会合格权威机构的考核，并被宣告有资格在教会中服事。

At the same time, the man himself declared his intention to remain loyal to the Lutheran Confessions. 与此同时，该人本人须声明他愿忠于路德宗信仰告白。

3) Hereupon he was ordained in some assembly representing the whole church and by competent and authorized representatives of the whole church. 3）此后，他须在某个代表全体教会的集会中，由全体教会具备资格且经授权的代表为其行按立礼。

4) He was then given his specific charge or appointment, and installed into it by competent authorities of the whole church. 4）然后，他会被分配到具体的职责或任职岗位，并由全体教会的合格权威机构将其安置其中。

If this happened to be a local congregation (Pfarramt), he was usually placed there by the consistories. 若该岗位恰好是某地方会众（德语：Pfarramt，即堂区牧职），他通常由教会监理会（consistories）安置于此。

Local congregations did not issue "calls" in the manner in which we now do in America. 地方会众并不以我们现今在美国所通行的方式发出"呼召"。

---

Thus the call, in reality, was consummated by the whole church, and his eventual placement or appointment was only a minor part of that call. 因此，呼召在实质上是由全体教会所完成的，而某人最终的安置或任命，不过是该呼召中的一个次要部分。

In fact placements and appointments as well as transfers from one specific field of labor to another, were regarded as being rather a result of the actual call, than an essential part thereof. 事实上，安置、任命以及从一个具体服事岗位调往另一岗位，都被视为实际呼召的结果，而非呼召的本质组成部分。

Certainly they were never regarded as being exclusively the only essential feature of the call. 这些事项当然从未被视为呼召唯一的本质特征。

The call was considered as being already virtually complete before the specific appointments. 呼召在具体任命之前，就已被视为实质上完整的了。

That specific appointments would follow was taken as a matter of fact. 具体任命随后跟进，被视为理所当然之事。

For that reason also, ordination often took place prior to a man's specific appointment, and never was repeated, no matter how many times he was transferred thereafter. 正因如此，按立往往在某人获得具体任命之前就已进行，此后无论调任多少次，按立皆不重复举行。

---

A few statements from early Lutheran Theologians will show that the call was understood thus: 以下几位早期路德宗神学家的论述将表明，呼召确实是如此被理解的：

Johann Gerhard, 1582-1637; "Erztheologe;" "der unbestritten groeste Vertreter der aelteren Lutherischen Dogmatik:" "Private individuals may exercise the duties of a pastor in a congregation which has no legitimate pastor." "...if no legitimate servant of the church is available." 约翰·格哈德（Johann Gerhard，1582—1637），"首席神学家"，"无可争议的旧路德宗教义学最杰出代表"："私人个体可以在没有合法牧师的会众中履行牧师的职责。" "……当没有合法的教会仆人可供使用之时。"（参见华尔特《教会与职分》1852年版，第380页）

Chemnitz, 1522-1586: "The manner in which one is legitimately called as a servant of the church, is also the manner in which one is deposed, or transferred from one congregation to another... And, as in the case of calling, so also in the case of deposition, is the whole church concerned therewith, in due order." 开姆尼茨（Chemnitz，1522—1586）："一个人被合法呼召为教会仆人的方式，也是他被罢免或从一个会众调往另一会众的方式……正如在呼召的情形中，在罢免的情形中，全体教会也依照适当的秩序与此事相关。"（《教会与职分》第412—413页）

F. Balduin (—1626, Wittenberg): "It is our opinion that ordination concerns the whole church, for she (the whole church) calls and ordains servants (of the church), which right she exercises through the 'ministerium.' Ordination is not an act of the ministerium, but of the whole church, and can be performed even by a corrupt ministerium." F·鲍尔丁（F. Balduin，卒于1626年，威登堡）："我们认为，按立涉及全体教会，因为她（全体教会）呼召并按立教会的仆人，这一权利她通过'事奉团体'（ministerium）来行使。按立不是事奉团体的行为，而是全体教会的行为，即便是一个腐败的事奉团体也可以执行按立。"（《教会与职分》第101—102页）

Heshusius, 1527-1588: "In cases of dire necessity, where it is impossible to obtain legitimately called servants of the Word, there is no doubt that every individual Christian has the power (Macht habe), and is empowered by the Word of God in accordance with the principle of Christian love, to exercise the ministry, including the proclaiming of the Word and the administration of the Sacraments. 赫休修斯（Heshusius，1527—1588）："在极度紧迫的必要情形下，若不可能获得合法受呼召的圣道仆人，则毫无疑问，每一位基督徒个体都有能力（Macht habe），并被上帝的话语按照基督之爱的原则所授权，来执行事奉职分，包括传讲圣道和施行圣礼。

When we speak here of a case of dire necessity, we mean this: when it is impossible to obtain legitimate (rechtschaffene) and true servants of the church ... 我们在此所说的极度必要情形，是指这样的情况：当不可能获得合法（rechtschaffene）且真实的教会仆人之时……

what a Christian may do in such a case, as when a number of Christians are in a place where there is no appointed shepherd (bestellter Seelsorger) anywhere available. 在这种情形下一位基督徒可以做什么，譬如当一些基督徒身处某地，而周围任何地方都没有受任命的牧者（bestellter Seelsorger）可以寻得。

...I do not say that two or three Christians have the right to sever their connections with the true church, and to despise the appointed and legitimate pastors, and to create factions (Rotten anrichten), but in cases of dire necessity, when there are either no pastors available, or when those who are available disseminate false doctrine and must be avoided." ……我并非说两三位基督徒有权与真实的教会断绝关系，轻视受任命的合法牧师，并制造派别（Rotten anrichten），而是说在极度必要之时，当没有牧师可以寻得，或当现有的牧师传播错误教义而必须避开之时。"（《教会与职分》第371—373页）

Luther: "And the same insignificant bishop or pastor, St. Augustine consecrated and ordained many pastors and bishops which were sought for and called by other cities, in his own little congregation (there being as yet no consecrating or ruling bishops ...all being at that time simple pastors), just as we may ordain and send pastors out of our congregation in Wittenberg to other cities which desire them and have none of their own." 路德："同样这位微不足道的主教或牧师，圣奥古斯丁在他自己小小的会众中，为被其他城市寻求和呼召的许多牧师和主教行了祝圣与按立之礼（彼时尚无主持祝圣之主教或治理主教……那时人人都只是普通牧师），正如我们可以从威登堡的会众中按立牧师，并差派他们前往其他渴望他们、却没有自己牧师的城市。"（《教会与职分》第331页）

It is evident, therefore, that when the confessional writings of the Lutheran Church state that ordination is a confirmation of the call, they refer to the call of the church at large, and not to the specific placements and appointments. 因此可以清楚看出，当路德宗教会的信仰告白文件声明按立是对呼召的确认时，它们所指的是全体教会的呼召，而非具体的安置与任命。

## 第二页

## 美国路德宗对呼召的理解（会众联合会）

## American Lutheran concept of the call (Synodical Conference)

Nevertheless, our synods continued to function very much in the same manner as the Lutheran Churches-at-large in Germany had done ever since the days of Luther. 尽管如此，我们的会众（synods）继续以与自路德时代以来德国路德宗整体教会大体相同的方式运作。

They sought to interest gifted young men to study for the ministry. 他们致力于引导有天赋的年轻人研读神学、为事奉做准备。

After being trained, these men were examined and, being found fit for the ministry, were then placed into specific fields of labor. 经过培训后，这些人接受考核，若被认定适合事奉，便被安置到具体的服事岗位。

Candidates were even assigned to local congregations at the discretion of assignment committees. 候选人甚至由分配委员会酌情分派至地方会众。

After thus placing ministers into their first specific charge, the synods, through their representatives, still claimed and usually exercised the right of supervising and virtually governing transfers from one specific field of labor to another. 在以此方式将牧师安置到其首个具体职责之后，会众仍通过其代表宣称并通常行使监督权，乃至实际主导从一个具体服事岗位调任至另一岗位的事务。

Usually only such men were transferred from one congregation to another whose name appeared on synodical lists. 通常只有名字出现在会众名册上的人，才会从一个会众调往另一个会众。

In a few exceptional cases, local congregations "called" men of their own choosing, although they usually incurred some measure of censure and disrespect for having disregarded synodical advice and for having acted contrary to the laws of love and order. 在少数例外情况下，地方会众会"呼召"自行选定的人，但这样做通常会因无视会众的建议、违背爱与秩序的法则而受到一定程度的批评和轻视。

Yet, even in such cases, synods did not institute disciplinary action against the congregations, since the men whom they chose usually were men belonging to the synod, whose gifts and fitness were known. 然而，即便在这些情况下，会众也不会对地方会众采取纪律处分，因为他们所选之人通常是隶属于会众的人，其恩赐与资格已为人所知。

However, if congregations arbitrarily called someone who was unorthodox or otherwise unfit, our synods did not tolerate such a situation. 然而，若地方会众擅自呼召不合正统或在其他方面不适合的人，我们的会众则不会容忍这种情况。

The synods further claimed and exercised the right to depose pastors who became unfit. 会众还进一步宣称并行使罢免不适任牧师的权利。

---

Also in regard to ordination, the practice was much the same as it had been. 在按立方面，惯例与从前大体相同。

Men were ordained upon their graduation from the seminary or following a successful colloquy. 人们在神学院毕业时或通过成功的信仰对话（colloquy）后接受按立。

In a great number of cases they were ordained in their home congregation, although the pressure of those who regarded that as being wrong, often caused many to be ordained in their first local congregation. 在许多情况下，他们在自己的本堂会众中接受按立，尽管那些认为此举有误之人的压力，常常导致许多人改在其首个地方会众中接受按立。

Moreover, the ordination could not be performed except upon authorization of the proper synodical officials. 此外，按立只有在获得适当的会众官员授权后才能进行。

In some cases, candidates were ordained en masse at seminary graduation ceremonies. 在某些情况下，候选人在神学院毕业典礼上集体接受按立。

---

All this seems to indicate that there was an underlying instinct born of the scriptures and of the Holy Spirit that the European Lutheran concept of the Church and of the ministry and of the call and of ordination was essentially correct. 这一切似乎表明，有一种源于圣经和圣灵的内在本能，使人认识到：欧洲路德宗对教会、事奉职分、呼召与按立的理解，在本质上是正确的。

And it was. 确实如此。

The only trouble in Europe was that the church in Europe had become hopelessly entangled in the meshes of the State, and for that reason could not function as it should. 欧洲唯一的问题在于，欧洲的教会已不可救药地纠缠于国家的网罗之中，因而无法按其应有的方式运作。

Having forfeited its rights to the state, the church became hamstrung by the state. 教会将自身的权利拱手让给国家，遂被国家所束缚。

The practice of our Synodical Conference, therefore, was mainly correct, although its theoretical stand in regard to the above-mentioned doctrines was not, and this inconsistency became the source of many of our troubles. 因此，我们会众联合会的实践在总体上是正确的，尽管其在上述教义方面的理论立场并不正确，而这一矛盾成为我们诸多麻烦的根源。

---

In spite of the fact that our practice was correct in most instances, there were many who held that the local congregation alone has the right to issue a call, that only after receiving such a call may a man be ordained, and that the ordination may take place only in the congregation which first gives him such a call. 尽管我们的实践在大多数情况下是正确的，仍有许多人坚持认为：只有地方会众才有权发出呼召，一个人只有在接受这样的呼召之后才可以接受按立，且按立只能在首先向他发出呼召的会众中举行。

They were then also forced to explain ordination on this wise, that ordination is in reality nothing more than the first installation, without making any attempt to explain why the first installation should be any different from the others. 他们于是也被迫如此解释按立：按立实际上不过是第一次就职安装（installation），却完全不尝试解释为何第一次就职安装与其后的任何一次有所不同。

---

This confusion regarding the call also resulted in other absurd inconsistencies. 这种关于呼召的混乱还导致了其他荒谬的矛盾。

For example, men who entered the service of the Church as professors or missionaries could not be ordained. 例如，以教授或宣教士身份进入教会服事的人无法接受按立。

Men who had so-called "temporary calls" also could not be ordained, even though they had just as much of a permanent call from the church-at-large as other men who were ordained. 持有所谓"临时呼召"的人也无法接受按立，即便他们从全体教会所得到的永久性呼召与其他已被按立之人一样充分。

Unordained men were often said to be ineligible to synodical offices, and in order to make them eligible, they were given calls as assistant pastors to local congregations so that they might be ordained. 未受按立者常被认为没有资格担任会众职务，为使其具备资格，便给他们发出担任地方会众助理牧师的呼召，以便对其进行按立。

Another example; men who ought to have been transferred to another congregation were often conscience-bound to wait until some local congregation called them, and oftentimes conscientious men who minded their own business in their own congregation, had to wait a lifetime since they were not known in other congregations. 另一个例子是：本应调往另一会众的人，往往出于良心约束而等待某个地方会众呼召他们；而那些恪尽职守、专注于本堂事务的诚实之人，则常常要等上一辈子，因为他们在其他会众中并不为人所知。

They were told to wait until the Lord called them, but since the Lord no longer calls immediately but rather mediately through men, and since the men who were responsible often failed to assume responsibility, such pastors who needed a change, were left to languish where they were. 他们被告知要等候主的呼召，但由于主如今不再直接呼召，而是藉着人来间接呼召，而那些负有责任的人又常常未能承担起责任，这些需要改变的牧师便被遗留在原地，日复一日地消磨下去。

Others made it their business to become known and engineered calls for themselves. 另一些人则千方百计地让自己为人所知，并为自己谋取呼召。

In general, I believe it will be conceded that the matter of calls and ordination have caused much ill-will and confusion among us, which could have been avoided, had our stand in such matters been more in keeping with the spirit of Scripture. 总体而言，我相信大家都会承认，关于呼召与按立的问题在我们当中造成了许多不满与混乱，倘若我们在这些事务上的立场更符合圣经的精神，这些本可以避免。

## 第三页

For it is a legalistic view, entirely contrary to Scriptures to teach and believe that a local congregation alone is a true church with the sole right to call. 因为，教导并相信只有地方会众才是真实的教会、才有唯一的呼召权，这是一种律法主义的观点，完全违背圣经。

It is further contrary to Scriptures to teach and believe that a call from a local congregation alone makes the minister and gives him the right to be ordained a minister. 进一步说，教导并相信只有来自地方会众的呼召才能成就牧师、并赋予他接受按立为牧师之权利，同样违背圣经。

---

A visible church is a church not because of the manner in which it is organized, but because it is a group of people having a common confession of faith. 有形教会之所以是教会，不是因为其组织方式，而是因为它是一群拥有共同信仰告白的人。

That makes a synod a church in the same manner as it makes a local congregation a church. 这使得一个会众（synod）以与地方会众成为教会相同的方式成为教会。

The law of brotherhood and love confers upon any body united by a common confession the authority to function as a church in every respect, regardless of legalistic pronouncements to the contrary. 弟兄之情与爱的律法，赋予任何因共同告白而联合的团体在各方面作为教会运作的权柄，无论律法主义的宣告对此有何异议。

---

And what constitutes a call according to Scriptures? 那么，按照圣经，什么构成一个呼召呢？

I know of no iron-clad Scriptural restrictions giving any definite organized body the sole right to call. 我不知道有什么不可动摇的圣经限制，将呼召的唯一权利赋予任何特定的有组织团体。

On the contrary, when any group of Christians united by a common confession, recognizes a man as being gifted of God and fit for the ministry of the Word, and by the guidance of the Holy Spirit, lets that man know in one way or another that his services are desired, and he lets it be known that he is willing to render such services, thus creating a sort of unwritten contract, that is a call. 恰恰相反，当任何因共同告白而联合的基督徒群体，认定某人蒙上帝赐予恩赐、适合圣道事奉，并在圣灵的引导下，以某种方式让该人知道他的服务是被渴望的，而他也表明自己愿意提供这样的服务，从而形成一种无言的契约——这便是一个呼召。

That is what a synod does when it finds, trains and recommends men for the ministry; and in order to make the unwritten contract binding, the ordination ceremony publicly confirms the whole church's call. 这正是一个会众在发掘、培训并推荐人才从事事奉时所做的事；而为了使这无言的契约具有约束力，按立仪式公开确认了全体教会的呼召。

The consensus is publicly confirmed by the ceremony. 这一共识通过仪式得到公开确认。

---

This I believe the correct Lutheran understanding of ordination and the Scripturally sound use thereof. 我相信，这是对按立的正确路德宗理解，也是对其圣经上合理的运用。

**Alfred H. Maaske** **艾尔弗雷德·H·马斯克**
