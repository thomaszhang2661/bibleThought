### Communion: Remembering the Blood on the Mercy Seat

If baptism is a one-time "putting on" of Christ, communion is the **regular reminder** that you're still covered.

### Why We Need Communion

Here's the problem: We forget.

- You sinned again
- You failed again
- You doubted whether God still accepts you

Satan whispers: _"You've blown it too many times. You're not really covered anymore."_

Communion doesn't re-sacrifice Jesus. It doesn't reapply His blood (that happened "once for all").

But it **reminds you** that the blood is still there. That the covering hasn't been removed.

"For as often as you eat this bread and drink the cup, you proclaim the Lord's death **until he comes**" (1 Corinthians 11:26).

Every time you take communion, you're saying:

### Living in the Freedom of Being Covered

Okay, so you've been baptized. You take communion. You understand the theology.

Let me give you three implications:

#### **1. You Can Approach God with Confidence**

Remember the mercy seat? The blood on top of the Ark, covering the Law's accusations?

That's your reality now.

"Therefore, brothers, since we have **confidence to enter the holy places by the blood of Jesus**... let us draw near with a true heart in **full assurance** of faith" (Hebrews 10:19, 22).

- You don't have to clean yourself up first
- You don't have to earn the right to ask
- You don't have to wonder if God is angry with you

**You come covered. And God sees the blood before He sees your sin.**

---

#### **2. You Can Stop Performing**

This is huge.

Most Christians live under a crushing burden: trying to be good enough.

- Good enough for God to bless them
- Good enough for God to answer their prayers
- Good enough for God to use them

But here's the truth: **You're already covered.**

Your acceptance before God is not based on your performance. It's based on Christ's.

This is the freedom of the gospel:

### When You Feel Uncovered

Let's be honest. There will be days when you don't _feel_ covered.

You sin. You fail. You wonder: _"Am I still covered? Or have I finally crossed the line?"_

#### **1. Distinguish Between Conviction and Condemnation**

**Conviction** (from the Holy Spirit):

- "You sinned. Come back. You're still mine."
- Leads you **to God**
- Offers hope and a way forward

**Condemnation** (from Satan or your own guilt):

- "You sinned. You're done. God's finished with you."
- Drives you **away from God**
- Offers no hope, only despair

"There is therefore now **no condemnation** for those who are in Christ Jesus" (Romans 8:1).

If the voice is condemning, it's not God. God convicts to **restore**, not to destroy.

#### **2. Go Back to the Mercy Seat**

Remember what the high priest did on Yom Kippur?

He didn't look at his own goodness. He looked at the **blood on the mercy seat**.

You do the same.

When you feel uncovered, don't look at your performance. Look at Christ.

_"Jesus, I've sinned. But You died for this exact sin. Your blood is still on my mercy seat. I'm still covered."_

This is why the church practices communion regularly (weekly for many churches).

_"His body was broken for me. His blood was shed for me. I am covered."_

Don't wait until you _feel_ worthy to take communion. You'll never feel worthy.

Take communion **because you need the reminder** that you're covered by grace, not by merit.

Here's what living under the covering looks like long-term:

Not perfection. Not performance. Not pretending.
